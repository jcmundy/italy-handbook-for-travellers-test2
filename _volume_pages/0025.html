{% raw %}find it convenient to have as nearly as possible the exact fare ready{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before taking tickets. In addition to the fare a tax of 5c. is payable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on each ticket, and the express fares are J 0-12'/2 per cent higher{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the ordinary. It is also very important to be at the station{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early, as, in accordance with the regulations, the ticket-office closes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 min., and the luggage-office 1/i hr. before the departure of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}train. At the end of the journey tickets are given up at the uscita,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}except in the case of the very large stations, where they are collected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before the passengers alight.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller is recommended to ascertain the weight of his lug¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gage, if possible, before going to the station, in order to guard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against imposition. No luggage is allowed free (but see below, inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}national through-tickets), except small articles taken by the pas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}senger into his carriage. Porters who convey luggage to and from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the carriages are sufficiently paid with a few sous, where there is no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fixed tariff. Those who intend to make only a short stay at a place,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially when the town or village lies at a considerable distance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the railway, had better leave their heavier luggage at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station till their return (dare in deposito, or depositare, 10c. per{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day for each article).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The best collection of time-tables is the 'Indicatore Ufficiale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delle Strode Ferrate', etc. (price 1 fr.), with which every traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be provided. The local time-tables of the Tuscan, Roman,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Neapolitan lines, which may be procured at the railway stations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a few sous, are also useful.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Through Tickets to different parts of Italy are issued in London{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(at the principal railway stations; by Messrs. Cook & Son, Ludgate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Circus; etc.), in Paris, and at many of the principal towns in Ger¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many and Switzerland. They are generally available for 30 days,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and each passenger is allowed 56 Engl. lbs. of luggage free. When{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through tickets are taken from Italy to Switzerland, Germany, etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that portion of the journey which lies beyond the Italian frontier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must be paid for in gold. Travellers about to cross the frontier in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}either direction are strongly recommended to superintend the custom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house examination of luggage in person.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Circular Tickets (viaggi circolari) to the principal towns in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy , available sometimes for 50 days , may be purchased in Lon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}don, in France, and in Germany, as well as in Italy, at a reduction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 45 per cent (but usually without a free allowance of luggage).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther particulars will be found in the time-tables, or at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}•agenzie1, or railway-offices in the larger towns. For Central Italy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alone there are five different circular tours , for which 10-30 days{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are allowed. These tickets require to be stamped at the office at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each fresh starting-point.{% endraw %}
