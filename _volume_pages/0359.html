{% raw %}S. Clemente. The Lateran.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Colosseum (p. 233; PL II, 24) three streets run to¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards the S.E. : to the left the Via Labicana to the Thermae of Titus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 236), to the right the Via de' Quattro Santi to SS. Quattro Coro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nati (p. 266), uniting with the following street near the Lateran,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and lastly, between these two, the Via di S. Giovanni in Late¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rano (12 min. in length) to the Piazza of the Lateran and the Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Giovanni. Following the latter street we reach, in 5 min., a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small piazza, where on the left is situated —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Clemente (PL II, 27; side-entrance from the street generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open; if not, visitors ring at the principal door under the portico),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the best-preserved basilicas of Rome, and rendered still more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting by the result of recent excavations, which were zealously{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and successfully prosecuted hy the Prior Mullooly. These operations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have brought to light, below the present church, three different layers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of masonry, the first being of early Christian, the second of imperial,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the third of republican origin. The early Christian basilica{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(now the lower church) is mentioned hy St. Jerome as early as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year 392, and in 417 was the scene of a council of the church. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was almost entirely destroyed in 1084 on the entry of Robert Guis-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}card into Rome, and in 1108 Paschalis II. erected on its ruins the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present upper church, with which he incorporated several orna¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of the lower, such as the choir and the ambos. The upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church also underwent frequent restoration, and was finally deco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rated with considerable taste hy Clement XI., who however un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortunately added the unsuitable ceiling. St. Clement (90-100),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}according to Roman tradition, was the third successor of St. Pe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ter, and suffered martyrdom in the Black Sea. The church which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands on the traditional site of his house gives a title to a cardinal,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and belongs to Irish Dominicans.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}silicas, without a transept.{% endraw %}
