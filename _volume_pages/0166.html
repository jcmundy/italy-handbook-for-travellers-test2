{% raw %}Near Pesaro is Monte S. Bartolo, where the Roman dramatist. L. Att.ius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is said to have been born and to be interred. Beyond it lies L'Imperiale,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once a favourite villa of the dukes, erected by Leonora Gonzaga, praised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Bernardo Tasso, and adorned with frescoes by Raffaello dot Colle, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abandoned to decay since the 18th cent. In the vicinity is the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Girolamitani, with an unfortunately damaged picture of St. Jerome by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giovanni Santi. One of the finest 'prospects in the environs is obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from an eminence behind the monastery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An Excursion to Urbino is most easily accomplished from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesaro. Diligence daily, ascending in 5, and descending in 4 hrs.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}departure from Pesaro at 9 a.m., from Urbino at 3y2 p. m. (4 fr.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comp. p. 87). The road leads through the valley of the Foglia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which falls into the sea at Pesaro, and then gradually ascends, pass¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing several unimportant villages. At the inn 'del Cappone', half¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way, the horses are changed. Beyond Moline the road ascends in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long windings. The diligence stops in the main street, flanked on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left by arcades, in which the inn and several cafe's are situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Urbino {Albergo dell'Italia , tolerable), the ancient Vrbinum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hortense, celebrated as the birthplace of the greatest painter of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all ages, Raphael Santi (b. 28th March 1483, d. at Rome, 5th April{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1520), lies on an abrupt hill, surrounded by barren mountains. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, with 16,000 inhab. (incl. villages), boasts of a university{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with as many professors as students, and merits a visit as well for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its picturesque situation, as for its monuments and historical as¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sociations.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the 13th cent, the town came into the possession of the Monlefeltro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family, and under Federigo Montefeltro (1444-82) and his son Guidobaldo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1182-1508) attained to such prosperity as entirely to eclipse the neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bouring courts of the Malatestas at Rimini and the Sforzas at Pesaro.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Federigo Montefeltro, who distinguished himself as a condottiere in the feuds{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ol the 15th cent., married his daughter in 1474 to Giovanni della Rovere,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a nephew of Sixtus IV., and was in consequence created Duke of Urbino.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}His court was regarded as a model among the princely courts of that period.{% endraw %}
