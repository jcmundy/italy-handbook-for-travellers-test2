{% raw %}This ancient edifice, which was entered from the E., was entirely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remodelled by Honorius III. (1216-27), who added the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nave to the apse, and transferred the facade with the porch to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. end. An angle formed by the outer walls shows where the new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part was added. Under Nicholas V. and Innocent X., and finally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under Pius IX. in 1864-70, the church underwent extensive altera¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions, and is now at least partially freed from the patchwork by which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it was formerly disfigured. S. Lorenzo is a patriarchal church, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the seven pilgrimage-churches of Rome (p. 128). Festival,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10th Aug.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the piazza in front of the church is a Column with a bronze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statue of St. Lawrence. The Facade of the church has been recently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embellished with paintings resembling mosaic, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founders and patrons of the church: Pelagius II., the Emp. Con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stantine, Honorius III., Pius IX., Sixtus III., and Hadrian I. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vestibule is supported by six ancient columns, above which is an ar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chitrave with mosaics (St. Lawrence and Honorius III.), and contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retouched frescoes of the 13th cent., two tombs in the form of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temples, and two rude Christian sarcophagi. The door-posts rest on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior consists of two parts. The anterior Later Chukcii, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly dates from Honorius III., consists of naveand two aisles, separated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by 22 antique columns of granite and cipolline of unequal thickness. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the capital of the 8th column on the right are a frog and a lizard, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is therefore supposed, but without authority, to have been brought{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the colonnade of Octavia, where two sculptors Batrachus (frog){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Saurus (lizard) are said to have adopted this method of perpetuating{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their names. Above the plain entablature rises a wall recently adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with frescoes by Fraccassini (on the right, history of St. Lawrence; on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, that of St. Stephen), and the gaudily painted open roof. The pavement,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in opus Alexandrinum, dates from the 12th century. Under a mediaeval canopy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right of the entrance is an ancient 'sarcophagus with a representation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a wedding, in which in 1256 the remains of Card. Fieschi, nephew of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Innocent IV., were placed. In the nave are the two elevated ambos, that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the :! left for the gospel, near which is a wreathed candelabrum for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Easter candle , that to the right for the epistle (12th cent.). On the trium¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phal arch are modern paintings (resembling mosaics) of the Madonna and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saints. At the extremity of the N. aisle a flight of 12 steps, on the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends to a chapel and the catacombs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining this building of Honorius on the E. is the Older Church,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Pelagius, the pavement of which lies 3 ft. lower. The raised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}central space, to which seven steps ascend on each side of the Confessio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from the time of Honorius, who converted the nave of the older{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church into a choir with a crypt by laying a pavement halfway up the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, and caused the aisles to ' be filled up. The rubbish has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently removed, and the original level of the aisles exposed to view.{% endraw %}
