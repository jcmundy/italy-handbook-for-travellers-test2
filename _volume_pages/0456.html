{% raw %}340 Environs. GROVE OF THE ARVALES.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Excursions in the Campagna may be performed by carriage, on horse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, or on foot, each mode possessing its peculiar advantages. The tra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veller is particularly cautioned against the risk of taking cold, owing to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great and sudden change of temperature which generally takes place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about sunset. Lying or sitting on the ground in winter, when the soil is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extremely cold in comparison with the hot sunshine, is also to be avoided.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In crossing the fields care should be taken to avoid the formidable herds of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cattle, especially in spring; and the same remark sometimes applies to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dogs by which they are watched when the herdsman is absent. Predatory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attacks on travellers are of rare occurrence, but enquiry as to the state{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the country is by no means superfluous. Those whose residence in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome is sufficiently prolonged should make the excursions in the plain in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter, and those among the mountains in the warmer season.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the following description the principal points of interest only can{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be pointed out.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Short Excursions in the Campagna.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This first list contains those excursions from Rome which occupy a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}few hours only, and which will be found refreshing after a morning spent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a church or museum. As far as the gates, and for '/a M. or more be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yond them, the roads are dull and uninteresting from being Hanked by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lofty walls. A carriage should therefore be taken at least as far as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gate; fares, see p. 112; for longer distances a bargain must be made. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller should, if possible, so arrange his excursion as to regain the city{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shortly after sunset.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The excursions are enumerated according to the order of the gates{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from S. to E. and N.; comp. the Map.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Porta Portbse (PL III, 15).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grove of the Arvales. This excursion, occupying about 4 hrs.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is interesting to the archaeologist only. About 1 M. from the gate,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Via Campana diverges to the left from the old Via Porluensis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and chiefly follows the bank of the Tiber. About li/2 M. farther it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reaches the Vigna Ceccarelli (where the railway to Civita Vecchia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the road), and, farther on, the stat. Magliana (p. 8). It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has been recently ascertained that the Vigna Ceccarelli is the site of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sacred grove of the 'Fratres Arvales'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The corporation of the Twelve Arvales was of very ancient Latin origin,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having been founded according to tradition by the sons of Acca Larentia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I'mother of the Lares'), the foster-mother of Romulus. The society, whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}original object was to offer a yearly sacrifice to the Dea Dia ('goddess of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plenty), and call down her blessing on the fields, was entirely remodelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Augustus. The three-days' festivities in May, with their ceremonies and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrifices, were still celebrated, but a number of other rites were added{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which related exclusively to the worship of the prevailing dynasty. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}homely 'agricultural brotherhood' became a privileged order, consisting of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the relations and friends of the emperor, whose time was more occupied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with prayers for the prosperity of the imperial house, sacrifices and banquets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the occasion of victories and birthdays, etc., than with the worship of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Dea Dia. Records of these festivities were engraved on stone and pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served in thesacred grove.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The first discovery of these records was made in this vigna (then the Vigna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gallettil in 1570, when, besides nineteen fragments of inscriptions, seven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bases of statues of Roman emperors in their capacity of 'fratres arvales' were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found. Two other inscriptions were excavated here in 1699, and several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other fragments in 1857. This must therefore have been the site of the{% endraw %}
