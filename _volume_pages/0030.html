{% raw %}The principal Parisian newspapers are to be found at all the larger{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cafes, English rarely.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wine Shops (osterle), especially at Rome, are a favourite haunt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the lower classes, who bring their own eatables from the pizzi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carolo, or dealer in comestibles. The rooms are generally dirty and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uninviting, but the wine is often good.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cigars in Italy (Sicily excepted) are a monopoly of Government,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bad; those under 3-4 soldi scarcely smokable. Good imported{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cigars may be bought at the best shops in Rome for 25-60 c. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Passers-by are at liberty to avail themselves of the light burning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in every tobacconist's, without making any purchase.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Churches are open in the morning till 12 or 12.30, and generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again from 4 to 7 p.m. , while some of the most important remain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open the whole day. Visitors may inspect the works of art even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during divine service, provided they move about noiselessly, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}keep aloof from the altar where the clergy are officiating. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occasion of festivals the works of art are often entirely concealed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the temporary decorations. The verger (sagrestano , or nonzolo){% endraw %}
{% raw %}receives a fee of J/2 fr. or upwards, if his services are required.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museums, picture-galleries, and other collections are usually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open from 10 to 3 o'clock. By a law passed in 1875 all the col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lections which belong to government are open on week-days at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charge of 1 fr., and on Sundays (and sometimes on Thursdays also){% endraw %}
{% raw %}gratis. They are closed on the following public holidays : New{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Year's Day, Epiphany (6th Jan.), the Monday and Tuesday during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Carnival, Palm Sunday, Easter Sunday, Ascension Day, Whit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sunday andWhitmonday, Fete de Dieu (Corpus Christi), the Festa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dello Statuto (first Sunday in June), Assumption of the Virgin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(15th Aug.), and on Christmas Day. A good many other days are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also sometimes observed as holidays, such as the Thursday before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Carnival and the day sacred to the local patron saint.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Valets de Place (servitori di piazza) may be hired at 5-6 fr. per{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day. They are generally respectable and trustworthy, but, as they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are seldom good judges of what is really worth seeing, the traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should specify to them the places he desires to visit. Their services{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may generally well be dispensed with by those who are not pressed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for time. Purchases should never be made, nor contracts with vet-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turini or other persons drawn up, in presence or with the aid of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commissionnaire, as any such intervention tends considerably to in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crease the i'rices.{% endraw %}
