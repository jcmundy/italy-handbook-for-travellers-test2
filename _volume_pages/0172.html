{% raw %}94 Route 7l>.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}CAGLI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Fano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contracts; to the right rises the hill of Pietralata, occasionally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}named Monte d'Asdrubale. Here, according to the popular tradition,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was fought the memorable battle of the Metaurus in which, B.C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}207, Hasdrubal, whilst inarching to the aid of his brother Hannibal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 60,000 men, was signally defeated and slain by the consuls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Livius Salinator and Claudius Nero. This was the great event{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which decided the 2nd Punic War in favour of Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The valley now becomes still more confined and is bounded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}precipitous slopes. At the narrowest part, where the rocky walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approach so near each other as to leave space for the river only,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the celebrated * Furlo Pass (Furlo from forulus — passage, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient petra intercisa), a tunnel 17 ft. wide, 14ft. high, and about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}32 yds. in length. The founder of the work was the Emp. Ves¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pasian, as the inscription preserved at the N. entrance records (Imp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Caesar. Augustus. Vespasianus. pont. max. trib. pot. VII. imp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XXVIII. cos. VIII. censor, faciund. curavit).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A short distance beyond it stands the small church Badia del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Furlo. At the confluence of the Candigliano and Burano, 9 M. from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fossombrone, is situated the village of Acqualagna. The road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the Candigliano and thenceforward follows the left bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Burano. At the foot of the hill on which Cagli is situated, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antique bridge, consisting of huge masses of rock, crosses a tribu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tary brook.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}31 M. Cagli {Posta, in the Piazza, charges according to bar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gain), with about 10,000 inhab., occupies the site of the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}borough of Cales, or Calle. S. Domenico contains one of the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}important works of Giovanni Santi, Raphael's father, a Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with saints, al fresco. The angel on the right of the Madonna is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to be a portrait of the young Raphael. There is also a Pieta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with St. Jerome and Bonaventura, by the same master. 5. Francesco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and S. Angelo Minore also possess several pictures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers beyond Cagli are generally conveyed in smaller car¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}riages. About 6 M. beyond Cagli is Cantiano, with 3000 inhab.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church della Collegiata contains a Holy Family by Perugino. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road ascends rapidly, and reaches the height of the pass,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2400 ft. above the sea. A little before arriving at Schieggia the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses a ravine by the curious Ponte a Botte, constructed in 1805.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}431/-2 M. Schieggia, an insignificant place, lies at the junction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Fossato and Foligno, and the Gubbio roads. On Monte Petrara,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the vicinity, stand the ruins of the celebrated temple of Jupiter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Apenninus, whose worship was peculiar to the Umbrians. Several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronzes and inscriptions have been discovered in the environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Picturesque oak-plantations in the neighbourhood.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The main road continues to descend the green valley of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chiascio, and leads by Costacciaro and Sigillo (stalactite caves) to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}55 M. Fossato, a station on the Ancona and Rome line, see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 102.{% endraw %}
