{% raw %}Climate (comp. Introd.). The mean temperature at Rome is 60" Fahr.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the greatest heat in summer being about 100° in the shade, and the greatest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cold about 21°. Snow falls rarely, and does not lie long. The average{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperature in January is 45°, in July 75°. The pleasantest season is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the beginning of October to the end of May. In summer when the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fever-laden aria cattiva prevails, all the inhabitants who can afford it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}make a point of leaving the city. The prevalent winds are the Tra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}montane*, or north wind, which generally brings clear and bracing wea¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ther , and the Scirocco, or south wind, which is relaxing and rainy. As{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the temperature usually falls rapidly after sunset, and colds are very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}easily caught, the traveller should not dress too lightly. Invalids should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of course consult their medical advisers before choosing rooms, but even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}persons in robust health will do well to remember the Roman proverb:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Dove non vo il sole, va il medico''.{% endraw %}
