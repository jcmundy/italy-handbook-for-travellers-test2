{% raw %}The *Lago di Nemi (1066 ft.) is an extinct crater, about 3 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in circumference, and like the Alban lake, which lies 100 ft. lower,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is of considerable depth (328 ft.), and is also drained by an artificial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emissarius. The water is beautifully clear , and rarely ruffled by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wind. The precipitous lava-slopes of the crater, 328 ft. in height,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are carefully cultivated. In ancient times it was called the Lacus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nemorensis, and sometimes the 'Mirror of Diana', from a temple, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which substructions have been discovered below Nemi, and from a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nemus , or grove sacred to that goddess, whence the present name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is derived. Tiberius (or Trajan) constructed a magnificent vessel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here , a beam of which is preserved in the Museo Kircheriano at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome (see p. 152). This exquisite lake is the gem of the Alban Mts.{% endraw %}
