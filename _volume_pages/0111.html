{% raw %}to Perugia. AREZZO. 6. Route. 39{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arretium was one of the most powerful of the twelve confederate cities of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etruria, and (like Cortona and Perusia) concluded peace with the Romans{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the great war of B.C. 310, after which it continued to be an ally of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome. In 187 the Consul C Flaminius constructed the Via Flaminia from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arretium to Bononia (Bologna), of which traces are still distinguishable. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the civil war Arretium was destroyed by Sulla, but was subsequently colo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nised (Colonia Fidens Julia Arretium), and again prospered. Its manufactures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were red earthenware vases, of superior quality, and weapons. — In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}middle ages the town suffered greatly from the Goths and the Lombards,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and at a later date from the party-struggles of the Guclphs and Ghibellines,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which it generally took the part of the latter against the Guelphs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence. In the 14th cent, it was for a time subject to the rule of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tarlati, and in 1337 temporarily, and in the 16th cent, under Cosmo I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally to that of Florence.{% endraw %}
