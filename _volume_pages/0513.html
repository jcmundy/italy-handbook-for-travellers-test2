{% raw %}Veii, near Isola Farnese, may be visited from Rome, from which it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11 M. distant, in one day. Carriage for the whole excursion about 25 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For pedestrians the route is too long to be pleasant, and they should drive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at least as far as the Tomba di Nerone (5 M., fiacre 4 fr.), or La Storta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(s'/a M.). Those who, are so disposed may return from Veii by a somewhat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longer route, skirting the Fosso di Valchetla, the valley of which descends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Via Flaminia between the 6th and 7th milestones. Provisions for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the journey should be carried from Rome, as the tavern at Isola is ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tremely poor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Rome to the Ponte Molle, see p. 351. By the Osteria,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the Via Flaminia diverges to the right, we follow the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cassia, gradually ascending to the left. The district soon becomes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}desolate. Near the 5th milestone, to the left of the road, on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dilapidated pedestal, stands a sarcophagus with a long inscription,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commonly called, but without the slightest authority, the Tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nero. The inscription (at the back, facing the ancient road) ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pressly records that the monument, which probably dates from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 2nd cent, after Christ, was erected hy Vibia Maria Maxima to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the memory of her father P. Vibius Marianus and her mother{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Regina Maxima. — An ancient route, somewhat shorter than the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern, diverges here to the right to Veii, but as it is not easily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traced the high road is preferable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We next reach (8>/2 M.) the post-station of La Storta (inn),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which in ancient times was the last station on the route to Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One mile beyond it the road diverges to the right to Isola Farnese;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'/2 M. farther, where the road divides, we select that to the right;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road to the left leads to Formello.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Isola Farnese, a poor village, belonging to the Rospigliosi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numbering scarcely 100 inhab., and harassed by fever in summer,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was a place of some consequence in the middle ages, having been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded on account of the security of its site. A guide is engaged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here f 1- 11/2 fr., bargaining necessary) to conduct us to the site{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Veii. Imposing ruins must not be looked for, but the landscape{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is interesting and picturesque. A minute inspection of the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}city is interesting to the archaeologist only. The following are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal points, a visit to which occupies 2-3 hrs. We first descend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the brook to the mill (molino), where there is a picturesque water¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fall, not far from which the brook is crossed by the ancient Ponte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dell' Isola. Farther on is the Ponte Sodo, hewn in the rock, under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the brook is conducted. Then the Porta Spezieria with remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a columbarium, the recesses of which gave rise to the name. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vicinity is the Grotta Campana. We next follow the Cremera{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Piazza d'Armi, the anpient citadel (tine view), and then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return to Isola. Pedestrians, by descending the valley of the stream{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Piazza d'Armi, may reach the Via Flaminia in 2 hrs.{% endraw %}
