{% raw %}Amongst the crowd of statues which fill the galleries and cham¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bers of the Vatican and Capitol of Rome are to be seen the noblest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}examples of Antique Sculpture. These do not, however, stand in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same relation to Imperial Rome as, for example, the frescoes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fiesole in the Cloisters of St. Mark, or those of Andrea del Sarto in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Church of the Annunziata to Florence, or as the master-pieces{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Raphael and Michael Angelo to mediaeval and pontifical Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These latter originated, so to speak, with her, were her peculiar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributes, the fitting emblems of her ecclesiastical supremacy. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}genius which created them, she inspired, fostered, and rewarded.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of ancient history, though attracting to herself the accumulated{% endraw %}
