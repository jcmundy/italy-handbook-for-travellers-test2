{% raw %}In this pleasing fable Psyche obviously represents the human soul purified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by passions and misfortunes, and thus fitted for the enjoyment of celestial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}happiness.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the compartments below the pendentives twelve Cupids with divine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributes. The garlands which surround the different paintings are by Gio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vanni da Udine. — The frescoes, having suffered from exposure to the atmo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sphere, were retouched by Maraita. The blue ground, which was origin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally of a much warmer tint, as is apparent from the few portions still un-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}faded, was most seriously injured. The whole nevertheless produces a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charming and brilliant effect owing to the indestructible beauty of the de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}signs. The felicity with which the scenes have been adapted to the un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}favourable spaces is also remarkable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The smaller apartment adjoining the principal hall, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also once an open Loggia, contains a second mythological picture by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Raphael, which is no less charming than the Psyche series, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even far surpasses them in point of execution : ** Galatea , borne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}across the sea in a conch, and surrounded by Nymphs, Tritons, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cupids, painted entirely by the master's own hand in 1514. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*ceiling of this room was decorated and painted hy Bald. Peruzzi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ceiling pictures: Perseus and Diana. The hexagonal spaces contain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods of the planets and mythological scenes. In the lunettes were after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards added scenes from the Metamorphoses, the first Roman work of Seb.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Piombo. The colossal head in the lunette on the left lateral wall is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to have been drawn by Michael Angelo in charcoal, whilst waiting for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dan. da Volterra who was also engaged here, but is more probably by Peruzzi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the corner of the entrance wall, to the left of Galatea, Seb. del Piombo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted the Polyphemus, afterwards almost entirely obliterated, and badly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored. — The landscapes are erroneously attributed to G. Poussin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The restorations which the two rooms have recently undergone have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only been partially successful.{% endraw %}
