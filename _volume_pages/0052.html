{% raw %}ROMAN ART.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christian basilicas , the most impressive effect being reserved for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the interior. And to this end, especially in earlier mediaeval times,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a ready expedient for supplying decorative material was adopted in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the plunder of the monuments of antiquity. Columns were carried{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oil and set up in Christian churches without regard to congruity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of material or consistency of style. Thus in the churches of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. Maria in Trastevere and S. Lorenzo Fuori le Mura are to be seen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(illars of different material and workmanship. The churches of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. Sabina, S. Maria Maggiore and others give evidence of similar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}depredations. Crosses and lustres in metal, tapestries bestowed by-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}papal piety contributed to the ornate effect of these interiors. But{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal decorative feature were the pictures in mosaic which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered the recess of the apse in particular as well as the arch which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connected the apse with the nave (the Triumphal Arch). These{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mosaic Pictures, as far, at least, as the material was concerned,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demanded a novel artistic treatment, massive and monumental in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character. In them we find the traditions of antiquity abandoned,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}giving place to a style which from its harshness as well as austere{% endraw %}
{% raw %}solemnity of conception has been confounded with the Byzantine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. In reality the art was of indigenous growth; and its salient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}characteristic may be defined as the substitution of the real for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}symbolical in general treatment. Now for the first time the pop¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ular mind became thoroughly imbued with ecclesiastical senti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment, of which the crucified Saviour was the chief embodiment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The oldest mosaics, composed of glass cubes, are to be seen in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church of S. Pudenziana. They date from the 4th century{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like those in S. Costanza and the Baptistery of Naples; while those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in S. Maria Maggiore and S. Sabina belong to the 5th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The mosaics in SS. Cosma e Damiano in the Forum (526-30) may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be pronounced as the most beautiful.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rudiments of Christian art are to be found in Rome; but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its further development was promoted in an equal degree by other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian states. Building was still active in the 9th century, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Popes, especially Leo III., of the 7th and 8th centuries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did good service in church decoration. But during this period{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there is no evidence either of progress or continuous develop¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment in the Mosaic art and as little in architecture itself. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}experiment(as seen in S. Prassede, 9th century) of combining piers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the pillars of the nave as a support to the walls and of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necting these with transverse arches was not repeated. Finally it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be said of the Mosaics (S. Prassede, SS. Nereo ed Achilleo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}•V. Marco'), that, while they bear a superficial resemblance to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of the 5th and 6th centuries, they show unmistakable signs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corruption and decline. This may be accounted for to some extent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the evil times which had fallen upon Rome since the 9th century,{% endraw %}
