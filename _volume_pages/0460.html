{% raw %}344 Environs of Rome. VIA APPIA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Snort{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jewish Catacombs (p. 337), and reach the church of S. Sebastiano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated IV2 M. from the gate. This church has from a very early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period been one of the seven churches frequented by pilgrims, being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected over the catacombs where the remains of so many martyrs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reposed. Mention of it is first made in the time of Gregory the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great. The form was originally that of a basilica, but in 1612 it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altered by Flaminio Ponzio and Giovanni Vasanzio. The portico is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supported by six ancient columns of granite.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The 1st Chapel on the right contains the original 'footprint of Christ'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on stone. The last chapel on the right was designed by C. Maratta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Over the High Altak is a painting by Innocenzo Tacconi, a pupil of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Annibale Caracci. The second chapel on the left contains a good Statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Sebastian , designed by Bernini and executed by Giorgini. A stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case on the left, by the egress, descends to the Catacombs , but they are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uninteresting compared with those of Calixtus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately before we come to the church, the Via delle Sette{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chiese diverges to the right, intersecting the Via Ardeatina after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10 min., and in 4 min. more reaching the recently excavated re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mains of the Basilica of St. Petronilla, or of SS. Nereo ed Achillea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see p. 336). Thence to S. Paolo Fuori ll/2 M., see p. 341.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Continuing to follow the Via Appia we come to a large gateway{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left, which we pass in order to reach the * Circus of Maxen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tius, situated on the left side of the road. The circus, which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}350 yds. long, and 86 yds. broad, was constructed in 311, and is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now sufficiently excavated to show the arrangement of the struc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ture, which was destined for chariot-races.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Facing the Via Appia was an extensive portico, and behind it one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal Entrances, with another opposite to it in the semicircle which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terminated the building (on the above-mentioned branch of the road). At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sides were other gates, of which the first on the right is supposed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be the Porta Libitina, by which the dead were carried out. On each side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the first-mentioned main entrance were the carceres, or barriers. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chariots starting hence had to perform seven times the circuit of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course, which was formed by the seats of the spectators and the spina,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a wall erected longitudinally in the centre of the arena, and embel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lished with statues and obelisks, one of which last now stands in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Navona (p. 199). At the ends of this wall stood the metae, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goals. The spina was placed somewhat obliquely, for the purpose of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equalising the distance as much as possible to those starting in different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}positions, and for the same reason the carceres are in an oblique line.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The spectators sat on ten surrounding tiers of steps, on which about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18,000 persons could be accommodated. It is worthy of remark that pottery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has been used in the formation of the tiers of seats.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ruins of a circular building near the circus, on the Via Appia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are supposed to be those of a Temple of Romulus, the son of Maxentius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who died at an early age, and in whose honour the circus was perhaps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also constructed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road again ascends, and, in 35 min. from the Porta S. Se¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bastiano, leads us to the *Tomb of Caecilia Metella, which forms so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conspicuous an object in the views of the Campagna. It is a circular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure, 65 ft. in diameter, which, as well as the square pedestal,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was originally covered with travertine. The frieze which runs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}round the building is adorned with wreaths of flowers and skulls of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oxen, from which last the tomb is sometimes called Capo di Bove.{% endraw %}
