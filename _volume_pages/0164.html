{% raw %}The *Porta Romana (PL D, 6) is a triumphal arch of travertine,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of simple design , erected to Augustus in B.C. 27 out of gratitude{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the restoration of the Via Flaminia , as the inscription records{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(which, however, has been inaccurately restored; the letters to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right outside the gate also belong to it). Above are medallion figures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the outside those of Jupiter and Minerva, on the inside those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Neptune and Venus. — The scanty remains of an Amphitheatre (to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the Via dell' Anflteatro, the second side-street of the Corso{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Porta Romana, leads) do not merit a visit.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Palazzo del Comunb (PL 9), in the Piazza Cavour, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small picture-gallery comprising an *altar-piece by Domenico del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghirlandajo, and a Pieta by Giovanni Bellini (about 1470). — In front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of it rises a bronze Statue of Pope Paul V. (inscription on the pedestal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obliterated). Beyond the Teatro Vittorio Emanuele(PL 10), erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1857, is the ancient Palace of the Malatestas {'Castello Sigismondo';{% endraw %}
{% raw %}PL B, 5), now a prison, and in a very dilapidated condition. Their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arms are still to be seen over the entrance. The townwall, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right of the palace, commands a fine view of the mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Library (PL 8), in the Via Gambalunga to the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Cavour, founded in 1617 by the jurist Gambalunga, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}23,000 vols, and several MSS. An arcade in a court to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now half built up, contains Roman inscriptions and sculptures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among which are a female figure in relief, with thin, close-fitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drapery; a fine female head, etc.{% endraw %}
