{% raw %}ROME. IV. Ancient Rome. 253{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The most conspicuous objects in the broad Campagna are the long rows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of arches of the Aqua Claudia and the Acqua Felice towards the S., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tombs of the Via Appia with that of Ceccilia Metella.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By taking the second road ascending from the Via Salara to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, about 200 paces to the S. of the Piazza Bocca della Vcrita{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. 251), we reach the three Churches on the Aventine,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are situated close together immediately above the road and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river, and may be conveniently visited either in going to, or re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turning from S. Paolo Fuori (comp. p. 254).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Sabina (PL 111, 18), which probably occupies the site of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient temple, was erected in 425, in the pontificate of Coles-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine I., by Petrus, an Illyrian priest, and restored in the 13fh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}15th, and 16th centuries. Since the time of Innocent III. it has be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed to the Dominicans. It is usually entered by a side-door; if{% endraw %}
{% raw %}closed, visitors ring at the door to the left, and proceed through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monastery to the old portico , now closed , and the principal portal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The doors are adorned with wood carvings representing biblical sub¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jects (5th cent. ?).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, with its twenty-four ancient Corinthian columns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parian marble and open roof, has well preserved the character of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early basilica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance-Wall. Over the door, an ancient Mosaic (5th cent.); in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scription with the name of the founder; on the left a figure emblematical of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ecclesia ex Circumcisione (Jewish Christians), on the right that of tlie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ecclesia ex Gentibus (Pagan Christians).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nave. On the pavement in the centre of the nave is the tomb of Munio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}da Zamora, principal of the Dominican order (d. 1300), adorned with mosaic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— At the extremity of the right aisle , the '-Madonna del Rosario with St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dominicus and St. Catharine, an altar-piece by Sassoferrato, regarded as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his master-piece. Other paintings (by Zucchero and others) are of no great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}value. Festival, 29th Aug.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The adjoining Monastery possesses handsome cloisters with up¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards of 100 small columns. The garden commands a line*View of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome, with the Tiber in the foreground.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Alessio (PL III, 18) is an ancient church with an entrance-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court. The date of its foundation is unknown , but it was re-conse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crated hy Honorius 111. after the recovery of the relics of the saint{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1217. In 1426 it came into the possession of the order of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jerome. In the neighbouring monastery a blind asylum (lstituto de'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ciechi) has been established. We enter the fore-court, and, if the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church is closed, ring at the door on the left (l/2 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior was modernised in 1750, and again recently. The N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aisle contains a well and a wooden staircase belonging to the house of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parents of the saint, which formerly stood on the site of the church. Two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small columns adorned with mosaic in the choir are, according to the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scription , the remains of a work of 19 columns by Jac. Cosmas.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}this piazza, contains the celebrated *Key-hole through which St.{% endraw %}
