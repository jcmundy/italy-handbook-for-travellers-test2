{% raw %}The best hotels have fixed charges. Attendance, exclusive of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boots and commissionnaire, is charged in the bill. This is not the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case in the smaller inns, where 1 fr. per diem is usually divided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the waiter and the facchino, or less for a prolonged stay.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Copper coins are never despised by such recipients.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotels Garnis and Private Apartments are recommended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a prolonged residence. A distinct agreement as to rent should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be made beforehand. When a whole suite of apartments is hired, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}written contract on stamped paper should be drawn up with the aid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of some one acquainted with the language and customs of the place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(e.g. a banker), in order that 'misunderstandings' may be prevented.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For single travellers a verbal agreement with regard to attendance,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}linen, stoves and carpets in winter, a receptacle for coal, and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}details will generally suffice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The popular idea of cleanliness in Italy is behind the age, dirt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being perhaps neutralised in the opinion of the natives by the bril¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liancy of their climate. The traveller will rarely suffer from this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shortcoming in hotels and lodgings of the best class; but those who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quit the beaten track must be prepared for privations. Iron bedsteads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should if possible be selected, as they are less likely to harbour the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enemies of repose. Insect-powder (polvere di Persia , or Keating's){% endraw %}
{% raw %}or camphor somewhat repels their advances. The zanzare, or gnats,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a source of great annoyance, and often of suffering, during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}autumn months. Windows should always be carefully closed before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a light is introduced into the room. Light muslin curtains (zan-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}zarieri) round the beds, masks for the face, and gloves are employed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to ward off the attacks of these pertinacious intruders. The burning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of insect powder over a spirit-lamp is also recommended, and pas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tilles may be purchased at the principal chemists' for the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purpose.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}IX. Restaurants, Cafes, Osterie.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Restaurants (trattorie) are chiefly frequented by Italians and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gentlemen travelling alone, but those of the better class may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visited by ladies also. Dinner may be obtained a la carte, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sometimes a prezzo fisso, at any hour between 12 and 7 or 8 p. m.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for 2-5 fr. ; the waiters expect a gratuity of 2-5 soldi. The diner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who wishes to confine his expenses within reasonable limits, should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refrain from ordering dishes not mentioned in the bill of fare.{% endraw %}
