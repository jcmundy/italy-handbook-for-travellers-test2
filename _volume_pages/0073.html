{% raw %}Civita Vecchia, the seaport of Rome, with 11,600 inhab., the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Centum Cellae founded by Trajan, and sometimes called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Portus Trajani, was destroyed by the Saracens in 828, but in 854{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the inhabitants returned into the 'ancient city'. The fortifications,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in the 16th and 17th cent., have recently been restored by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the French. The entrance to the harbour, in front of which rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small fortified island with a lighthouse, is defended by two strong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towers. Visitors are permitted to inspect the Bagno, where the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}galley-convicts are at work. The town contains little that is inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting. The traveller may best spend a leisure hour in walking on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the quay.{% endraw %}
