{% raw %}4. From Florence to Siena and Chiusi by Empoli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}116 M. Railway. From Florence, to Siena, 5S'/2 31., in 3-31/./hrs.; fares{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10 fr. 45, 7fr. 20, 5 fr. — From Siena to Chiusi, 57'/2 M., in 3'/.,-5 hrs.:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fares 9 fr. 70, 0 fr. 65, 4 fr. 63 c. — No quick trains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence, see vol. i. of this Handbook. The line skirts the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. bank of the Arno, passing the Cascine and numerous villas.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond stat. <S. Donnino the valley of the Arno expands. 7 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Signa, with its grey pinnacles and towers, is famed for its straw-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plaiting establishments. The line intersects undulating vineyards,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the Ombrone, which falls into the Arno, and enters the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defile of the Gonfolina, which separates the middle from the lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Arno. 15'/a M. Montelupo, approached by an iron{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge across the Arno. Beyond it, on the right, is seen the Villa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ambrogiana, founded by Ferdinand I. on the site of an old castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Ardinghelli. Then, crossing the small river Pesa, the train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reaches —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}19 M. Empoli, a small town with 6400 inhab., with antiquated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buildings and narrow streets, situated in a fertile district. Halt of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10-30 min. (no railway buffet); passengers to Siena change carriages.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The main line pursues a W. direction towards Pisa and Leghorn ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}see vol. i. of this Handbook.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The line to Siena traverses the fertile valley of the Elsa, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right bank of the stream. To the right, on the height, S. Mi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niato dei Tedeschi, picturesquely situated, and possessing a lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediceval tower. Beyond stat. Osteria Bianca, a fruitful valley is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traversed. 31 M. Castel Fiorentino; the town, on the height to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, is the principal place in the Vol d'Elsa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}36 M. Certaldo; the town, on the hill to the left, was the na¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tive place of the poet Giovanni Boccaccio, who died here, 21st Dec.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1375, at the age of 62. Down to 1783 his tomb was in the church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Michele e Giacomo (La Canonica); it was erected in 1503{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and adorned with a statue of the poet, who held the 'Decamerone'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in his hand. The monument was afterwards removed and the bones{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scattered. The house of Boccaccio was restored in 1823 by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Countess Carlotta Lenzoni-Medici, and fitted up in the mediaeval{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. The remains of his monument were also brought hither.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}44 M. Poggibonsi (Aquila, opposite the station, tolerably com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortable); the town (4000 inhab.) lies to the right. On the hill{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above it rise the old castle and the monastery of S. Lucchese. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of the castle is an altar-piece and in the former Refectory are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes by Gerino da Pistoja.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Poggibonsi to Volterra about 15 fr., a drive of 3-4 hrs.{% endraw %}
