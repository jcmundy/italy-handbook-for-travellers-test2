{% raw %}The Via Salara, a very ancient road, quits Rome by the bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Tiber, and then turns towards the district of the Sabines. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes the Villa Albani (p. 165), and reaches the Anio about 2!/4 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the city-gate. On the hill to the left, in the angle formed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Anio at its junction with the Tiber, once lay Antemnae, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was destroyed by Romulus. The summit (203 ft.) commands a noble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prospect. The Ponte Salaro over the Anio, with its two arches, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed by Totilas, and afterwards renewed by Narses, but during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the invasion of Garibaldi in 1867 it was again blown up; the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foundation of tuffstone may be distinguished from the superstructure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of travertine. Beyond the bridge an ancient tomb, built over in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}middle ages, and for some time used as an osteria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 5 M. from the gate is the Villa Spada. From this point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the height on the right extended the ancient Fidenae, once allied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Veii against Rome, and only subdued together with its con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}federate after protracted struggles.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Few traces of the ancient city are now recognisable. The fortress lav{% endraw %}
{% raw %}close to the river, on the hill which is now occupied by Castel Giubileo{% endraw %}

{% raw %}view. The castle was erected by Boniface VIII. in 1300, and is said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derive its name from a family to whom it once belonged.{% endraw %}
