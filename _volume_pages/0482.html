{% raw %}35 S Environs of Rome. ALBA LONGA. Alban Mts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The wooded summit of Monte Cavo. the ancient Mons Albanus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is reached (turning to the right at the beginning of the crater) by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the well-preserved and shady Via Triumphalis, an ancient road,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paved w ith basalt, by which the generals to whom the senate refused{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a triumph at Rome, ascended, and celebrated one on their own re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sponsibility. From two open spaces, about three-quarters of the way{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up, a better *view than from the top is obtained of Marino on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, the Lago d'Albano, Ariccia with the viaduct, Genzano, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lago di Nemi, and Nemi itself.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the summit of the Mons Albanus, 3147 ft. above the sea-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level (an ascent of 3/4 hr. from Rocca di Papa), stood the venerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sanctuary of the Latin League, the Temple of Jupiter Latiaris,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the great sacrificial festival of the Feriae Latinae was cele¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brated annually. Its ruins, 82 yds. long and 38 yds. wide, with col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns of white and yellow marble, were in tolerable preservation till{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1783 , when Cardinal York, the last of the Stuarts, converted them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into a Passionist Monastery. A portion only of the ancient founda¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions is preserved on the S.E. side of the garden-wall. The **View{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from several different points is incomparable. It embraces the sea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the coast from Terracina to Civita Vecchia, the Volscian and Sabine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mts., Rome and the Campagna with a number of towns and villages,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and below the spectator the beautiful Alban Mts. The distant view,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally obscured by mist, is seen to the best advantage immed¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iately before sunrise, after sunset, or after a passing shower has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cleared the atmosphere. The traveller had better take refreshments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with him ; the monks are very civil, but the food and sleeping ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commodation which they offer are exceedingly poor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Monte Cavo we may reach Nemi in iy2 hr. by pleasant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forest-paths (see p. 362).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Those who wish to proceed direct to Albano by Palazzuola should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retrace their steps to the Campo d'Annibale, pass above Rocca di Papa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and visit (1 M.) the chapel of the Madonna del Tufo (2323 ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence a fine view of the Alban Lake and the plain is enjoyed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 1 M. beyond it they will Teach the suppressed Franciscan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monastery of Palazzuola, dating from the 13th cent., and situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above the E. margin of the Lake of Albano (p. 360). The garden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a remarkable rock-tomb in the Etruscan style, about which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little is known.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above the monastery, on the narrow space between the base of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Cavo and the Alban Lake, once lay in a prolonged line, as its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name indicates, the city of AlbaLonga, of which no traces now re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}main. It will be observed, however, that the rocks in the direction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Palazzuola have been hewn perpendicularly, in order to render{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town more impregnable.{% endraw %}
