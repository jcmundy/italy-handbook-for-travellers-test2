{% raw %}Rome is situated (41° 53' 54" N. lat., 12° 29" E. longit., meri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dian of Greenwich) in an undulating volcanic plain, which extends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Capo Linaro, S. of Civita Vecchia, to the Promontorio Circeo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a distance of about 85 M., and between the Apennines and the sea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a width of 25 M. The city is built on both banks of the Tiber, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}largest river in the Italian peninsula, 14 M. from its influx into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mediterranean. The prospect from one of the hills of Rome — and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}no city is more replete with ever-varying and delightful views —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is bounded towards the E. by the unbroken chain of the Apennines,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which rise at a distance of 10 to 20 M. In the extreme N. towers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the indented ridge of Soracte (2260 ft.), occupying an isolated po¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sition in the plain, and separated by the Tiber from the principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}range of the Apennines. Farther E., and still more distant, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leonessa group (7257 ft.), which approaches the Central Apennines.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Considerably nearer lies the range of the Sabine Mts. The summit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the angle which they form by their abutment on the Campagna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is M. Gennaro (4163 ft.), the Lucretilis of Horace; the village at{% endraw %}
{% raw %},the base is Monticelli (1295 ft.). Farther off, on the slope of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill, lies Tivoli, recognisable by its villas and olive-gardens. More{% endraw %}
