{% raw %}ruin. It contains a picturesque, overgrown fountain-basin, and a{% endraw %}

{% raw %}II. Excursions from Rome to the Mountains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Alban Mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The railways to Frascati (p. 354), Marino (p. 357), Albano (p. 359), etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}render the Afban Mts. so easily accessible from Rome, that the traveller may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obtain a glimpse at some of the most interesting points in a single day.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome should, if possible, be quitted in the evening, in order that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excursion may be begun at an early hour on the foffowing morning.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plan of Excursion. Time necessary for Frascati, the villas, and Tus-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}culum l3/4-2 hrs., thence to Rocca di Papa (p. 357) 1 hr. (guide necessary,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I-I1/2 fr.), ascent of Monte Ceevo 3/i hr., descent 20 min., to Nemi 13/,| hr.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genzano l3/4 hr., Ariccia V2 hr-, Albano '/i hr., i. e. 8-i) hrs. (without halt),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which may be somewhat diminished if the route from Rocca di Papa direct{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Albano by Palazzuola (p. 358) be taken. In the reverse direction, begin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ning with Albano, the excursion occupies about the same time. If the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cursion be made by Genzano and Nemi, Castel Gandolfo (p. 360) should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be visited first. If time permit, it is of course far more enjoyable to devote{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several days to a tour among these mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The only good Inns are at Frascati and Albano, but the smaller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}villages afford accommodation for the night in case of necessity. Albano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is recommended for a stay of several days, as a number of the most beau¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiful excursions are most conveniently accomplished thence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller is recommended to hire a Donkey at Ariccia (where the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best are to be had), or at Frascati; charge, with guide, 4-5 fr. daily.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In spring and autumn Walking in this district will also be found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pleasant, in which case travellers intending to perform the excursion in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one day had better take a guide (about 1lj-z fr.). Those who have more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time, and do not object to an occasional deviation from the direct route,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will have no difficulty in finding their way with the aid of the map and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}following directions. — A precise programme of the excursion should be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}agreed upon with the guides, as they are apt to cut short the journey to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the traveller's disadvantage. A supply of provisions for the expedition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will also be found desirable, as the osterie on the route are very poor.{% endraw %}
