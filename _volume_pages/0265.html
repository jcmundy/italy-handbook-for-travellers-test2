{% raw %}The church is adjoined by the Campo Verano, an extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churchyard, consecrated in 1837, and considerably enlarged in 1854,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the upper part of which commands a beautiful view of the mountains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Campagna. A monument with appropriate inscriptions was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected here in 1870 to commemorate the Battle of Mentana. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tufa rock of the hill are observed tomb-niches from the cata¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}combs of St. Cyriaca, discovered when the cemetery was extended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in this direction.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About a hundred paces beyond the Trofei di Mario, the street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leading to S. Bibiana and the Porta Maggiore diverges to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from that which leads to the Porta S. Lorenzo mentioned at p. 181.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As already stated, the ground here is undergoing a complete{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transformation , and fragments of ancient walls are still frequently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discovered.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 5 min. we reach the church of S. Bibiana (PI. II, 31),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consecrated as early as 470, and rebuilt for the last time in 1625{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Bernini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior contains eight antique columns; above these are frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the life of the saint, on the right by Ciampelli, on the left by Pietro da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cortona (modernised). The statue of St. Bibiana at the high-altar is by Ber¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nini. To the left by the entrance is the stump of a column, at which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the saint is said to have been scourged to death. Festival, 2nd Dec.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A few hundred paces farther on, in the same direction , is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so-called Temple of Minerva Medica (PL II, 32), the picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruin of an ancient Nymphseum in the form of a decagon, 55 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circumference, with deep niches in the walls, and originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with marble below and stucco above. It must have be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed to some splendid bath-establishment, as a number of an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cient statues have been found in the vicinity. One of these, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Minerva Giustiniani of the Braccio Nuovo in the Vatican (p. 307),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has given rise to the otherwise unfounded appellation of 'Temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Minerva'. In the middle ages the ruin was called Le Terme di Gal-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luccio, a name which has been conjectured, without any authority,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be a corruption of 'Gaius and Lucius Caesar'. The vaulting was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in existence down to 1828. The building, which is interesting in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an architectural point of view, dates from about the 3rd cent, after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christ.{% endraw %}
