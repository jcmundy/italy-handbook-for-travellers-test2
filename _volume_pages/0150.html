{% raw %}74 Route 10. SPOLETO. From Perugia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cathedral of S. Rufino (PI. 4), in the upper part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, named after the first bishop (240), dates from the first half of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 12th cent., the crypt from 1028. The ancient facade is adorn¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed with three fine rose-windows. The interior modern. Entrance to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, before reaching the church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the cathedral a broad , unpaved road to the right leads in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few minutes to the Gothic church of S. Chiara (PI. 2), near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gate, erected by Fra Filippo daCampello in 1253. afterwards altered,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and recently restored. Beneath the high-altar are the remains of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}>S. Clara, who, inspired with enthusiasm for St. Francis, abandoned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her parents and wealth, founded the order of Clarissines, and died{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as first abbess. A handsome crypt of different coloured marbles has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently been constructed about her tomb. On the arch above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high-altar, frescoes by Giottino; those in the right transept are at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tributed to Giotto. — The piazza in front of the church commands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a fine view of the fertile valley.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the E. end of the town are remains of a Roman Amphitheatre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. 1).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In a ravine of the lofty Monte Subasio (3612 ft.), at the back of Assisi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is situated the hermitage delle Carceri, to which St. Francis was wont to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retire for devotional exercises.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Assisi to Spello a very beautiful drive of 5 M. (one-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horse carr. 4-5 fr.). By train it is reached in 13 min. To the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the road as the town is approached are the ruins of an amphitheatre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the imperial period, but they are not visible from the railway.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}22 M. Spello, with 4000 inhab., picturesquely situated on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountain-slope, is the ancient Hispellum {Colonia Julia Hispellum).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The gate by which the town is entered, with its three portrait-sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tues, as well as the Porta Urbana, the Porta Veneris, and portions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the wall, are ancient.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Cathedral of S. Maria Maggiore contains some good paint¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ings.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}consecrated water. To the left the Cappella del Sacramento with -fres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coes by Pinturicchio (1501): on the left, the Annunciation (with the name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and portrait of the painter), opposite to us the Adoration, Christ in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple; on the ceiling, the Sibyls. — The Cmoik contains a magnificent{% endraw %}

{% raw %}'Pieta, on the right a Madonna by Perugino, 1521. — Above the altar in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sacimsty, a Madonna by Pinturicchio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Francesco (or Andrea), consecrated in 1228 by Gregory IX.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains in the right transept an altar-piece, Madonna and saints,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Pinturicchio (1508), with a letter by G. Baglione, addressed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the painter.{% endraw %}
