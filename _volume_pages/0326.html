{% raw %}the externa] circumference of the elliptical structure measures 576{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The exterior of the still preserved N.E. portion, on the side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}next the Esquiline , consists of four stories, the three first being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed by arcades, the pillars of which are adorned with half-columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian order in the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stories respectively. A wall with windows between Corinthian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pilasters forms the 4th story. Statues were placed in the arcades of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 2nd and 3rd stories, as appears from the representations on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient coins. At the ends of the diameters are the four triple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Principal Entrances , those next to the Esquiline and Caslius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being destined for the emperor, the others for the solemn proces¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sion before the beginning of the games, and for the introduction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the animals and machinery. On the side next the Esquiline are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen traces of the stucco-decorations, which were restored under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pius VII., and were once used as models by Giovanni da Udiue,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pupil of Raphael. The arcades of the lowest story served as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrances for the spectators, and were furnished with numbers up to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ixxx. (Nos. xxiii. to liv. still exist), in order to indicate the stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cases to the different seats. Below, on the exterior, are two rows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of arcades, and then a massive substructure for the seats. Every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fourth arch contains a staircase.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Part of the Tiers of Seats is still distinguishable, the foremost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which, called the Podium, was destined for the emperor, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}senators, and the Vestal Virgins. The emperor occupied a raised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seat here, called the Pulvinar, and the others had seats of honour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above the Podium rose three other classes of seats, the first of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was allotted to the knights. The humbler spectators occupied the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}last division, in a colonnade, on the roof of which were stationed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sailors of the imperial fleet for the purpose of stretching sail-cloth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the whole amphitheatre to exclude the burning rays of the sun.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Apertures are still seen in the external coping, with corbels below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them, for the support "of the masts to which the necessary ropes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were attached.{% endraw %}
