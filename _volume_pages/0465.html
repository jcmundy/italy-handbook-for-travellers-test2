{% raw %}route, which, lying higher, affords a more unobstructed view than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the present lower level of the road, and may be reached by crossing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fields. About 2 M. from the city-gate we reach the ruins called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tor de' Schiavi, which probably belonged to an extensive villa of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gordians.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}First, to the left of the road, is a hexagonal structure, almost entirely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fallen to decay. A column in the centre and the additional erection on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the summit, both mediaeval, impart a grotesque appearance to the place.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Farther on is a circular building with niches and dome, used in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}middle ages as a church, and decorated with now nearly obliterated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes; below, entered from the back, is a vault supported by strong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pillars in the centre. Both these buildings are supposed to have belonged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a bath-establishment. Among the extensive ruins on the right of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road are a few columbaria.{% endraw %}
