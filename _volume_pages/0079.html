{% raw %}to Voltirra. VOLTERRA. -J. Route. 11{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Cathedral (PL 8) was consecrated in 1120 by Pope Calix-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tus II. , enlarged in 1254 by Niccolh Pisano, and restored in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}16th cent. The facade dates from the 13th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Intekiou is remarkable for its rich marble decorations and sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tures. The old Pulpit is adorned with sculptures of the 13th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those on the high altar are by Mino da Fiesole. The ''Oratorio di S. Carlo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the right transept contains several unimportant pictures and an ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mirable 'Annunciation by Luca Signorelli, 1491.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent is the baptistery of S. Giovanni (PI. 6). an octagonal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church, supposed to date from the 7th cent. ," which occupies the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}site of an ancient temple of the sun. The entrance-archway and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capitals of the columns, decorated with animals and birds, are works{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 13th cent. ; the fine arch of the high-altar is by Balsimelli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}da Settignano (16th cent.), the octagonal font by Andrea di San-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sovino (1502), and the ciborium by Mino da Fiesole (1471).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Lino, a church and monastery, founded in 1480 by Raffaele{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maffei, contains the tomb of that scholar with a recumbent statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Silvio da Fiesole.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Francesco (PL 10), with the Gothic chapel of the Confra-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ternita della Croce di Giorno of 1315, contains frescoes from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life of the Saviour and the legend of the Cross by Cienni di Fran¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cesco di Ser Cienni of Florence, 1410.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The most interesting object in Volterra is the *Museo Nazio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nale, formerly called the Museo Civico, consisting of a valuable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection of inscriptions, coins, bronzes, statues, and vases, now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contained in the Palazzo Tagassi(P\. 20), Via Vittorio Emanuele.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tickets of admission (1 fr.) are obtained in the Cartoleria Maris,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Guidi (Sundays free).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The museum, established in 1731, and greatly enriched by the collections{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the erudite Mario Guarnacci in 1761, has lately been admirably arranged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Cavaliere N. Maffei. Seven rooms on the lower floor and as many on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the upper are occupied by the collection of Cinerary Urns (upwards of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}400). These are generally about 3 ft. in length, and date from the latest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period of Etruscan art, i.e. the 3rd or 2nd cent. B.C. The subjects are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more interesting than the execution, which is for the most part very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediocre. A few of them are composed of terracotta and sandstone, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most of them are of the alabaster of the environs. On the lid is the greatly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reduced recumbent effigy of the deceased; the sides are adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs, and some of them bear traces of painting and gilding. The re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presentations on the urns are partly derived from the peculiar sphere of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etruscan life, partly from Greek mythology. From the former, parting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scenes are the most frequent; the deceased, equipped as a rider, is escorted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a messenger who bears a long sack containing provisions for the jour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ney or is accompanied by Charon with the hammer. Sacrifices and funeral-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}processions occur frequently, as well as banquets, races, contests of skill,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. Greek mythology has supplied an abundant selection of subjects, e.g.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ulysses with the Sirens and with Circe, the abduction of Helen, death of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Clytemnestra, Orestes and the Furies, the Seven before Thebes, Polynices and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eteocles, CEdipus with the Sphynx , CEdipus slaying his father. There is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a singular blending of luxuriance and melancholy in the subjects as well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as in the treatment of these works, and the same peculiarity is often ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served in the subsequent development of Etruscan art. — Five other rooms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contain marble sculptures, vases (mostly of a later style), coins, bronzes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}utensils, gold ornaments, and fine glass vessels.{% endraw %}
