{% raw %}The ** Piazza di S. Pietro is a square with an elliptical space{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in front, enclosed by the imposing colonnades of Bernini. Its length{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as far as the portico of the church is 370 yds., and its greatest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breadth 260 yds. Each of the colonnades, which were erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1667, contains four series of columns of the Doric order. Three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered passages, the central of which has space for two carriages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abreast, are formed hy 284 columns and 88 buttresses. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roofs are placed 162 statues of saints in Bernini's style. The cost of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the construction amounted to 850,000 scudi; the pavement, laid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down under Benedict XIII., alone cost 88,000 scudi. The effect is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very imposing, and the piazza forms an appropriate adjunct to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}largest church in the world.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The great Obelisk in the centre of the piazza, which is destitute{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of hieroglyphics, was brought from Heliopolis to Rome by Caligula{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and placed in the Vatican Circus, and is the only monument of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kind which has never been overthrown.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under Sixtus V. in 1586 this huge monument, estimated by Fontana to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}weigh about 500 tons, was removed by means of rollers from its origi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nal position, and on 10th Sept. erected under the superintendence of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Domenico Fontana on its present site. Representations of this extremely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}difficult undertaking are frequently seen. It is related that Fontana in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}construction of his machines had omitted to make allowance for the tension{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the ropes produced by the enormous weight, and that at the most criti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cal moment, although the bystanders were prohibited under pain of death{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from shouting, one of the 800 workmen, the sailor Bresca di S. Rcmo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exclaimed: 'Acqua alle funi!' (water on the ropes), thus solving the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}difficulty. As a reward, his relations (of Bordighera near S. Remo) were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granted the privilege of providing the palm-branches on Palm Sunday for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Peter's.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the pavement round the obelisk is placed an indicator of tlie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}points of the compass. At the sides are two handsome *Fountains,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}45 ft. in height, the one next the Vatican erected by Maderna, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other under Innocent XL On each side, between the obelisk and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fountains , is a round slab of stone indicating the centres of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}radii of the colonnades, each series of which appears thence as one.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the sides of the steps leading to the portico of St. Peter's, formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stood the statues of SS. Peter and Paul which are now at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance to the Sacristy (p. 286), and were replaced under Pius IX.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by works of De Fabris and Tadolini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right, at the end of the colonnades, is the entrance to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Vatican, called Portone di Bronzo, where the Swiss guard is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stationed (comp. p. 118).{% endraw %}
