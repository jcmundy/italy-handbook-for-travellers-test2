{% raw %}The Vatican Collection of Antiquities, the finest in the world, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun by the Popes Julius II., Leo X., Clement VII., and Paul III. in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Belvedere, which was erected by Bramante under Julius II. and commands a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnificent view of Rome. Here, for example, were preserved the Torso of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hercules, the Apollo Belvedere, and the Laocoon. Clement XIV. (Ganganelli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d. 1774) determined to institute a more extensive collection, in consequence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which the Museo Pio-Clementino arose under him and his successor Pius VI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The museum was arranged by the celebrated E. Q. Visconli. It was despoiled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its costliest treasures by the French in 1797, but most of these were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored to Pius VII. in 1816 after the Treaty of Paris. Pius VII. extended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the collection by adding to it the Museo Chiaramonti, and in 1821 the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Braccio Nuovo; and Gregory XVI. added the Egyptian and the Etruscan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museum.{% endraw %}
