{% raw %}At the small chapel beyond the church of Domine quo Vadis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 343) we take the field-road, which is very muddy after rain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left, leading for 1/2 M. between hedges. On reaching the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open fields, we follow the road descending to the left to the mill.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the latter is situated the so-called Temple of the Deus Redi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}culus , a Roman tomb of Hadrian's time, on an ancient road which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly issued from the now closed Porta Latina. The building has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been assumed hy some, but without authority, to he a temple erect¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed by the Romans after the retreat of Hannibal. The architecture{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is tasteful, and the brick ornaments, the Corinthian pilasters, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half-columns on the S. wall, and the cornicing should be noticed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The interior (25 c.) contains two stories with groined vaulting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Returning hence to the road, we may next ascend the valley of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Almo, or Caffarella. The carriage-road is followed in a straight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}direction; after 5 min. a gate (cancello) is passed through, immedi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ately beyond which a road diverges to the Tenuta on the left;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2 min. farther, beyond a second cancello, the carriage-road, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be quitted in order to follow the path hy the brook, ascends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right to S. Urbano (see below). This leads to the so-called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grotto of Egeria, which was sought for here owing to a misinter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretation of a passage of Juvenal, and a confusion between the Au¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relian and the Servian walls. The 'grotto' is a Nymphaum, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was originally covered with marble, the shrine of the brook Almo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which now flows past it in an artificial channel, and was erected at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a somewhat late period. A niche in the posterior wall contains the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mutilated statue of the river-god, standing on corbels from which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}water flows. The niches in the lateral walls were also once filled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The footpath now passes a small, but formerly more extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wood on the hill, commanding an admirable view of the Campagna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Alban Mts., where, according to the account of the ciceroni,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Numa is said to have held his interviews with the nymph Egeria.{% endraw %}
