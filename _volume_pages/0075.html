{% raw %}From Leghorn to Volterra. A visit to Volterra, the antiquities of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are interesting, is most conveniently accomplished from Leghorn.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Railway via Cecina to Saline, 501/2 M., in 23/4-3 hrs.; express to Cecina{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 fr. 90, 4 fr. 15, 2 fr. 65 c.; ordinary trains 5 fr. 60, 3 fr. 85, 2 fr. 65 c.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Cecina to Le Saline 3 fr. 25, 2 fr. 25, 1 fr. 55 c. — Dilioence from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Saline to Volterra in 2 hrs. (fare i'f-j fr.). Those who intend to continue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their journey southwards by the Maremme line should leave the bulk of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their luggage at Cecina.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Volterra to Siena (about 31 M.) there is no direct conveyance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A diligence runs twice a week only to Colle (generally on Mon. and Thurs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at noon), where it corresponds with another running thence to the rail¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way-station Poggibonsi, in time for the afternoon train to Siena and Or-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vieto. Comp. p. 15. One-horse carriage to Poggibonsi 14-15 fr., with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}digression to S. Gimignano (p. 16) 16 fr., fee 1 fr. ; the bargain should be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made beforehand.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Volterra may also be reached from Pontedera, a station on the Flo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rence and Pisa line, by driving up the valley of the Era (5-6 hrs.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Leghorn to (32 M.) Cecina (Maremme Railway), see pp. 1, 2.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The branch-line to Saline diverges here and ascends on the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank of the Cecina, traversing a district remarkable for its mineral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wealth. — 5^2 M- San Martino; l01/2 M. Casino di Terra; 15 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ponte Ginori; IS1^ M. Saline, the terminus, in a bleak situation,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where malaria prevails in summer. The extensive salt-works in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vicinity supply the whole of Tuscany with salt and yield a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable revenue.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following excursion, for which a carriage may be hired at Saline,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is interesting to geologists. We first drive to Pomarance, a pleasant town,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}famed in the Renaissance period for its earthenware, with a large cha¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teau of Count Larderello, and in about 3 hrs. reach Larderello on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Cerboli, the central point of the boracie acid works belonging to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Larderello family, which are politely shown to visitors. The ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cursion may be extended towards the S., by Bagno del Morbo, Casielnuovo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sasso, and Monterotondo, to Massa Marittima (p. 3), a drive of 3 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more. Near Sasso and Monterotondo in particular the country is covered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with clouds of smoke, and the hot surface of the earth with incrustations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of sulphur, sulphate of iron, etc. Near Monterotondo is the hot Lago Zol-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreo, a small lake strongly impregnated with boracie acid, which is ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained from it by evaporation by M. Duval, a Frenchman. Count Lar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derello's works yield about 850 tons, and M. Duval's 250 tons annually,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the whole quantity is sent by contract to England, where it is chiefly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used in the manufacture of glass and pottery. The lagoni, or pools{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through which the soffioni or jets of boracie acid in the form of steam{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bubble up, arc all, with the exception of those of Travale, in the region of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Cecina and Cornia, and most probably have a common volcanic origin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road from Saline to (5 M.) Volterra ascends. The country{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents a peculiarly bleak appearance.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Alabaster Works of Volterra are celebrated and afford occupation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to nearly two-thirds of the population , but the patterns chiefly in vogue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are unfortunately in very bad taste. The ordinary kinds of alabaster are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found in the vicinity, the more valuable in the mines of La Castellina,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the S. of Leghorn. A visit to the work-shops is interesting, and suit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able objects for presents, etc. may be purchased here far more advanta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}geously than at Florence or Leghorn.{% endraw %}
