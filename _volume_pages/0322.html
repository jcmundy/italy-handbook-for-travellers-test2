{% raw %}230 IV. Ancient Rome. ROME. Forum Romanum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left of the Via dell' Arco di Settimio Severo ascending{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Capitol, at the entrance to the Via di Marforio, we ohscrvc{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the small church of S. Giuseppe de' Falegnami. Below it (entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the first-named street, 1/2 fr.) is the Career Mamertinus, one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most ancient structures in Rome. It was originally built over a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well, named Tullianum, and thence traditionally attributed to Ser¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vius Tullius, and it was afterwards used as a prison.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It consists of two chambers, one below the other, of very ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}construction. The upper is an irregular quadrilateral, which was probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once adjoined by other similar chambers. An inscription on the front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}records that the building was restored in B.C. 22. The lower chamber,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was originally only accessible through a hole in the ceiling, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}19 ft. long, 10 ft, wide, and 6'/-2 ft. high. The vaulting is formed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gradual projection of the side walls until they meet. It contains a spring,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which, according to the legend, St. Peter, who was imprisoned here under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nero, miraculously caused to flow in order to baptise bis jailors. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building has therefore been named S. Pietro in Carcere since the 15th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}century. In this dungeon perished Jugurtha after having been deprived{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of food for six days, Vercingetorix, and other conquered enemies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sallust, in recording the execution of Catiline's confederates, describes the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prison thus: — 'Est in carcere locus, quod Tullianum appellatur, circiter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duodecini pedes humi depressus. Eum miniunt undique parietes atque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}insuper camera lapideis fornicibus vincta; sed incultu tenebris odore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fa-da atque terribilis ejus facies est.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nearly opposite stands the church of SS. Luca e Martina, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the site of an ancient building. It consists of an upper and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower church ; the latter being of very ancient origin, and the former{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in the 17th cent, hy Pietro da Cortona.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the opposite side of the Via Bonella, which leads to the Aca¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demy of S. Luca (p. 237) and the Forum of Augustus (p. 238), is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church of S. Adriano, with its unadorned facade, uninteresting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like the last-mentioned, and also occupying the site of an ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifice, prohably the Curia Hostilia, which was subsequently re-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected under the name of Curia Julia hy Caesar and Augustus, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was used as an assembly-hall by the senate. The church was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Honorius I. in the 7th cent, and afterwards restored.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Velia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Colosseum. Baths of Titus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A hill, named the Velia in ancient times, connects the Palatine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Esquiline, its highest point being marked by the Arch of Titus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}( 97 ft.). How far the Forum, the lowest part of which was at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the Vicus Tuscus (p. 226), extended up this hill, is a point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the. result of future excavations must determine. The Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sacra is flanked by an uninterrupted series of public monuments.{% endraw %}
