{% raw %}one at Tibur, is also pointed out by the guides. From S. Maria we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cross the meadows between old olive trees, and in about 20 min.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reach a road descending in a few minutes to the left, to the Ponte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dell' Acquoria, by which we cross the Anio. On the left bank of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river we ascend the hill to the left, partly by an ancient pavement,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and join the road from Rome. Here, to the left, are the colonnades,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which have long been visible from the opposite bank, of the so-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called Villa of Maecenas, where an iron manufactory is now estab¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lished. On the other side of the road is an ancient circular build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, known by the curious name of Tempio della Tosse, or 'temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the cough', probably a tomb of the Turcia, or Tuscia family. From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this point to the Porta del Colle of Tivoli about y2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Villa d' Este , near the Porta del Colle, is worthy of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visit. (The lower entrance being closed, we must ascend, and in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street to the right proceed to the upper entrance adjacent to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Francesco.) The Villa was erected by Pirro Ligorio in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1549 for Card. Ippolito d'Este, and presented by the Duke of Mod-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ena to Card. Hohenlohe. Though sadly neglected, it still retains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traces of its former splendour. In the casino are frescoes by Fede¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rigo Zucchero and Muziano (damaged). The garden contains terraces,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are best seen from the cypress plateau with the four foun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains, grottoes with cascades, densely shaded avenues, magnificent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groups of trees of most varied hues, and charming points of view.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Villa Braschi, founded by Pius VI., and the Terrace of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jesuits' College near the Porta S. Croce also afford magnificent views{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Campagna and Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Besides the ancient villas already mentioned there are traces{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of many others on the slopes near Tivoli. In those below the Greek{% endraw %}
{% raw %}college, which have been named villas of Cassius and M. Brutus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were found a number of works of art, several of which are ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hibited in the Sala delle Muse in the Vatican (see p. 312).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}From Tivoli to Subiaco, 25 M., a Vetlurino runs daily in 5 hrs.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starting early in the morning (fare 4 fr.); a seat in other conveyances may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also frequently be procured. Carriage with two horses generally 20 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a fee. (From Rome to Subiaco 8 fr.; starting-point and booking, see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 113.){% endraw %}
