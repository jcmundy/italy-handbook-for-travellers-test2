{% raw %}342 Envir. of Rome. TRE FONTANE. Short{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abbey delle Tre Fontane (ad aquas Salvias), now almost de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serted on account of the unhealthiness of the situation. The build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing was made over in 1868 to French Trappists. By extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plantations of the rapidly growing Eucalyptus the sanitary con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dition of the place is said to have improved since 1874. The name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is derived from the legend that the apostle Paul was executed here,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and that his head was observed to make three distinct leaps, cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}responding to which there welled forth three different fountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The court surrounding the three churches is approached by an arch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way bearing traces of painting, which is believed to have belonged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to an'earlier church of John the Baptist (visitors ring; 30 c; a monk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acts as guide, 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"SS. Vincenzo ed Anastasio, the largest of the churches, a basilica in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient style, was founded by Honorius I., restored in 1221 by Hon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orius III., as the inscription to the left of the choir records, and is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again undergoing restoration. It has retained many of its mediaeval{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peculiarities, and in particular the marble windows over the nave. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portico bears traces of paintings, including the portrait of Honorius 111.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pillars are embellished with the figures of the Twelve Apostles,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Marcantonio's engravings of designs by Raphael, recently spoiled by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restoration.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right of this is the second church, the circular S. Maria Scala{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Coeli, so called from the 'vision' here vouchsafed to St. Bernhard, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom Innocent III. had presented the monastery, of a heavenly ladder,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on which angels were conducting to heaven the persons whom his prayers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had released from purgatory. In its present form the church dates from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the close of the loth century. The tribune contains good mosaics by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}F. Zuccaro: the saints Zeno, Bernard, Vincent the deacon, and Vine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Anastasius, who are revered by Clement VIII. and Card. Aldobrandini,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the finisher of the church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The third of the churches, S. Paolo edle Tre Fontane, stands on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spot where the apostle is said to have been beheaded, and contains the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three springs already mentioned. In the centre is an antique mosaic re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presenting the four seasons, found in Ostia, and presented by the Pope in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1869. On the spring to the right stands the column of white marble to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which St. Paul is said to have been bound at the time of his execulion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The present edifice dates from 1599.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hills above the abbey, which are honeycombed with puzzo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lana pits, command delightful views.{% endraw %}
