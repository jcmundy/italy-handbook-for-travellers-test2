{% raw %}Florence, see vol. I. of this Handbook. The train describes a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}curve round the town and runs along the N. bank of the Arno. By{% endraw %}
{% raw %}degrees the valley contracts; Fiesole on the height to the left long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains visible. 7'/2 M. Compiobbi. The surrounding heights are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}barren, the slopes and valley well cultivated; to the left is seen the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountain chain of the Pratomagno. 12*^ M. Pontassieve, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}influx of the Sieve into the Arno; to the left a beautiful glimpse of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the valley of the Sieve. The train passes through a short tunnel,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and then crosses to the left bank of the Arno. 17y2 M. Rignano;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the train passes through another tunnel and reaches (22 M.) Incisa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a couspicuous castle. The river forces its way here through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}limestone rock, whence the name of the village. 25 M. Figline. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a paheontological point of view the valley of the Arno near Figline,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and farther on, near Montevarchi and Arezzo, is very interesting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}owing to the great number of fossil bones of the elephant, rhino¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceros, mastodon, hippopotamus, hyaena, tiger, bear, etc., which have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been found here. This basin seems to have been filled with a fresh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}water lake at some remote period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30 M. S. Giovanni, a small town to the left, the birthplace of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the celebrated painter Masaccio (in 1402; d. at Florence 1443), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Giovanni da S. Giovanni (1590-1636). The Cathedral contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pictures by the latter: Beheading of John the Baptist, Annuncia¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion, etc. The sacristy of the church of S. Maria delle Grazie, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old town-wall, contains a Madonna, formerly attributed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Masaccio, and a few other old paintings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}34 M. Montevarchi (Locanda a"Italia , in the main street), a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small town with 9600 inhab. The loggia of the principal church in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the piazza is embellished with a richly sculptured relief by Della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Robbia ; opposite is the house of Benedetto Varchi (d. 1555), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florentine historian and independent favourite of Cosmo. The .-le-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cademia di Val d'Arnese contains a valuable collection of fossil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bones (see above).{% endraw %}
