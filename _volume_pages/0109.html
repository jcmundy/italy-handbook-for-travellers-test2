{% raw %}from Siena. MONTE OLIVETO. 5. Route. 37{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brone, and reach Buonconvento, a small town with 3400 inhab., where the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emperor Henry VII. died in 1313. It was fortified by the Sienese in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}136G; the churches contain early Sienese pictures. — P.eyond Buoncon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vento we leave the high road by a road diverging to the left, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads across chalk hills in numerous windings (pedestrians may effect a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saving of fully V/z M. by taking the short-cuts) to the famous, but now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suppressed Benedictine monastery of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Monte Oliveto Maggiore (a tolerable dinner may be obtained from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the four monks who are left in the building as custodians; for a leng¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thened stay 4 fr. per day), founded in 1320 by Bernardo Tolomei, after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards greatly enriched by donations, and still affording an excellent idea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a. great establishment of the kind. The monks must have been won¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derfully energetic to have been able to transform the sterile chalk-soil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here into a smiling oasis. jEneas Sylvius Piccolomini (Pius II.) gives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an interesting description of the monastery in his annals.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls of the Monastery Court are adorned with celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Frescoes by Luca Signorelli (1497) and Ant. Bazzi, called Sodoma (1505),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing scenes from the legend of St. Benedict. The order of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pictures does not correspond with the date of their execution. The series{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begins with the first picture on the wall opposite the entrance (adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance to the church), representing St. Benedict's departure from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}home, by Sodoma. The earliest part of the series are the frescoes on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance-wall, executed by Signorelli, eight in number: Totila kneeling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the saint; Soldier in disguise, attempting to deceive the saint; Temp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tation of the fasting monk; Punishment of two monks addicted to dainties ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Resuscitation of a dead man whom Satan has thrown from a wall; Con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}juration of Satan; Fall of the idol; Fall of a house. — The 'Sending forth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Missionaries', on the left of the corner to the right, is by Riccio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but all the other pictures are by Sodoma, whose sense of beauty is every¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where apparent, though he is doubtlessly far inferior to Signorelli in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}depth and excellence of conception and execution. In the first pictures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sodoma we can trace a resemblance to the frescoes of Pinturicchio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Cathedral library at Siena, and, in the others, features that recall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leonardo da Vinci. — The Church (entrance to the left of the monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court) which was modernised in the last century, contains little to detain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}us, beyond the handsome choir stalls and reading-desk, in inlaid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work, by Fra Giov. da Verona (1502-5). — In the Libreria arc a door and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cabinet, also beautifully inlaid by the same master. — The visitor should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also notice the extensive stables at the back of the monastery, the dif¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ferent sections of which bear the names and arms of the chief towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Italy, in order that guests might know on arriving where to put up{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their horses.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}beautiful views. It was a populous and prosperous place down to 1348,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when the plague swept away nearly all the inhabitants, but is now of no{% endraw %}
