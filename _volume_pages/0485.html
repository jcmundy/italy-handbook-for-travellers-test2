{% raw %}The Emissarius, an imposing work, was constructed according to tradi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion by the Romans in B.C. 397, during the siege of Veii, when the lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rose to an unusual height, but it is probably of still more remote origin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is hewn in the solid rock. At the entrance is a large stone building{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resembling a nympha:um. The channel is 7-10 ft. in height, and issues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3/4 M. below Albano by the village of La Mola, where the water is used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a motive power for mills, descending thence to the Tiber. The custo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dian floats lighted pieces of candle on boards down the stream, in order{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to give visitors an idea of its length (about, 1300 yds.).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}About 3/4 M. to the S.E. of Albano lies Ariccia. The road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes the Etruscan tomb mentioned above. It then crosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imposing * Viaduct, erected by Pius IX. in 1846-63, 334 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length, and 192 ft. in height, consisting of three series of arcades{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of six, twelve, and eighteen arches respectively, one above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other, which crosses the valley separating Albano from Ariccia. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, a view of the extensive plain as far as the sea, to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the *Park of the Palazzo Chigi, built by Bernini, and situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}immediately to the left beyond the viaduct. This park, containing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine old timber, is kept in as natural a condition as possible. Per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mission to visit it should be obtained from the porter or gardener in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the palace (fee i/2-l fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ariccia (Cafe in the piazza), an insignificant place, frequently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attracts visitors in summer on account of the proximity of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}woods. The women of Ariccia and Genzano are famed for their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beauty. The ancient Aricia, which belonged to the Latin League, lay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towards the S., in the Valle Aricciana (981 ft.), an extinct crater{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below the modern town , while the latter occupies the site of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Arx or citadel. According to Horace (Sat. i. 5) this was the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first station on the Via Appia, which runs towards Genzano on mas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sive, still visible substructions, at the foot of the modern town.{% endraw %}
