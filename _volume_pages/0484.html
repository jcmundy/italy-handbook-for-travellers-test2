{% raw %}The wine of Albano is praised by Horace, and is still much esteemed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the upper part of the town, between the monastery of S. Paolo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the loftily situated Capuchin monastery lay an Amphitheatre, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scanty remains of which are seen from the road. The church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria della Rotonda stands on the foundations of an ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular temple. The ruins in the street of Gesil e Maria are sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posed to be the remains of baths.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via Appia intersects Albano in a straight direction. Outside{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. entrance of the town, to the right of the road, rise the re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mains of a large tomb, called without foundation the Tomb of Pom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pey. The avenue, diverging to the right by this tomb, is known as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Galleria di Sotto, see below. •— On the S. side of the town, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}■the right on the road to Ariccia (to the left of the ancient road),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands another ancient *Tomb in the Etruscan style, consisting of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}massive cube, originally surrounded by four obtuse cones, of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two are still standing, with a fifth in the centre. It was formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supposed to be the tomb of the Horatii and Curiatii, and now, on no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}better authority, is regarded as that ofArnns, a son of Porsena, who{% endraw %}

{% raw %}About ll/t M. to the N.W. of Albano lies Castel Gandolfo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is reached by two shady avenues of beautiful evergreen oaks:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the so-called *Galleria di Sopra, or 'upper gallery', beginning at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Capuchin monastery above Albano, and affording fine views of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lake; and the Galleria di Sotto, beginning near the tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pompey at the N.W. entrance to the town, passing the Villa Bar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}berini and Castel Gandolfo, and leading to (33/4 M.) Marino (p. 357 ).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Castel Gandolfo, which belonged to the Savelli in the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages, has been the property of the Popes since 1596. It is an in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}significant place with a large Papal Palace, erected by Urban VIII.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from designs by Carlo Maderna, and splendidly situated high above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the precipitous margin of the Alban lake. The chateau was formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a favourite summer residence of the popes (including Pius IX.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and by a law passed on 13th May, 1871, received the privilege of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exterritoriality.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Lake of Albano (964 It. above the sea-level, 490 ft. deep),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 6 M. in circumference, is the crater of an extinct volcano, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sombre and melancholy aspect, although its banks are well cultivat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed. It is fed by abundant, subterraneous springs, and is drained by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a very ancient Emissarius which issues below Castel Gandolfo.{% endraw %}
