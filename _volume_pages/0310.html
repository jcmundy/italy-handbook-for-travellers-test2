{% raw %}In the most ancient times the Capitol and Palatine were separated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a deep and marshy valley. The pavement by the column of Phocas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still lies 38 ft. only above the level of the sea, and 22 ft. above the level{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Tiber, but 13 ft. lower than the height of an ordinary inundation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In consequence of the lowness of this valley, it was, as may well )je{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supposed, a difficult and tedious task to raise the level and drain the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marsh. For this purpose Tarquinius Priscus, the fifth of the kings, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to have constructed the Cloaca Maxima, which still renders good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}service (p. 249); and several canalicolae, or tributary drains which fell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into the main channel, have recently been discovered. Tradition makes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this hollow the scene of the conflict of the Romans under Romulus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against the Sabines under Titus Tatius after the rape of the Sabine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}women. After the hostile tribes were amalgamated into a single state,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they chose the Forum as its centre, and it was here that some of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most famous scenes in the history of the Roman Republic were enacted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. side (S. Adriano) lay the Curia Hostilia, or council-hall, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is said to have been erected by King Tullus Hostilius; while on the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side, at the foot of the Palatine (S. Maria Liberatrice), rose the Temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Vesta, with its eternal fire, and the Regia, or dwelling of the Pontifex{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maximus, the president of the Roman hierarchy. The Comitium, or open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}space in the centre, was the place where popular assemblies were wont{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be held. The Forum was bounded by streets, the most important of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was the Via Sacra ascending to the Capitol. In the Forum and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its environs building operations and various changes have been taking{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place at intervals for upwards of two thousand years, and it is therefore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not to be wondered at that a number of topographical questions regarding{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it are still unsolved, and that the imagination of scholars has indulged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the most extravagant flights with regard to this spot more than any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other in Rome. It is, however, ascertained that the Forum extended from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the foot of the Capitol, sloping downwards towards the E., although it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has sometimes been erroneously supposed that it extended from N. to S.{% endraw %}
