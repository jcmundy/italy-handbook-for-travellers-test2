{% raw %}by Clement V., and decorated with paintings by Giotto. A second{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fire destroyed the church in 1360, after which is was rebuilt by Ur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ban IV. and Gregory XL It was again altered by Martin V. (1430),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eugene IV., and Alexander VI., and modernised by Pius IV. (1560),{% endraw %}

{% raw %}(1734). Five important Councils have been held in this church,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}viz. those of 1123, 1139, 1179, 1215, and 1512.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Facade by .Alessandro Galilei, with a portico and open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loggia above it, is the best of this description in Rome. From the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}central arcade of the loggia the Pope used to pronounce his bene¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diction on Ascension-day.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left in the Portico is an ancient statue of Constantine the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great (PL 1), found in the Thermae of that emperor. Of the five entrances{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Porta Santa (PL 2) on the right is closed, but is opened in the year{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of jubilee. The central entrance (PI. 3) possesses two bronze doors with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garlands and other decorations. The portico is 33 ft. in depth and 174 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in width; the church 426 ft. in length.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. The Nave, which is flanked by double aisles, is supported by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twelve pillars, the work of Borromini, partly enclosing the ancient columns.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the niches are the Twelve Apostles, of the school of Bernini; reliefs by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Algardi. Over these are the figures of twelve prophets. The ceiling, said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to have been designed by Michael Angelo, is more probably by Giacomo della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porta. The richly inlaid pavement dates from the time of Martin V. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right and left at the end of the nave are the only two ancient granite columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now visible. Below, in front of the Confessio (PL 4), is the 'monument of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pope Martin V. (d. 1431), in bronze by Simone, brother of Donatello. — In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre of the Transept, which is raised by four steps, is the "Canopy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. 5), a beautiful work of 1367, lately restored, with paintings by Barna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}da Siena, dating from 1391), but freely restored. It contains numerous relics,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}including, it is said, the heads of SS. Peter and Paul. Below it is the high-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar (altare papale), at which the pope alone reads mass, containing a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wooden table from the catacombs which is said to have been used as an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar by St. Peter. The transept was restored under Clement VIII. by Giac.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Porta (1603) and adorned with frescoes. Here to the left is the great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Altar of Ike Sacrament (PL 6), with four ancient columns of gilded bronze,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which once belonged to the original basilica. The chapel of the choir{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 7; generally closed), to the left of the tribune, contains a portrait of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Martin V. by Scip. Gaetano, and an altar-piece by the Cav. d'Arpino.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Tribune and Choir Passage are at present undergoing restoration.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Tribune (PI. 8) is embellished with 'mosaics, either originally executed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or of ancient workmanship restored by Jacobus Torriti (1290) : the Saviour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enveloped in clouds ; below at the sides of a cross, (1.) the Virgin, at whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feet Nicholas IV. kneels, SS. Francis, Peter, and Paul, and (r.) John the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baptist, St. John , St. Andrew, and other saints. To the right in the transept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two fine columns of giallo antico. Adjoining the organ is a monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the philologist Laurentius Valla (d. 1465), a canon of this church. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Choir Passage, called 'Portico Leonino' from having been constructed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Leo I., entered to the right behind the tribune, is embellished on each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side with mosaic tablets, the subjects of which relate to the construction of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church; farther on, to the right, the kneeling figure of a pope (10th cent.);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left, in the centre, an altar with ancient crucifix, on each side statues{% endraw %}
