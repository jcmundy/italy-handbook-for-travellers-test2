{% raw %}The *Pincio (PI. I, 18), the collis hortorum, or 'hill of gardens',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the ancients, probably derived its name of Mons Pincius from a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palace of the Pincii situated here in the later period of the empire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Here were once the celebrated gardens of Lucullus, in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Messalina, the wife of Claudius, afterwards celebrated her orgies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A vineyard belonging to the monastery of S. Maria at the foot of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill was converted by Valadier, during the Napoleonic regime,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into beautiful pleasure-grounds, the Passeggiata of Rome. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a fashionable drive in the evening, when the visitors to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grounds frequently pay and receive visits in their carriages, pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}senting a gay and characteristic scene which the traveller should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not omit to see. A military band generally plays here on Sundays{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Thursdays (but the days are sometimes changed), two hours{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before sunset, attracting a large audience of all classes. The walks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are shaded by plantations and groups of trees, and, on the suggest¬{% endraw %}
