{% raw %}Once more Rome occupies a foremost place in the history of art{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when Pope Nicholas V. (1447-1455), a Humanist, vies with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medici in his passion for books and building. He is bent upon a re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}novation of the Vatican Quarter; his ambition is to erect a papal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence of surpassing splendour; nay, he entertains designs on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the St. Peter's pile itself and contemplates its reconstruction. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most imposing work of this period was the Venetian Palace begun by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro Barbo (1455), afterwards Pope Paul II., which, like the Albergo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dell' Orso recently as it were again discovered, is to a great extent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediaeval in character. Leon Battista Alberti, who resided in Rome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about this time and died there in 1472, is supposed to have furnish¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed the plans for this palace.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}So far indeed had the fostering of art become obligatory on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupants of the papal chair, that they could not neglect this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}function without forfeiting their individual influence, and impairing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dignity of their office. The right powers were not, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}immediately at hand, which should give effect to the building pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jects of these Sovereign Pontiffs, enamoured as they were of splen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dour in every shape. The architect who during the pontificate of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sixtus IV. (1471-1484) was most employed, Baccio Pintelli, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a practitioner of moderate skill, and far behind the great Florentines{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his day. The building of S. Agosti.no and S. Pietro in Montorio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as well as the facades of SS. Apostoli and S. Pietro in Vincoli were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from his plans. His most celebrated work is the Popes' private{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chapel in the Vatican, called after the Pope Sixtus the Sistine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chapel, which owes its chief attractions far less to its architectural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merits, than to the artistic decoration of wall and ceiling.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abundant employment together with the favour which artists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found with dignitaries of the Church had already allured numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscan and Umbrian Painters to Rome. Amongst those thus engag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed in beautifying the churches of Rome and the Vatican Palace we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meet such Florentine celebrities as Maestri Sandro Botticelli, Filip-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pino Lippi, Domenico Ghirlandajo, Cosimo Rosselli; and from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Umbrian School the immediate forerunner of Michael Angelo, bold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luca Signorelli, along with Perugino and Pinturicchio. An attempt{% endraw %}
