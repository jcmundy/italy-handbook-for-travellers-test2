{% raw %}338 The Catacombs. ROME. Catac. of St. Peter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of SS. Peter and Marcellinus, near Torre Pignattara{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lp. 349), are among the most extensive. The ceiling of a lofty chapel bears{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an Enthroned Christ, with St. Paul on the right, and St. Peter on the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with four saints below, quite in the style of the earliest mosaics. Other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes, such as two scenes of Agapae (love-feasts), belong to the 3rd cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The representation is often very realistic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of St. Pontianus, >/« M. from the Porta Portese, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excavated in the breccia of Monte Verde. At the foot of a staircase de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scending into them, is a basin with water, serving as a baptistery. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wall beyond is the Baptism of Christ (with a stag near the Jordan),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above a large cross in the later style. Above the staircase are two large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}medallions with heads of Christ of the 6th and 9th centuries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Oratorio of S. Alessandro, 6 M. from the Porta Pia (p. 350), in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tenuta del Coazzo (permesso at the Propaganda), is a long, half-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subterranean building, the very poor masonry of which is well preserved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the lower part. According to an inscription on the altar, this was the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tomb of Pope Alexander. The oratory is surrounded with lofty passages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still containing undisturbed tombs.{% endraw %}
