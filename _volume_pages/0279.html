{% raw %}The Italian translation runs thus: —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lQuesti e quel Raffaele, cui vivo vinla{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Esser temea Ncduru, e morto estinta'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A lengthy inscription beside it announces that Raphael's remains were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}placed in a new sarcophagus in 1833. The statue of the Madonna on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar, by Lorenzetto, was executed in accordance with Raphael's last will.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Pantheon is also the last resting-place of Ann. Caracci, Tadd.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zucchero, Bald. Peruzzi, Perino del Vaga, Giov. da Udine, and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated artists.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A visit to the interior by moonlight should on no account be omitted,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the sacristan must be informed in good time. Visitors are then ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mitted by the door at the back of the sacristy, Via della Palombella 10.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To ascend the dome a special permesso must be obtained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the back of the Pantheon are situated the ruins of the Ther¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mae of Agrippa, the proximity of which to the Pantheon once gave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rise to the absurd conjecture that it originally belonged to the baths,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was afterwards converted into a temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Piazza of the Pantheon we may proceed towards the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. through the Via de' Pastini to the Piazza di Pietra (p. 149); or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we may turn at once to the left, towards the N., cross the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Capranica, with the small theatre of that name, and reach Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Citorio (p. 148); or, lastly, we may follow the Via del Seminario.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which also runs towards the E., to S. Ignazio (p. 149).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leaving the Pantheon, we now proceed towards the S. E.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the Via della Minerva to the Piazza della Minerva{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL II, 16), where the church of S. Maria sopra Minerva lies on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, and the Hotel de la Minerve opposite to us. In the centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the piazza stands an elephant in marble, on the back of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small obelisk was placed by Bernini in 1667, being one of those,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are said once to have risen in front of a temple of Isis for¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merly situated here. The other obelisk is in the Piazza della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rotonda (p. 195).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Maria sopra Minerva, erected on the ruins of a temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Minerva founded by Domitian, the only Gothic church at Rome,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was probably begun about 1285 by the builders of S. Maria No¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vella at Florence. It was restored in 1848-55, and contains several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valuable works of art.{% endraw %}
