{% raw %}IV. Ancient Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This part of the description embraces the southern portion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the city, beginning with the Capitol, and extending eastwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as far as the Lateran : i. e. the hills of the Capitoline , Palatine,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aventine, Caelius, and the S. slope of the Esquiline. The imposing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monuments and reminiscences of classical antiquity, more of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are daily being brought to light by the excavations, impart its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}characteristic aspect to this, the principal, but now almost deserted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarter of the Republican and Imperial city. A number of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churches, which are extremely interesting to students of Christian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}architecture, as well as the imposing collections of the Capitol and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lateran, also attract numerous visitors.{% endraw %}
