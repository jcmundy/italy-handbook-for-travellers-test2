{% raw %}town and the buildings in the Campus Martius by means of a vast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cutting between the Capitol and the Quirinal was at length carried{% endraw %}

{% raw %}width, and of still greater length. In the part already excavated{% endraw %}

{% raw %}four rows of columns, belonging to the five-hailed Basilica Ulpia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which lay with its sides towards the end of the present piazza.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The central hall was 27 yds., and the whole building 61 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}width. The pavement consisted of slabs of rare marble. It is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uncertain whether the remains of granite columns which have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found and erected here are in their original positions. — Between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this Basilica and the Forum of Augustus lay the Forum Trajani{% endraw %}
{% raw %}properly so called, part of the S.E. semicircular wall of which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still seen in the court of No. 6 Via del Campo Carleo, two stories{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height. The chambers of the ground floor were probably shops.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre of this forum stood Trajan's equestrian statue.{% endraw %}
