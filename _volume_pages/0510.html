{% raw %}The Volscian Mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This mountain-range, which attains an elevation of 4600 ft., is separ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ated on the E. from the principal chain of the Apennines by the valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Sacco, and on the N. from the Alban Mts. by a narrow depression ; it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extends to the S. as far as the Bay of Gaeta, and on the W. is bounded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a dreary and in some places marshy plain adjoining the sea. This district{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was in ancient times the chief seat, of the Volsci, but was at an early period{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subjugated by the Romans and Latinised. Its towns, picturesquely rising{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the mountain-slopes , still bear many traces of the republican epoch of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy , which add great interest to the naturai attractions of the scenery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These mountains, however, have hitherto been seldom visited, partly tin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}account of the poorness of the inns, and partly owing to their insecure state,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which has improved only quite recently. An excursion to Cori may be ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}complished in one day with the aid of the railway as far as Velletri; so{% endraw %}
