{% raw %}in the passages, Etruscan cinerary urns and Latin inscriptions. Contents of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rooms similar. In the 1st Room the longest Etruscan inscription known,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consisting of 45 lines, as yet undeciphered, and ancient Etruscan sculptures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd Room : Mediaeval coins and other objects. 3rd Room : Ancient bronzes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among which are bronze and silver plates, found in 1810, appurtenances of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chariot, or, as is now supposed, from a tomb. 4th Room: Terracottas and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several painted vases. Lid of a sarcophagus. Death seizing his victims.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5th Room: Cinerary urns of terracotta with traces of painting. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre a sarcophagus of terracotta, with sacrificial procession.{% endraw %}
