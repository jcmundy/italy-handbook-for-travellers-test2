{% raw %}15'/2 M. from Volterra lies Colle, where the roads to Siena and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Poggibonsi diverge. This town, which is frequently mentioned in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}history of the Renaissance, now consists of two parts, Colle Alto and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Colle Basso. The first of these contains the palaces of the old, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now greatly impoverished aristocracy; the Cathedral, dating from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}13th cent., with a facade modernised in bad taste, a marble pulpit,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which the lower part belongs to the 13th cent.. and the upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part, with reliefs of saints, to the 16th, and handsome carved choir-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stalls and episcopal throne of the 17th cent.; and the house of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated architect Arnolfo di Cambio. — At Colle Basso there are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now important iron and glass works.{% endraw %}
