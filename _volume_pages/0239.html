{% raw %}The Palazzo Colonna, begun by Martin V.. and afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}much extended and altered, is now in great part occupied by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French ambassador, and a number of rooms on the ground-floor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing interesting frescoes, are therefore closed to the public.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Picture Gallery, situated on the first floor (daily 11-3, ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cept Sundays and holidays), is entered from the Piazza SS. Apostoli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the gate No. 53. We turn to the left in the court, and ascend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the broad staircase.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the top of the staircase, opposite the entrance to the saloons,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the painted cast of a colossal Medusa head. Traversing a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hall containing family-portraits, we turn to the right into three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ante-rooms adorned with Gobelins, in the second of which are four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient draped statues ; in the third a small ancient statue, belong¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing to a group of playing girls. We then ring at the entrance to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Gallery (fee of y2 fr- on leaving). — The chief objects of in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terest are eleven water-colour landscapes by Gaspar Poussin in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4th Room. They represent a mountain road close to a profound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ravine, a bleak plain lashed by a storm, a calm lake enclosed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}majestic trees, a riven rocky landscape with waterfall, and various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other subjects. Notwithstanding the simplicity and uniformity of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the materials used, these works will not fail to interest the spec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tator owing to the excellence of the composition and drawing. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The portraits of the ancestors of the Colonna family also are deserv¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing of attention, e. g. that of Isabella Colonna by Novelli. a Sicilian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painter of the 17th cent. The following pictures also may be men¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tioned : Lorenzo di Credi, Madonna; Bonifazio, Madonna and saints;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tintoretto, Saints; Rubens, Assumption of Mary. The two early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Flemish Madonnas, surrounded hy small circular pictures (1st{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room), are remarkable for their delicate and miniature-like exe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cution. — All the pictures bear the names of the masters.{% endraw %}
