{% raw %}ELBA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the middle ages it was subject to the Pisans, then to Genoa, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lucca, and to the Appiani of Piombino, and was finally presented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Emp. Charles V. to the Grand-Duke Cosmo I. of Florence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who fortified the harbour of Porto Ferrajo in 1548. As the name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the town indicates, the export and manufacture of iron form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal occupation of the inhabitants (22,000), others of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom are supported by the tunny and sardine fisheries. Elba has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acquired a modern celebrity as the retreat of the dethroned Na¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poleon, from 5th May, 1814, to 26th Feb., 1815. The small palace{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied by the emperor is still shown at Porto Ferrajo, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height above the harbour, between the forts Stella and Falcone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which were erected by Cosmo I., and command a view of the bay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in front, and of the sea in the direction of Piombino at the back.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is now the residence of the governatore, and contains remini¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scences of its former imperial occupant. The cathedral, theatre,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arsenal, etc. of which the island boasts contain nothing which re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quires comment. After the fall of Napoleon in 1815 Elba was re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stored to Tuscany, in the fortunes of which it has since then par¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticipated. Length of the island about 18 M., breadth 6L/2 M., area{% endraw %}
{% raw %}147 sq. M.; it contains several fertile valleys, but lofty and pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cipitous mountains predominate. Monte Capanne, the highest point,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}near the village of Marciana, is 3304 ft. in height. The coast on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the side next the mainland is less abrupt, and produces wine and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fruit of remarkably fine quality, especially in the environs of Ca-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poliveri, where excellent Aleatico is grown. Most of the villages,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such as the picturesque stronghold of Porto Longone, founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Spaniards, are situated on the coast. Rio, where the iron-mines{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are worked, lies more inland. The yield of ore is still abundant,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in ancient times formed a source of wealth to the Etruscans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ferriferous strata lie on the surface, and are recognised at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a distance by the reddish-black appearance of the hills.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Elba and the mainland are the two small islands of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palmajola and Cerboli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. lies the island oiPianosa, the ancient Planasia, which,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as its name indicates, is perfectly flat. To this island Agrippa Post-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}humus, grandson of Augustus, was once banished, and to him are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}referred the considerable Roman remains which still exist here. Far¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ther S. rises Monte Crista, consisting of granite-rock, 6 M. in cir¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cumference. It contains numerous springs, and the ruins of a mon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}astery destroyed by pirates in the 16th cent. Nearer the coast is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giglio, Lat. Igilium, a considerable island containing a village and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vestiges of [Ionian palaces. The highest point is 1630 ft. above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea-level.{% endraw %}
