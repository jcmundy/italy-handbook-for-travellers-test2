{% raw %}In the middle ages, and down to the present day, the Forum was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}popularly known as the Campo Vaccino. Its desolate area was covered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the teams of buffaloes and oxen of the peasantry, and smiths and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carpenters established their workshops around it, while a few isolated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, protruding from the rubbish, alone formed a reminiscence of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}departed glory. And thus it remained until the 19th century. As early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as 1519 Raphael had indeed formed a plan for restoring the ancient city,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and especially the Forum, by means of extensive excavations; and during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his lifetime, and subsequently, particularly in 1546-47, the work was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun in the neighbourhood of the temple of Castor and Faustina. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}object, in view, however, being merely the discovery of monuments and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of art, the excavations were soon filled up again, and in the 17th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 18th centuries were entirely discontinued. At length, during the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}century, the plan was revived by the modern spirit of investigation. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1803 the arch of Severus, in 1813 the column of Phocas, and in 1816-19{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Clivus Capitolinus with its temples, were disinterred under the super¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intendence of Carlo Fea, while the French during their occupation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome appear to have directed their attention to more productive loca¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lities. In 1S35, and during the republic in 1848, part of the Basilica Julia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was excavated by Canina, but from that year down to 1871 the work was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discontinued. The Italian government resumed the excavations again{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with considerable energy; and by these last operations the Basilica, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lemples of Castor and Caesar, and a great part nf the Comitium and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the neighbouring streets have been brought to light, and an admirable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clue to the arrangements of the whole locality has thus been obtained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The excavations are carried on by Cav. Lanciani under the superintendence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Senator Fiorelli, but serious obstacles are presented to the work by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}growing requirements of modern business. It is hoped, however, that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}undertaking, which was planned and begun when the Renaissance was at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its zenith and has since been so frequently resumed, will ere long be finally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and satisfactorily completed, and that the most memorable spot in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}history of Europe will at length be fully brought to light and purged of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the unseemly accumulations of the rubbish heaped upon it by the neglect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of centuries.{% endraw %}
