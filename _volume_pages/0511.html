{% raw %}The road, especially the first part, traversing a dreary plain, is un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting. To the left lies the (4t/2M.) Lago di Giulianello, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extinct crater. A little farther is a wood , which used to be fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently infested by banditti. From (6'/2 M.) the poor village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giulianello the road to the left ascends to Rocca Massimo, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that to the right leads to Cori. The mountain slopes now become{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more attractive. About 3M. from Giulianello, at a chapel of the Ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donna del Monte, a road to the left diverges to the upper part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town. The road to the right, leading to the lower part, is preferable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cori (Filippuccio; the trattoria is near the Porta Romana, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bedrooms farther up in the Piazza, rustic accommodation, but civil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people). That no time may be lost, a guide to the chief points of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interest should at once be engaged (V2-I fr-)- The ancient Cora{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was at an early period a member of the Latin League; it is men¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tioned, B.C. 493, as one of the thirty confederate towns. During the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}empire it still prospered, but its name afterwards fell into oblivion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pop. 4000. Tobacco is extensively cultivated in the neighbourhood.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Besides the modern walls, which date chiefly from the 15th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerable remains of *Ancient Walls of various periods are pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served at Cori. Those of the earliest style consist of large blocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without mortar, the interstices being filled up with smaller stones;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the best example of this is seen near the gate to Norma and S. Maria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The second and more perfect kind is constructed of hewn polygonal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blocks, the outsides of which alone are left rough. Lastly there are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls of regularly hewn square stones, perhaps dating from the time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Sulla , such as those above S. Oliva, and those separating the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upper town (Arx) from the lower. The town seems to have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surrounded by differently situated walls at different periods.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A deep ravine outside the Porta Ninfesina is spanned by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very ancient *Ponte della Catena, constructed of tuffstone in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style of the Cloaca Maxima at Rome. In order that the great solidity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the arch with its double layers of masonry may be appreciated,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it should be surveyed from below.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Our attention, however, is chiefly arrested hy the colonnade of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the so-called *Temple of Hercules (perhaps of Minerva), standing on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the highest ground in the town. The cella of the temple is in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corporated with the church of S. Pietro; hut the eight columns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Doric colonnade, with frieze of travertine bearing traces of stucco-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decoration, are preserved. The inscription, recording the erection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the edifice by the duumviri, or chief magistrates of the place,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from the time of Sulla. Fine *View hence over the town to¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards the sea, and of the plain with the isolated M. Circeio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Oliva is also erected on the foundations of an ancient temple,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and possesses antique columns. In the street of S. Salvatore once{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stood a Temple of Castor and Pollux, as the inscription, still pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served, records, but it is now incorporated with other buildings.{% endraw %}
