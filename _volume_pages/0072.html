{% raw %}Toscanella (669 ft. above the sea; Inn at the Viterbo gate), the an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cient Tuscania, a small town with 3900 inhab. whose walls and towers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impart a mediaeval aspect tu the place, contains two fine old Romanesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structures: :::S. Pietro, on the height, with crypt and antique columns, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the exterior fine sculptures; and "S. Maria, smaller but even more inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting. Both churches are now disused. On the hill of S. Pietro stood the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient citadel. Etruscan tombs in the vicinity.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campanari's Garden, situated in the lower part of the town, em-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bellisheil with sarcophagi and other relics, and containing an imitation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an Etruscan tomb, is an interesting spot. The sarcophagi, with the life-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}size portraits of the deceased, framed in living green, are very impres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sive, and the traveller will nowhere acquire a more accurate idea of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contents of an Etruscan tomb. Sign. Carlo Campanari, who is obliging{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and well-informed , has with his father conducted many of those extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excavations which have filled the museums of Europe with Etruscan vases,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goblets, mirrors, and other interesting relics.{% endraw %}
