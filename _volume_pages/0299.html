{% raw %}The Capitol. ROME. IV. Ancient Rome. 213{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Central Approach, a gently ascending staircase paved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with asphalte ('la cordonnata), leads to the Piazza del Campidoglio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the foot of the steps are two handsome Egyptian Lions, and at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the top a group of the horse-taming Dioscuri (Castor and Pollux),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are said once to have adorned the theatre of Pompey. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left of the highest steps a she-wolf is kept in a cage in reminis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cence of the story of the foundation of Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The design of tlie present *Piazza del Campidoglio, or square{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Capitol (PL II, 20), is due to Michael Angelo, and its execu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion was begun in 1536 by Paul III. The palaces of the Conser¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vatori and Senators were already in existence, but their facades{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were altered. — At the sides of the Dioscuri, in front of the ba¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lustrade, are the so-called Trophies of Marius, from the water-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tower of that name of the Aqua Julia (p. 181), and the statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Emp. Constantine and his son Constans from the Therma-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Constantine on the Quirinal; on the right the first ancient mile¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone of the Via Appia (on the left a modern counterpart).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre of the piazza stands the admirable bronze ""Eques¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trian Statue of Marcus Aurelius (161-181), once gilded, and ori¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ginally placed in the forum near the arch of Sept. Severus. In 1 1ST{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it was erected near the Lateran, and, as the inscription records,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transferred hither in 1538. For its excellent state of preservation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it has been indebted to the popular belief that it was a statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Constantine, the first Christian emperor (see also p. 126).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond this monument rises the Palazzo del Senatore (PI. II,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20, 2) which was re-erected by Boniface IX. in 1389 on the site{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the ancient Tabularium, and provided with its handsome flight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of steps by Michael Angelo, under whose directions it is probable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that the facade was constructed by Giac. della Porta. The river-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods which adorn it are those of the (right) Tiber and (left){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile. In the centre a fountain, above which is a sitting statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Rome. The palace contains a spacious hall for the meetings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the senate, the offices of the civic administration, an obser¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vatory, and dwelling-apartments. The Clock-Tower was erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gregory XIII. in 1572 to replace an older one, probably belonging,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like the four corner-towers, one of which towards the Forum, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, is still recognisable, to the edifice of Boniface. The roof of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the clock-tower, which is embellished by a standing figure of Roma,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands an extensive *View. The ascent is somewhat fatiguing.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}obtainable at the municipal offices: Via del Campidoglio, first gate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left, on the 5th landing of the staircase turn to the left in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the passage, and enter the third door on the right. Visitors apply{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to one of the custodians. The permesso is also available lor the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saloons of the Conservatori. — On the ground-floor of the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house is the entrance to the Tabularium (p. 221).{% endraw %}
