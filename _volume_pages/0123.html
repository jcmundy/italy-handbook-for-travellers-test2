{% raw %}Perugia, the capital of the province of Umbria, with 16,700 in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hab. (including the villages 49,500), residence of the prefect, of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}military commandant, and a bishop, and the seat of a university,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies on a group of hills about 1300 ft. above the valley of the Tiber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1707 ft. above the level of the sea). The town is built in an an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiquated style, partly on the top of the hill, and partly on its slope.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Numerous buildings of the 14th and 15th cent, (when the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was in the zenith of its prosperity), the paintings of the Umbrian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school, and the fine and extensive views of the peculiar scenery,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}render Perugia one of the most interesting places in Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Perusia was one of the twelve Etruscan confederate cities, and not less{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient than Cortona, with which and Arretium it fell into the hands of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romans, B. C. 310. It subsequently became a municipium. In the war{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between Octavianus and Antony, who in the summer of 41 occupied Pe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rusia, and after an obstinate struggle was compelled by the former to sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}render (bellum Perusinum), the town suffered severely, and was finally re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duced to ashes. It was afterwards rebuilt and became a Roman colony{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the name of Augusta Perusia. In the 6th cent, it was destroyed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Goth Totila after a siege of seven years. In the wars of the Lombards,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Guclphs, and Ghibellines it also suffered greatly; in the 14th cent, it acquired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the supremacy over nearly the whole of Umbria, but in 1370 was compelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to surrender to the pope. Renewed struggles followed, owing to the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flicts between the powerful families of Oddi and Baglioni. In 1416 the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shrewd and courageous Braccio Fortebraccio of Montone usurped the su¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preme power, whence new contests arose, until at length Giovanni Paolo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baglioni surrendered to Pope Julius II. Leo X. caused him to be executed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Rome in 1520. In 1540 Paul HI. erected the citadel, 'ad coercendam{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Perusinorum audaciam', as the inscription, destroyed during the last revo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lution, recorded. In 170S the town was captured by the Duke of Savoy, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}31st May 1849 by the Austrians, and in 1860 by the Picdinontese.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Umbrian School of Painting. As early as the time of Dante an Um¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brian artist, the miniature painter Oderisi of Gnbbio, was celebrated,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and art was practised in Oubbio, Fabriano, Perugia, etc. The neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bouring Siena doubtless exercised an influence on the prevailing style{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of art, which was confirmed by the situation of the towns, the character{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of their inhabitants, and the religious atmosphere diffused by Assisi and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Loreto. Neither dramatic power, nor wealth of imagination is to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found in the Umbrian style, its characteristic features being reverie,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tranquillity, and gentleness of sentiment. The men pourtrayed often ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pear destitute of individuality and vigour, the female figures, on the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hand, excite our admiration owing to their winning and devout expres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sions. Technical improvements seem to have been introduced but slowly,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the old style was thoroughly cultivated ;.nd rendered more attractive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by frequent use of decorative adjuncts.{% endraw %}
