{% raw %}The Frescoes date from different periods, extending over seven centu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ries. We begin with the Vestibule. Immediately to the left by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase is a female head with a halo, believed by De Rossi to date from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 5th cent. — Farther on, under the first arch on the left, -Christ bless¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing in the Greek mode, with first, middle, and little finger extended,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the archangels Michael and Gabriel and SS. Andrew (1.) and Cle¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment (r.). Before him kneel SS. Cyril and Methodius (9th or 10th cent.). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures in this, as well as in the following scenes, have their names attached.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Opposite (on the right), a Mother finds at the altar of St. Clement her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}child who had been swallowed up by the sea and thrown on shore a year{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later. Under it the family of the donor grouped round the medallion portrait{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Clement. To the right is the dedication: Ego Beno de Rapiza pro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}omore dei et beati Clementis pingere feci (11th cent.). — On the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther on, the Transference of the relics of St. Cyril from the Vatican{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to S. Clemente in the reign of Pope Nicholas, with the dedication: Ego{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maria Macellaria pro timore Dei et remedio anime mee haec pingere feci.—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the end of the vestibule on the right is the entrance to the Left Aisle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Over the door of the latter are three badly preserved frescoes, of which that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the centre appears to represent the resuscitation of a child. Two only of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the frescoes at the end of this aisle are distinguishable : on the posterior wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the corner, St. Cyril before the Emp. Michael; on the lateral wall, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Youth baptised by St. Methodius (10th cent.). — The Nave is now entered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the arch in the right wall. Here, immediately to the left, is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}-fresco in three sections, one above the other. Half of the uppermost,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Enthronement of St. Clement, is destroyed. That in the centre re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents St. Clement celebrating mass; on the right Theodora converted to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christianity and her husband Sisinius struck with blindness; the smaller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ligures on the left are those of the donor Beno ]and his wife. Below it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also the dedicatory inscription : Ego Beno de Rapiza cum Maria uxore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mea, etc. The lowest represents Sisinius causing a column to be bound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}instead of St. Clement (11th cent.). The lateral surfaces of this pillar are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also adorned with frescoes (1. St. Antony, Daniel in the lions' den ; r. St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egidius, St. Blasius), but the adjoining wall prevents them from being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen. Farther on towards the vestibule, on the same wall, is another and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}larger 'fresco in three sections. The highest, now half obliterated, repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sents Christ between Michael and St. Clement (1.), and Gabriel and Nicholas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(r.). In the centre are three scenes from the life of St. Alexius, placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one above the other as is the case with scenes on Roman sarcophagi:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}o. Alessius returns unrecognised to Rome as a hermit; 6. Pope Boniface{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1. blesses the dying man; c. The betrothed of the dead man recognises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his corpse. The lowest of the three frescoes is of a decorative character{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with flowers and birds. — At the end of this wall are three scenes from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the life of Christ. Next to them, on the wall of the vestibule, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, the Crucifixion, on the left, the Assumption. Over the latter, Christ{% endraw %}
{% raw %}borne by four angels ; at the corners St. Vitus (r.) and Leo IV. (1.) with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the inscription S. Dom. Leo IV. P. P. Ro., and the square nimbus with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which living persons were usually represented (9th cent.). — The frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the external wall of the Right Aisle are almost obliterated. A niche here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a group of Mary with Jesus. On the arch above, Christ (beard¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}less), with figures of angels and saints on each side.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Below the apse are the remains of the Structure of the Imperial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Era (No. II. and marked black, in the plan), built of brick. The first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the three adjoining chambers is adorned with stucco enrichments. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}next is an ante-chamber to a Chapel of Mithras, in which the statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Good Shepherd (!) was found. These chambers are damp and partly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}filled with water. The staircase descending to them is at the end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right aisle.{% endraw %}
