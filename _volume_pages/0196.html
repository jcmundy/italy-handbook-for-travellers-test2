{% raw %}114 Prelim. Information. ROME. Church Festivals.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Church-Festivals. Since the annexation of Rome to the kingdom of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy on 20th Sept. 1S70 the Pope has not quitted the Vatican, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great ecclesiastical festivals have consequently lost most of their former{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attractions. The public ceremonies at which the Pope formerly officiated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in person, such as those of the Holy Week, the benedictions, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public processions including that of the Fete de Dieu, have been discon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tinued. The Pope still officiates on high festivals in the Sistine Chapel,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but visitors are not admitted without an introduction from very high{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarters. The illumination of St. Peter's and the Girandola, or fireworks,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with which the festivals of Easter and St. Peter and St. Paul used to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated, have also been discontinued.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following enumeration of the various festivals, as they were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated prior to 20th Sept. 1870, will still be found useful in many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}respects. Details are contained in the Gerarckia Catlolica , and the Diario{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Roma, published annually. The best work on the ceremonies of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Holy Week and their signification is the Manuale delle cerimonie che hanno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luogo nella seliimana sanla e neW ottava di pasqua al Vaticano (1 fr., also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a French edition), obtainable at the bookshops mentioned at p. 109. Ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mission to the Sistine Chapel, as well as to St. Peter's, on great occasions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(to the reserved part), is accorded only to gentlemen in uniform or evening-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dress, to ladies in black dresses, and black veils or black caps. Gentlemen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stand; seats are reserved for ladies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Pope used to officiate in person three times annually, on Christinas-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day, Easter-day, and the Festival of St. Peter and St. Paul (29th June); and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}four times annually he imparted his benediction, on Holy Thursday and Master-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day from the balcony of St. Peter's, on Ascension-day from the Lateran,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and on 15th Aug., the anniversary of the 'Assumption of the Virgin', from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria Maggiore. The most imposing ceremonies were those of the Holy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Week, from Palm Sunday to Easter-day, the most important of which took{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place in the Sistine Chapel, accompanied by the music ('lamentations', etc.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Palestrina and other old masters, on which occasions the papal band{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(cappella papale) performed.{% endraw %}
