{% raw %}The Interior, which is lighted by a single aperture in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre of the dome, produces so beautiful an effect that it was cur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rently believed at an early period that the temple derived its name{% endraw %}

{% raw %}resemblance to the vault of heaven. The height and diameter of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dome are equal, being each 140 ft. The surface of the walls is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}broken by seven large niches, in which stood the statues of the gods,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among which those of Mars, Venus, and Caesar are ascertained to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been placed. The architrave is borne by fluted columns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}giallo antico or pavonazzetto in couples, the shafts being 26 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height. Above the latter, and corresponding with the niches, for¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merly rose a series of round arches, borne by Caryatides, but they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appear to have been removed on the occasion of a very early re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}storation of the edifice. The coffered ceiling of the vault, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consists of concrete, was decorated with gold-leaf, and the whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roof was covered with gilded bronze tiles, which the Emp. Con-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stans II. caused to be removed to Constantinople in 655. Under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gregory III. these tiles were replaced by lead. The building has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently been restored, as, for example, by Domitian. Trajan,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Septimius Severus, and Caracalla. The names of the last two are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recorded by an inscription on the architrave of the portico.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 609 the Pantheon was consecrated by Pope Boniface IV. as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Christian church, under the name of S. Maria ad Martyres (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 333), and in commemoration of the event the festival of All{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Saints was instituted. It was originally celebrated on 13th May,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but afterwards on 1st Nov. A palace, a cathedral-chapter, and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cardinals title were afterwards attached to the church, which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally known as S. Maria Rotonda, or La Rotonda. Under LTr-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ban VIII. (Barberini) the two campanili were erected by Bernini, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'asses' ears' of the architect as they have been derisively named.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The same pope removed from the portico the brazen tubes on which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the roof rested, and caused them to be converted into columns for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the canopy of the high-altar of St. Peter's, and cannons for the{% endraw %}

{% raw %}complaint of Pasquin , 'Quod non fecerunt barbari, fecerunt Barbe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rini'. Pius IX. caused the church to be judiciously restored.{% endraw %}
