{% raw %}The Villa Medici (PI. I, 18), erected in 1540 by Annibale Lippi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for Cardinal Ricci da Montepulciano, came into possession of Car¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dinal Alessandro de' Medici about 1600, and subsequently into that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the grand-dukes of Tuscany. In 1801 the French transferred{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thither the seat of their academy of art, founded by Louis XIV. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance to the garden, to which visitors are readily admitted, is by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the gate to the left, or by the staircase to the right in the house{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(5-6 soldi).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ancient reliefs have been built into the walls of the tastefully deco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rated facade of the villa next to the garden. The right wing contains a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Collection of Casts (open daily, except. Saturdays, 8-12, and in the afler-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iidiiii till near sunset), comprising many from statues, etc., not pieserved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Rome, e. g. from the Parthenon of Athens, and the museum of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Louvre, which are valuable in the history of art. Adjoining the wing is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a terrace, the front-wall of which is adorned with casts. We then enter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a side-door, opposite the museum of casts, which if closed will be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opened by the porter (25 c.), and ascending, traverse the oak-grove in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}straight direction to the right. We next ascend 60 steps to the 'Belvedere,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence a charming panorama is enjoyed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. the shady pleasure grounds of the Villa Medici are bounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Pincio. Most of the statues with which they arc embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are modern.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The street passing the front of the Academy ends in the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Trinita, where to the left rises the church of SS. Trinita{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de' Monti. The Obelisk in front of it, a conspicuous object from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many points, is an ancient imitation of that in the Piazza del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Popolo, and once adorned the gardens of Sallust.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}SS. Trinita de' Monti (PI. I, 20), erected by Charles VIII. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}France in 1495, and plundered during the French Revolution, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored by Magri in 1816 by order of Louis XVIII. The church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is open on Sundays only before 9 a, m., and in the evening during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vespers (1 hr. before Ave Maria), when the nuns, for whom Men¬{% endraw %}
