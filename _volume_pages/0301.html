{% raw %}We first traverse two Rooms with modern lists of Roman magistrates,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and then enter a long Cokhidok in which the so-called Protomoteca,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded by Pius VII., has recently been established. This is a collection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of busts of celebrated Italians, especially those who have distinguished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}themselves in art and science, including poets (such as Dante, Petrarch,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Ariosto), scholars, painters, architects, and sculptors. At the end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the corridor is a monument to Canova. Several eminent foreigners have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also been admitted: (1.) Winckelmann, (r.) Poussin and Raphael Mengs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— The last door but two in the corridor leads to the picture gallery (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below), and the last but one leads up two steps to the terracottas and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronzes which are connected with the newly arranged part of the Capito¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line collections. The principal entrance to the latter is on the opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the corridor (comp. Plan).{% endraw %}
