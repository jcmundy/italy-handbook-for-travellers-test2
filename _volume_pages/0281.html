{% raw %}* Piazza Navona (PL II, 13), the largest in Rome after that of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Peter, where, as its form still indicates, the Circus, or Sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dium of Domitian, was formerly situated. The name is derived{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the agones (corrupted to Navone, Navona), or contests which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}took place here. The piazza has recently been officially named{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Circo Agonale.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is embellished with three Fountains. That on the N. side,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}placed here in 1878, represents Neptune in conflict with a sea-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monster (polypus); round the central group are Nereids and sea¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horses. Not far from it, in the centre of a large ancient basin ot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pentelic marble, rises the large fountain erected by Bernini under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Innocent X.; at the corners of the lofty mass of rock, the different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parts of which represent the four quarters of the globe, are placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the gods of the four largest rivers, the Danube, Ganges, Nile, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rio della Plata, executed by pupils of Bernini. The whole is sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mounted by an obelisk, which was formerly in the Circus of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maxentius, and was originally erected in honour of Domitian. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The third fountain, at the S. end of the piazza , is adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}masks, Tritons, and the statue of a Moor by Bernini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The piazza was used from 1447 to 1871 as a vegetable market,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which after the Italian occupation was transferred to the Campo de'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fiori (p. 204). The singular custom formerly prevailed of laying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this piazza under water for the amusement of the people every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}August, by preventing the escape of the water from the fountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. side of the Piazza Navona stands the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Agnese, the interior of which, in the form of a Greek cross,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the campanili, are by C. Rinaldi. The facade is by Borromini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Romans used to maintain that the Nile on the great fountain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veiled his head in order to avoid being distressed by the tasteless{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appearance of the church-tower.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Over the principal door is the monument of Innocent X. by Maini;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left, in the chapel of the transept, is a statue of St. Sebastian, adapted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from an ancient statue by Maini. Beneath the dome are eight columns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vognatello'. The old church was situated in the side-vaults of the Circus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the saint suffered martyrdom. Two chapels with ancient vaulting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still remain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left of the church is the Palazzo Pamfili (PL I, 13, 18),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also erected by Rinaldi, now the property of Prince Doria. Oppo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}site to it is the dilapidated national church of the Spaniards, S. Gia-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}como degli Spagnuoli (PL II, 13, 23), erected in 1450. The entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is in the Via della Sapienza.{% endraw %}
