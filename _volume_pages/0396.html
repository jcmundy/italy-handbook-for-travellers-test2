{% raw %}The Vatican.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This, the most extensive palace in the world, was originally a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dwelling-house for the popes, erected by Symmachus near the an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terior court of the old church of St. Peter, and afterwards gradually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extended. Charlemagne, when in Rome, is believed to have re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sided here. This building having fallen todecay during the tumults{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the following centuries, Eugene III. erected a palace near St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peter's, which was greatly enlarged by Nicholas III. The Vatican{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did not, however, become the usual residence of the popes until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after their return from Avignon, when the Lateran was deserted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the death of Gregory XL the first conclave was held in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vatican in 1378, which resulted in the schism. In 1410 John XXIII.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed the covered passage to the castle of S. Angelo. In 1450{% endraw %}

{% raw %}offices and residences of the cardinals. The small portion completed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by him , afterwards occupied by Alexander VI. and named Tor di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Borgia, was extended by subsequent popes. In 1473 the Sistine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chapel was erected by Sixtus IV., and about 1490 the Belvedere, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garden-house, by Innocent VIII. Bramante, under Julius II.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}united the latter with the palace by means of a great court, which{% endraw %}
