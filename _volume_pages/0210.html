{% raw %}sides numerous chapels, and among them arose the five Patriarchal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Churches, presided over by the pope, and forming a community to{% endraw %}

{% raw %}sidered to belong. These five were <S. Giovanni in Laterano, S.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}5. Sebastiano, erected over the catacombs of the Via Appia, enjoyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}special veneration. These formed the 'Seven Churches of Rome' to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which pilgrims flocked from every part of western Christendom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The number of monasteries now steadily increased, and at the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time the inroads of poverty made rapid strides.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the 4th Century the cultivation of the Roman Campagna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}began to be seriously neglected, and in an official document of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year 395 it is stated that upwards of 500 square miles of arable land{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had been abandoned and converted into morass. The malaria at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same time extended its baneful sway from the coast into the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terior of the country. The storms of the barbarian irruptions greatly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aggravated the misery. Although the Vandals and Goths are often{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erroneously held responsible for the destruction of all the great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monuments of antiquity, which , on the contrary, Theodoric tlie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great did his utmost to protect, Rome doubtless suffered terribly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from having been the scene of their battles and pillagings. In 410{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the city was plundered by Alaric, and in 445 by the Vandals, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 537 it sustained its first siege from the Goths under Vitiges.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They laid waste the Campagna and cut off all the supplies of water{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought to the city by the aqueducts, but the skill of Belisarius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the strength of the walls, particularly those of the Castle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Angelo, effectually repelled their attacks on the city. In March{% endraw %}
{% raw %}538 they were at length compelled to abandon their designs, after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having beleaguered the city for upwards of a year. In December{% endraw %}
{% raw %}546, Totilas, the king of the Goths, entered Rome, and is said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have found not more than 500 persons within the walls of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}devastated city. Belisarius then repaired the walls which had been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}partially destroyed, and in 547 he sustained a second siege. In 549{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the city again fell into the hands of Totilas, but in 552 it was re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}captured by Narses and again united with the Byzantine empire.{% endraw %}
