{% raw %}Leaving the Piazza Capo di Ferro, and continuing our walk in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same direction, we reach the small Piazza de' Pellegrini. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left is the back of the Pal. Santacroce, now a Monte di Pietu{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL II, 14), or money-lending establishment, founded in 1539, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}established here in 1604 (some of the numerous pictures pledged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here are of great value). On the right is the church of S. Trinita{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de' Pellegrini, erected in 1614, with a high-altar adorned with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture of the Trinity, by Guido Reni. The neighbouring hospital{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is destined for the accommodation of convalescents and pilgrims. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains 488 beds, and can provide dinner for 944 persons at one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time. Italian pilgrims are entertained here at Easter for three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days, and foreigners for four. — In the vicinity is the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria in Monticelli (PL II, 14), which was consecrated by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paschalis II. in 1101. It has been restored several times, so that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tlie campanile and remains of mosaics in the tribune are now the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only relics of the period of its foundation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via de' Pettinari (PL II, 14) leads from the Piazza de'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pellegrini to the Ponte Sisto. At the end of the street, on the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the small church of S. Salvatore inOnda, re-erected in 1684, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left the Fontanone di Ponte Sisto, constructed by Giov. Fon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tana under Paul V. — The Ponte Sisto, see p. 324.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In a straight direction from the fountain, towards the N.W.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and near the river, runs the Via del Fontanone, prolonged by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Giulia (PL II, 14. 10), which was built by Julius II., leading{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 12 min. to the Ponte S. Angelo. To the left in the latter street,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite the garden of the Pal. Farnese, stands the small church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria della Morte, or dell' Orazione (PL II, 11), erected by Fuga{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about the middle of last century, and belonging to a burial society.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Then to the left, the Pal. Falconieri, built by Borromini, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once contained the picture-gallery of Card. Fesch. On the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side, farther on, the Carceri Nuovi, a prison founded by Innocent X.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then No. 66, the Pal. Sacchetti (PL II, 10), originally erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antonio da San Gallo as his private residence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the end of the street, on the left, is S. Giovanni de' Fioren¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tini (PL II, 10), the handsome national church of the Florentines.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The building was begun, by the desire of Leo X., from a design by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sansovino, which he preferred to the competing plans of Raphael,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sangallo, Peruzzi, others; and the laborious task of completing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}substructions on the bank of the river was executed by Sangallo.{% endraw %}
