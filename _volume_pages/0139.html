{% raw %}About 5 M. to the S.E. of Borghetto (carriages at the station) lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Civita Castellana, picturesquely situated 502 ft. above the sea (Posta,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Speranza, in the market-place). This was the site of Falerii, the town of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Falisci, which was captured by Camillus in B.C. 396. A lofty bridge,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1712, and recently restored after having been damaged by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an earthquake, carries the road into the town across a ravine, 120 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}depth. The Cathedral of S. Maria dates from 1210. The Citadel, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Alexander VI. in 1500 from a design by Sangallo, and enlarged by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Julius II. and Leo X., was last employed as a state-prison. Civita Castel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lana contains nothing to interest the traveller except its picturesque situa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion. The deep ravines by which it is enclosed testify to vast volcanic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}convulsions. They contain a few fragments of ancient walls and numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etruscan tombs hewn in the rock, especially near the citadel.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interesting excursion to the ruins of Falerii (pronounced Falleri), 3 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distant. Near the citadel the Ponte del Terreno is crossed to the left, where tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}honeycomb the rocks on all sides, this being the more direct route to Fa-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lerium Novum or Colonia Junonia, founded by the Romans about 240, situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the plain, 3 M. to the N. of Civita Castellana. Etruscan and Roman tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are here seen side by side. The town was nearly in the form of a triangle,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}l'/2 M. in circumference; the well preserved walls are protected by strong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}square towers and penetrated by gates, one of which on the W. (Porta di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giove) is still in good condition. Another gate towards the S. E., the Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Bove, is also worthy of a visit; near it is the theatre of Roman construction,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the piscina and what is regarded as the forum, at the back of the theatre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the Porta di Giove, within the walls, is the *Abbadia di S. Maria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 12th cent. In the nave, antique columns; in 1829 the roof fell in,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the damage has been repaired. The adjoining building contains in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions, statues , etc. , the result of excavations made here. An amphi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theatre has also been recently discovered.{% endraw %}
