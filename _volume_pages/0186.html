{% raw %}104 Route li. LA MUCCIA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another voad leads from S. Severino to the N. by Matelica (11 M.) to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fabriano (25 M.), see p. 102.]{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Roman road leads from Tolentino on the left bank of the Chienti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through a pleasant district and numerous plantations of oaks, to Belforte,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the post-stations Valcimara and Ponte della Trove, and (18'/2 M. from To¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lentino) —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}47 M. La Muccia (Leone), the usual halting-place of the vetturini. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountain slopes are studded with small villages on both sides. At Gelagno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road begins to ascend, the district becomes barren and bleak (the vet¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turini here procure the aid of oxen). The passage of the Apennines from La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Muccia to Foligno occupies about 6 hrs. by carriage. Serravalle lies in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}narrow ravine; above it rise the ruins of an old castle. I1/2 M. farther are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sources of the Chienti (p. 103). The road now ascends to the grassy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}table-land of Colfiorito (Locanda di Konelli), 2903 ft. above the sea-level,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirts a small lake, traverses a grove of oaks, and descends somewhat abruptly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Case Nuove and Pate to Foligno. Above Pale towers the lofty Sasso di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pale, one of the last spurs of the Apennines. In descending, the road affords{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a beautiful "view of Foligno and the charming valley of the Clitunno. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road follows the course of the hronk, and '/'^ M. from Foligno reaches the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Flaminia (p. 93).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}75 M. Foligno, see p. 75.{% endraw %}
