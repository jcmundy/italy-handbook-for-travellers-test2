{% raw %}THIRD SECTION.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ENVIRONS OF ROME,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The vast Campagna di Roma, bounded on the N. by the Ciminian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Forest, on the W. by the sea, and on the E. by the Apennine chain of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Saliina, affords an ample field for a number of the most interesting ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cursions. The mountains with their picturesque outlines, and the wild{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and deserted plain, covered in every direction with imposing ruins, chiefly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of ancient origin, present attractions of the highest order, to which a whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year of study might fitly be devoted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Campagna, which was once covered by the sea, owes its origin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to powerful volcanic agency; lava and peperine are of frequent occurrence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the red volcanic tufa is seen everywhere. A great number of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}craters may be distinguished, the most important of which are the lakes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Alban Mts., the lake of Bracciano, the lake of Vico in the Ciminian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Forest, and the crater of Baccano. The historical associations connected with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this plain are, however, of still higher interest than its natural features.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The narrow strip of land which stretches between the Alban Mts. and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tiber towards the sea is the ancient Latium , which victoriously asserted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its superiority over the Etruscans on the N., the Sabines on the E., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Volscians on the S., subsequently elfected the union of the Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peninsula, and finally acquired supremacy over the whole world. Once a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}densely peopled land , with numerous and prosperous towns, it is now a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vast and dreary waste, of which barely one-tenth part is furrowed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ploughshare. In May, when the malaria begins to prevail, herdsmen and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cattle retire to the mountains, while the few individuals who are compelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to remain behind are rendered miserable by continual attacks of fever. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause of this change dates from so remote a period as the last centuries of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}republic, when the independent agricultural population was gradually dis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}placed by proprietors of large estates and pastures. This system inevitably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entailed the ruin of the country, for a dense population and high degree of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}culture alone can avert the malaria, which is produced by defective drainage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the evaporation of stagnant water in the undulating and furrowed volcanic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soil. In the middle ages the evil increased. The papal government has repeat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edly endeavoured to promote the revival of agriculture, but such attempts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannot, be otherwise than abortive as long as the land is occupied by farms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pastures on a large scale. An entire revolution in the present system,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}energetically and comprehensively carried out, will alone avail to restore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the prosperity of the land. The present government also has appointed a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commission to take steps for rendering the Campagna cultivable, but as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yet without any practical result. About one-half of the Agro Romano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belongs to ecclesiastical corporations, one-third to the nobility, and barely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the remaining one-sixth to small proprietors. These large estates are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usually let to Mercanti di Campagna, or contractors on a large scale,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of whom there are not more than forty altogether , on leases of three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}years by the ecclesiastical bodies, and of nine years or more by the lay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proprietors. These contractors entrust the management of the land to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fattore, or bailiff, who resides at the Tenuta or Casale, as the farm-house{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is called. The system of tillage and the agricultural implements used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are of a very primitive character.{% endraw %}
