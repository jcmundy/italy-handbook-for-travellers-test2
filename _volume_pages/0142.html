{% raw %}Est, Est, Est. Propter nimium est,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The traveller should not omit to ascend into the town for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sake of the magnificent view: N. the lake of Bolsena as far as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chain of M. Amiata, E. the Umbrian Apennines, S. as far{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the Ciminian Forest, W. as far as the sea. The extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plain of ancient Etruria with its numerous villages may be sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veyed from this point; and it has therefore been reasonably con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jectured that the celebrated Fanum Voltumnae, the most sacred{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shrine of the Etruscans, once stood here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Montefiascone to Viterbo the road traverses the somewhat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bleak and unattractive plain between the Ciminian Forest and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lake of Bolsena (p. 64). Midway, near the Osteria della Fonta-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nella, part of the ancient Via Cassia lies to the right. About 2lfe M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther, to the left of the road, are situated the ruins of Ferento,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Etruscan Ferentinum, birthplace of the Emperor Otho. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11th cent, it was destroyed by the inhabitants of Viterbo on account{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its heretical tendencies, for the Ferentines represented the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Saviour on the cross with open eyes, instead of closed, as was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thought more orthodox. Such at least is the account of the chro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niclers. Among the extensive mediaeval, Roman, and Etruscan{% endraw %}
