{% raw %}Chiesa Nuova (PL II, 10), or S. Maria in Vallicella, erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Filippo Neri for the order of Oratorians founded by him, and{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Interior, which is dark and unfavourable for pictures, is richly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorated. The admirable stucco-work is by Cos. F'aniello, and Ercole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ferrata. The ceiling of the Nave, the dome, and the tribune are painted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Pietro da Cortona. — On the right, 1st Chapel, Crucifixion, Scip. di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gaetano; 3rd Chapel, dell' Ascensione, altar-piece by Muziano. — On the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd Chapel, Adoration of the Magi, Ces. Nebbia; 3rd Chapel, Nativity,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Durante Alberti; 4th Chapel, Visit of Elizabeth, Baroccio. — Left Transept:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Presentation in the Temple, Baroccio; Peter and Paul, statues in marble,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Valsoldo. Here also, adjoining the tribune, is the small and sumptuous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel of S. Filippo Neri, beneath the altar of which his remains repose.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above is the portrait of the saint in mosaic, after the original of Guido{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reni preserved in the adjoining monastery. — Over the High Altar, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its four columns of porta santa, a Madonna by Rubens; on the right "SS. Gre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gory, Maurus, and Papia, on the left "SS. Nereus and Achilleus, also by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rubens, who painted these pictures during his second stay in Rome in 1606{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for this church, which was then the most fashionable in the city. — Right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Transept. On the right, Coronation of Mary, Cav. d' Arpino; SS. John{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Baptist and John the Evangelist, statues in marble by Flaminio Vacca.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sacristy (entered from the left transept) was constructed by Marru-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celti. On the vaulting: Angel with instruments of torture, by Pietro da Cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tona. Colossal statue of the saint by Algardi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On 26th May-, the festival of the saint, and after Ave Maria every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sunday from 1st Nov. to Palm Sunday, concerts of sacred music, to which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}men only are admitted, arc given in the adjoining Oratorium, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derives its name from the oratories fitted up by S. Filippo Xeri. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saint was fond of music and advocated a cheerful form of divine service.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The adjoining Monastery, erected by Borromini, is of irregular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shape, but is remarkable for the massiveness of its construction. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a room once occupied by the saint, with various relics.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Corte di Appello, the Tribunate Civile e Correzionale, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tribunale di Commercio have recently been established in this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building. — The valuable Library founded by S. Filippo Neri, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gradually enriched by rare MSS., is open to the public on Wednes¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days, Thursdays, and Saturdays, 9-1 o'clock.{% endraw %}
