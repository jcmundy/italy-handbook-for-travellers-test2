{% raw %}Near the point where the Via S. Gregorio unites with the Via di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porta S. Sebastiano (PL III, 24, 26), was anciently situated the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porta Capena, or Capuan Gate, whence the Via Appia issued. We{% endraw %}
{% raw %}follow the Via di Porta S. Sebastiano to the left.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After 5 min., at the end of the avenue which runs parallel with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street on the right, a road ascends on the right to the church of S. Balbina{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL III, 23), situated on the slope of the Aventine, perhaps on the site{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of an ancient temple, and consecrated by Gregory the Great. The roof{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is still open, but the church is modernised and destitute of ornament.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It contains a relief (Crucifixion) by Mino da Fiesole and a monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Johannes Cosmas. (Visitors ring at the gate on the right of the church.){% endraw %}

{% raw %}tower commands ajfine '"view. — On the way back to the road we also{% endraw %}
