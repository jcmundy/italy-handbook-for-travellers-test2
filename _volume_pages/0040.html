{% raw %}The sculptors of Pergamum celebrated the victories of their kings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the Celts. The statue of the Dying Gaul (the so called 'dying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gladiator') in the museum of the Capitol, and the Group of Gauls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Villa Ludovisi are most impressive examples of the manner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which they were inspired by the theme. The northern barbarian.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}differing widely as he did in configuration, costume, and habit from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Greek, was a study of engrossing interest to the sculptor, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was reproduced with physiological accuracy. At the same time,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that the fame of the victor might be magnified to the utmost, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptor sought to embody all that was admirable in the character{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the vanquished: his ill-trained but chivalrous valour, his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inflexible determination to die rather than suffer disgrace. So late{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the 4th century A.D. there was still to be seen on the Acropolis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a votive offering of King Attalus in the shape of a group with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous figures representing the struggles of the gods with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}giants, of the Athenians with the Amazons, of the Athenians with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Persians, and Attalus himself with the Celts. Quite recently,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures have been recognised as belonging to these groups in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collections of Venice, Rome, and Naples. — Of the Rhodian School{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we have examples in the so-called Farnese Bull in the museum of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Naples, and in the Laocoon. The date of the Laocoon has not been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}established. Since the days of Winckelmann, who assigned it to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the time of Alexander, and of Lessing, who maintained that it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonged rather to that of Titus, there has been a constantly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recurring controversy on the subject. It is, however, highly im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probable that the Rhodian School retained, as late as the reign of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Titus, the vitality necessary for the production of so considerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a work.{% endraw %}
