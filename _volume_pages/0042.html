{% raw %}The noblest, happily also the best preserved building of old Rome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is unquestionably the Pantheon of Agrippa, built in the time of Au¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gustus. In spite of much disfigurement received in later times, this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vast edifice, comprised in its interior within the precincts of one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}majestic circle, and obtaining the light of heaven from the centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its wondrous dome, continues to impress the beholder with un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}failing, overwhelming effect. Therefore it is that the Pantheon, hav¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing survived the period of art's extinction and revival, better re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents the solidity, the daring, and the splendour of Roman archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tecture than the stupendous remains of palace, bath, and circus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An important innovation which Plastic Art experiences in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter days of the Roman republic remains to be mentioned. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}introduction of Eclecticism may be attributed toPAsiTELES, a native{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Southern Italy. It would appear that he had striven, by way of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}protest against the unrest and violence apparent in the works of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his predecessors of the Rhodian School, to restore to art something{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of her primitive simplicity, and to combine the excellences of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}older schools while he avoided their errors. His aim was to revert{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the stern simplicity and thoroughness of the earliest sculptors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the same time he studied nature independently and carefully;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while he was not neglectful of the minor graces of his art. Ste-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phanus was his pupil, whose pupil again was Menelaus, from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose chisel we have a group now in the Villa Ludovisi, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monly known as Electro and Orestes. Though the real intention{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the author will probably never be known, this work serves to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teach us how earnest was the endeavour, and how happy the result of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}art study in the school ofPasiteles. It is not, however, given to eclectic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schools to exercise a powerful or enduring influence. Accordingly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we find a comparatively small number of works belonging to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school of Pasiteles.{% endraw %}
