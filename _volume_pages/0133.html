{% raw %}8. From Florence by (Arezzo) Terontola and Chiusi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}196 M. Railway. This is the most direct route from Florence to Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Express in 8 hrs., fares 38 fr. 5, 26 fr. 40c; ordinary train in ll1/* hrs.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fares 34 fr. 511, 23 fr. 70, 16 fr. 55 c; no change of carriages. — Tlie re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cently completed line from Terontola to Chiusi now' connects the two rail¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ways of E. Tuscany, and greatly facilitates a visit to the interesting towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of this district.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Florence to Terontola, 76 M., see pp. 38-15. The main{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line to Rome diverges to the right (8.) from the branch line to Pe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rugia, Assisi, and Foligno, and at first skirts the Trasimene Lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. 45).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}82 M. Castiglione del Lago, with 10,600 inhab. (incl. suburbs).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lying to the left on a promontory extending into the lake, possesses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an obi palazzo of the Duchi della Cornia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right rise the hills which separate the valley of the Chiana{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}87 M. Panicale , a small place with unimportant frescoes in its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churches by Perugino and his school. The line takes a W. direction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and joins the line from Siena in the valley of the Chiana (R. 4).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}94 M. Chiusi. — Carriage with.one horse to, the town, lying to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right on a hill (>,., hr.), 1 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lion d'Oro, a mediocre inn, hardly to be recommended to ladies,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bargaining necessary. — * Trattoria Giul. Giometti, Via Porsenna, the land¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lord of which also provides rooms. The landlord of the Lion d'Oro keeps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a collection of Etruscan antiquities for sale at exorbitant prices. Tra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vellers are cautioeed against making purchases of the kind anywhere in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chiusi, as 'antiquities' from Etruscan tombs are largely manufactured here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A ticket of admission (1 fr.) to the Etruscan Tombs (see below) is ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained at the Municipio; the keys are kept by the custodian, who should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be consulted as to the time required (3-4 fr. per day). The services of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}any other attendant should be declined.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chiusi, the ancient Clusium, one of the twelve Etruscan capi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tals, frequently mentioned in the wars against Rome, and as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headquarters of Porsenna, was fearfully devastated by malaria in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the middle ages but under the auspices of the grand-dukes of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}House of Lorraine the Val di Chiana was gradually drained, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town recovered from these disasters. It now numbers 4600{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab. The walls are mediteval; a few relics of those of the Etruscan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period may be distinguished near the cathedral, outside the Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delle Torri. A walk thence round the town to the Porta Romana{% endraw %}
{% raw %}affords pleasing views of the S. portion of the Chiana Valley, Cittk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Pieve, the mountains of Cetona, to the N. the lakes of Chiusi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Montepulciano, and the latter town itself.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the town extends a labyrinth of subterranean passages,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the precise object of which is unknown (inaccessible); they are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}believed to have belonged to an elaborate system of drainage, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient Etruscans are known to have excelled in works of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kind, and were indeed, in this respect, far in advance of many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern nations.{% endraw %}
