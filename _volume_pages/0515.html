{% raw %}Bracciano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %},4 M. from Rome. A Vetturino conveys passengers to Bracciano every al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ternate day, but not always regularly, from the Piazza of the Pantheon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 5-6 hrs., fare 4 fr., returning on the following day. In May and .lune,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bathing-season at Vicarello (see below), the traffic is brisker.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond the Osteria Nuova (see above) the Arrone is soon reached.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A road to the right then diverges to Anguillara (p. 380), situated on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lake. The country continues dreary. About 3M. before Bracciano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is reached, the Lake of Bracciano becomes visible, with Trevignano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Rocca Romana, the highest point (2018 ft.) of the surrounding{% endraw %}
{% raw %}range of hills. The lake, the Lacus Sabatinus of antiquity, is 20 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in circumference , and lies nearly 500 ft. above the sea-level. Its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form and the heights encircling it indicate that it was once a crater.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is famed for its eels and abundance of fish, and the slopes are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well cultivated, the upper parts being wooded, but malaria is prevalent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near Bracciano the road divides : the upper branch, to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the Capuchin monastery; the other to the right, to the town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bracciano (*L,ocanda Piva, unpretending), a modern town with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2000 inhab., possesses a picturesque castle of the 15th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several iron-works in the vicinity. The * Castle, erected by the Or¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sini, now the property of Prince Odescalchi, is very interesting, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its towers and fortifications convey a good idea of the character of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediaeval stronghold. It is said on this account to have riveted the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attention of Sir Walter Scott far more powerfully than the more im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posing ruins of antiquity. The interior, still inhabited, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nothing worthy of note. The *view from the tower, extending over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the beautiful lake to Trevignano and Anguillara, with Soracte and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sabine Mts. in the background, is remarkably fine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A pleasant excursion may be made from Bracciano to Trevignano, 6'/2{% endraw %}
{% raw %}M. distant. The road skirts the lake. After l'/2 M. a path ascends to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left to the old church of the martyrs SS. Marco, Marciano , and Liberate,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected, as the inscription informs us, on the site of an ancient villa named{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pausilypon, and affording a fine view. In the vicinity stood Forum Clodii,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from which inscriptions and other relics are preserved. Pedestrians may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regain the road to Vicarello by another forest-path. — Vicarello is 4 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Bracciano. The baths, 3/t M. from the road, with a hot sulphureous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spring, are the Aquae Apollinares of antiquity. A proof of the estimation in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which they were held was'afforded in 1852 by the discovery of great num¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bers of coins and votive offerings, most, of which are now in the Museo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kircheriano (p. 150). Owing to the malaria, the bathing season is not pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed beyond the early part of summer. — By the road are seen many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains of opus reticulatum, belonging to villas of the imperial epoch.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trevignano, occupying the site of the Etruscan town of Sabate, which fell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early into oblivion, formerly the property of the Orsini, now that of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Conti, is a poor village. Roman remains very scanty. In the principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church two pictures of the school of Perugino. The ruined castle above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}village commands a fine view; its destruction was due to Csesar Borgia.{% endraw %}
