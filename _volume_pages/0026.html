{% raw %}Steamboats. There are now so many different railway routes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from N. Italy to Rome that few travellers will care to travel by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamer from Genoa or Leghorn to Civita Vecchia, or from Trieste{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Ancona, although many will still prefer the sea-voyage to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long railway-journey if bound for Naples direct (see vol. iii. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Handbook). In fine weather, especially in summer, when sea¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sickness need rarely be apprehended, a voyage on the Mediterranean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is very enjoyable, but as the steamers generally ply at night the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finest parts of the scenery are often missed.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Tickets should be purchased by the traveller in person at the office of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the company. The ticket is furnished with the purchaser's name and de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stination, the fare, the name of the vessel, and the hour of departure. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saloons and berths of the first class are comfortably and elegantly fitted up,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those of the second tolerably. Passengers of the second class have free{% endraw %}
{% raw %}access , like those of the first, to every part of the deck. Officers of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian and French armies, up to and including those of the rank of captain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are entitled to second-class berths only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luggage. First-class passengers are allowed 100 kilogr. (2 cwt.), second-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}class 60 kilogr. (135 lbs.), but articles not intended for the passenger's private{% endraw %}
{% raw %}use are prohibited.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Food of good quality and ample quantity is included in the first and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally in the second-class fare. The steward's fee for a voyage of 12-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}24 hrs. is usually 1 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Embaecation, with luggage, generally 1 fr. for each person (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pp. 1, 7). The fare should not be paid until the passenger and his lug¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gage are safe on deck. Passengers should be on board an hour before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}advertised time of starting.{% endraw %}
