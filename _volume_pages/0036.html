{% raw %}ANCIENT ART.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the master-pieces of the Italian and other schools of painting fill in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the galleries of London, Paris, and Dresden. Winckelmann was the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first to trace in bold and comprehensive outline the history of Art{% endraw %}
{% raw %}amongst the Ancients, from its infancy and earlier growth to its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}maturity and gradual decline. Following in the wake of Winckel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mann, unceasing research, patiently and persistently pursued, has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served to confirm and extend his survey, and to supply, in addition,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a host of particulars pregnant with interest. Those indeed, who have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conducted this laborious quest, stimulated and directed by the for¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunate recovery of monuments and various relics of antiquity, have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been the means of determining the history of Antique Art, so far at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}least as Sculpture and Architecture are concerned ; and this not only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in its more salient features , but with an elaboration of detail, so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}careful and so well authenticated, that the authorship of numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works is clearly established, and the interest and value of their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discoveries in so far unspeakably enhanced. Much indeed remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be done, and the path of the explorer is beset with doubt and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}difficulty; but the future promises farther encouragement, if not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}complete solution.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In Art, the Greeks were content to learn from nations whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}civilisation preceded theirs. Long before the first image was graven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Grecian hands, the Egyptians, as far as history acquaints us, had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been the first to overcome those difficulties in the choice of a suitable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of expression inseparable from every fresh manifestation of Art.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They had established a well defined system, a traditional style,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was exemplified as well in their Sculpture as in their Archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tecture. On the other hand the richly elaborated Art of the Baby¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lonians and Assyrians, with its sumptuous wealth of decoration, must{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also of necessity have had its influence on the Greeks of Asia Minor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grecian Art had, to begin with, not only adopted the method,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}implements, and workmanship of their predecessors ; it had possess¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed itself of their forms and not unfrequently of the motives of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their imagery. Greece did not, however, accept this heritage of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}art in the spirit of slavish imitation. Architectural models were not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adopted in their entirety. Each part separately, each ornament in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itself, became the centre of a new conception, and this conception{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once embodied became a distinct type, the expression of a taste{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purely Greek. In like manner they treated the conventional render¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing of the human form transmitted to them. This, by constant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reference to the living model, they continued to manipulate and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modify until they attained to that profound and comprehensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grasp of nature, which to this day remains the subject of wonder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and delight to our greatest artists. The way thus traversed was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long and wearisome. It carries us back through an incalculable suc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cession of ages into the impenetrable past. The oldest Ionic and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Doric temples, although well-nigh of prehistoric antiquity, exhibit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in all essential particulars a clearly defined architectural system :{% endraw %}
