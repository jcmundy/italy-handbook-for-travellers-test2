{% raw %}Palestrina, the Praeneste of antiquity, one of the most ancient towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Italy, was captured by Camillus, B.C. 380, and was thenceforth subject{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Rome. In the civil wars it was the principal arsenal of the younger{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marius, and after a long siege was taken and destroyed by Sulla, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards rebuilt it in a magnificent style as a Roman colony. Under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the emperors it was a favourite resort of the Romans on account of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refreshing atmosphere, and is extolled by Horace (Carm. iii, 4, 221 to¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gether with Tibur and Raise. A celebrated Temple of Fortune and an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oracle ('sortes Prsenestinaj', Cic. Div. ii, 41) attracted numerous visitors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the middle ages Palestrina was long the source of sanguinary conflicts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the powerful Colonnas and the popes, the result of which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the total destruction of the town in 1436. The territory was purchased{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1630 by the Barberini, who are still proprietors of the soil. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great composer Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina, who died at Rome as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}director of the choir of St. Peter's in 1594, was born here in 1524.{% endraw %}
