{% raw %}ANCIENT ART.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}xxxvu{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emperor Hadrian, adopting as it did not only Greek but also Egyp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tian models, was eclectic in the most extended sense of the word.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amongst its better known works is the figure of Hadrian's favourite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antinous, in which with undoubted beauty of form is combined a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}somewhat lugubrious solemnity together with a smooth glossy and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}superficial elegance little to the taste of the modern connoisseur.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Far more attractive are the basreliefs on various public monuments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— such as the Arch of Titus and Trajan's Column — celebrating{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the triumphs of Roman arms. With Greek artists a characteristic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mode of representing in relief the triumphs, actual or mythical,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of their heroes was to express battle by means of isolated groups{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of single combatants. And not only are the more stirring incidents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the battle thus signalised. Appeal is made to the gentler emo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions : a dying warrior is carried off the field; or the victor spares{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the life of a prostrate adversary. For plastic purposes this was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found to be the most effective mode of representation, serving as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it does to awaken the interest and rivet the attention of beholders.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Assyrians had, however, already executed reliefs in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the effect of pitched battle was rendered with more literal accuracy;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and that the Greeks, too, could on occasion meet the requirements{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of this more arbitrary taste, we learn from the friezes of the so-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called Nereid monument discovered in Lycia, where the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tending hosts close with each other in elaborate order of battle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The painter, favoured by conditions more submissive to his will, had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}already grouped the combatants in larger and denser masses. How{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admirably they contrived along with the crowd and confusion of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}battle, to give effect to traits of individual heroism and to give to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}single and central figures their appropriate expression, is exem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plified in the celebrated mosaic to be seen in Naples of the Battle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Alexander, which, there is no doubt, was copied from a painting of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the period. It may be premised therefore that this condensed and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elaborated treatment in relief — obviously akin to painting — in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the marches, battles, and triumphs, the operations of Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}armies and their imperial chiefs, were set forth with the utmost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attainable accuracy, with all detail of equipment and armament of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}camp and battle-field, was not the newly gathered fruit of Roman in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ventiveness , but must rather be ascribed to the age of Alexander{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and his successors. And the same may be assumed of the archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tectural form of these monuments. In Portraits, too, whether of full{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length or only busts, of emperors andempresses, warriors and states¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}men, as of persons of less exalted position, there were not wanting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impressive examples in Greek art; and here again Roman taste{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coincides with that of the Diadochan age. It may be conceded,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, that owing to the interest long taken in portraiture by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romans and to the attention which this branch of art had so long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}received in Rome and Etruria, it had acquired a more distinctly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman and Italian character, and so had a perceptible influence{% endraw %}
