{% raw %}ANCIENT ART.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}xli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman time is the Sarcophagus of L. Cornelius Scipio Barbatus in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Museum of the Vatican. It resembles an altar both in shape and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style of ornamentation, and is almost the counterpart of one still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing in Pompeii. It is only consistent with the then prevailing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}religious rites that sepulchral monuments should have been thus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}architectural in character. In Greece itself this was conspicuously{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the case: all sarcophagi which have been discovered within the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fines of Geece proper showing a distinctly architectural treatment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Roman sarcophagi combine much that is essentially Greek with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adaptations from the funeral urns of Etruria. They give signs, how¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ever, of an independent development, and although including a di¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}versity of shapes and decoration, have for the most part their bas-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs arranged on the front and sides (and, where extraordinary rich¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness of effect was desiderated, on the back also) as a frieze or band.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One naturally endeavours to trace in the decoration bestowed on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these repositories of the dead, some indication of their purpose. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many instances, however, it is evident, that appropriateness of design,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if originally acknowledged as indispensable, was presently lost in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promiscuously lavish decoration. Certainly there is no obscurity in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such allusions to the goal of life as we discover in Cupids rowing te{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lighthouse tower, or when we see them careering round the goal in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the circus. In such symbolical figures as those of the seasons we are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taught to Teflect on the inevitable course of creation, existence, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decay succeeding to maturity. AsHylas is borne away by the Nymphs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Ganymede by the eagle, so we may fancy the soul begrudged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from its earthly existence. Hippolytus may serve to recal the virtues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of such as came to an untimely end, Niobe, the grief of the survivors ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sleeping Cupids may symbolise sleep favoured by the Gods, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ariadne discovered by Dionysus, Endymion visited by Selene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present death itself as but sleep in unfamiliar guise. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other hand scenes of Bacchanalian revelry can hardly be accepted as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}allusions to the future state; and even in a less degree are Nereids{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Medeahs, and more of the like, in bas-relief, capable of such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interpretation: and rarely, too, does any reference of a distinctly per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sonal character go beyond a mere vague allusion to life and death.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is tolerably certain that these sarcophagi were made in large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numbers, in advance of immediate requirements. A somewhat extra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ordinary expedient for introducing a reference to particular indi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}viduals, was that of bestowing the lineaments of the departed upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such heroes of mythology as were made to figure in these reliefs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thus it is we find portraits of the deceased in such mythical per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sonages as Admetus andAlcestis, in Hippolytus, and, what is more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkable, in Phaedra herself. In a considerable number of cases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these reliefs are almost identical, and are evidently made after one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}model, with such modifications as might be effected by the intro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duction or omission of single figures or groups, showing nevertheless{% endraw %}
