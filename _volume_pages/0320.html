{% raw %}The Ground Plan of the basilica is a rectangle, about 111 yds. long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 53 yds. wide. A flight of six, and at places nine, steps ascended to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the basilica from the street. Along the four sides were double aisles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which enclosed a Central Space, about 90 yds. by 17 yds., paved with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}variegated African and Phrygian marble, and separated from the aisles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by iron railings. The greater part of the pavement has been restored,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few fragments of the original only having been preserved. The valuable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}material of which the pavement was composed renders it probable that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this space was covered with a roof. The sittings of the tribunal of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Centumviri, in four different sections, took place here. The Aisles were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paved with white marble, on which are still seen a number of circles,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and occasionally writing, scratched on the surface by visitors. These{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were used by them in playing a game resembling draughts, to which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Romans were as devoted as the modern. The aisles were separated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a triple row of Columns, sixteen on each side, and ten at each end,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed of brick and encrusted with travertine. On the side next the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street the pillars were adorned with Doric half-columns built against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them. Ten only of the ancient pillars, up to a height of about 16 ft.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are now preserved at the S.W. corner of the building. All the other trunks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of pillars which are seen here have recently been reconstructed, partly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the original materials. The pillars supported arches, which have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also been restored, but their original spring is still clearly distinguishable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The building had an upper story to which the steps still traceable on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. side ascended. On this side the basilica was adjoined by older{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buildings, constructed of tuffstone, which seem to have been 'tabernse',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or shops, but have not yet been thoroughly excavated or explored.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the E. of the Basilica, and separated from it by the street, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the * Temple of Castor and Pollux, dedicated to the twin gods out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of gratitude for the assistance they were supposed to have rendered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Romans at the battle of Lake Regillus in B.C. 496, in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theLatini were defeated, and inaugurated in484. It was afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rebuilt hy Tiberius and re-consecrated in A.D. 6. This was one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most famous temples of the Republic, and was frequently used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the meetings of the senate.{% endraw %}
