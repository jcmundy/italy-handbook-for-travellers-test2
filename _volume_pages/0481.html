{% raw %}Alban Mts. MARINO. Environs of Rome. 357{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A monument of Cardinal Consalvi, who died, of poison it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}believed, as abbot of Grotta Ferrata in 1824, and several ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptures are shown in the handsome Abbey. The small Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the altar is by Ann. Caracci; a bust of Domenichino is by Te¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resa Benincampi, a pupil of Canova. Fairs held here on 25th March{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 8th Sept. attract numerous peasants from the neighbourhood,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as well as strangers from Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 2M. farther to theS., and reached either by footpaths or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the high road, is Marino, a small town famous for its wine, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesquely situated on an eminence of the Alban Mts., 1322 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height, the site of the ancient Castrimcenium. In the middle ages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it was a stronghold of the Orsini, who defended themselves here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against their enemies, particularly the Colonna; but the latter, un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der Martin V. in 1424, captured Marino and are still proprietors of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the place. The town contains a Corso, which is the principal street,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Fountain, and a Cathedral dedicated to St. Barnabas. The church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Trinita, to the left of the Corso, contains a Trinity by Guido{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reni. In the church of the Madonna delle Grazie, St. Rochus by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Domenichino. In the Cathedral a badly-preserved St. Bartholomew{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Guercino.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Marino Station on the Rome and Naples line is situated in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campagna, 3 M. distant (railway to Rome in 35 min.; fares 2 fr., 1 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}40 c, 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the town of Marino a shady road, commanding extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}views, leads through the Parco di Colonna, the well-wooded valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Aqua Ferentina, a brook often mentioned in history as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rallying-point of the Latins, to the Alban lake, and by Castel Gan-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dolfo to (3% M.) Albano, see p. 360.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Frascati and Marino road, there diverges at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge in the valley, rather more than half-way to ,the latter, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}new road to Rocca di Papa, situated about 5 M. from Frascati (one-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horse carr. about 8 fr.). It may also be reached from Tusculum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 355) direct in 1 '/.> hr. by field and forest-paths (guide necessary,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1-1 Va fr-)-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rocca di Papa, a wretched little town with 2500 inhab., lying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the brink of the great crater of Campo d'Annibale (see below), in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the midst of beautiful forest-scenery, is well adapted for a summer-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence on account of its lofty situation (2647 ft.). Rooms, even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for one night, may be heard of at the Caffe dell' Aurora, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upper part of the town. The two Trattorie, one of which has a lo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canda in connection with it, are very poor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ascending the steep streets of the town, we reach in 15-20 min.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great crater of Campo d'Annibale, so named from the unfounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tradition that Hannibal once pitched his camp here during his cam¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paign against Rome. It is more,probable that the Romans were en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}camped here at that period, to repel the attacks of the Carthaginians.{% endraw %}
