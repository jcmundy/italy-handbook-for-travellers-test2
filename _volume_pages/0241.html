{% raw %}S. Marco, incorporated with the Pal. di Venezia, a church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very ancient origin, said to date from the time of Constantine, ru-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 833 by Gregory IV., adorned in 1455 by Giuliano da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Majano with a fine vestibule and probably with the coffered ceiling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the nave, and finally embellished in modern taste by Card. Quirini{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1744. Festival on 25th April.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vestibule. Roman and ancient Christian inscriptions, built into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls. St. Mark in relief, above the handsome inner principal portal. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior is approached by a descent of several steps. With the exception{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the tribune and the beautiful ceiling, all the older parts have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disfigured by restorations. The Tribune, with its handsome pavement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(opus Alexandrinum), lies a few steps higher than the rest of the church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The mosaics (in the centre Christ; left, the saints Mark, Agapetus, and Agnes;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, Felicianus and Mark escorting Gregory IV.) date from tlie period of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the utmost debasement of this art (about 833) and have been justly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}described as 'utter caricatures'. In the Right Aisle, 1st Chapel: altar-piece{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Palma Giovine, the Resurrection. 3rd Chapel: Adoration of the Magi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maralta. At the end, adjoining the tribune: sPope Mark, an admirable old{% endraw %}

{% raw %}St. Michael, Mola.{% endraw %}
