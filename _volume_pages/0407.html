{% raw %}The Vatican. ROME. V. Right Bank. 299{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door adjoining the 'School of Athens' leads to the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II. Stanza d'Eliodoro, the frescoes of which were painted in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1511-14. The mural paintings, from the first of which the sa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loon derives its name, represent the triumph and divine protection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the church, in connection with the age of the warlike Julius II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the elevation of Leo X.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Ceiling are four scenes from the old Covenant, unfor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunately much damaged : Jehovah appears to Noah, Jacob's Vision,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Moses at the burning bush, Sacrifice of Isaac.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mural Paintings. Below the Moses : 1. Miraculous Expulsion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}op Heliodorus from the Temple at Jerusalem by a heavenly horse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man (Maccab. ii, 3), being an allusion to the deliverance of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}States of the Church from their enemies. On the right Heliodorus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies on the ground; one of his companions attempts to defend him¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}self, a second shouts, a third strives to secure his booty; in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}background the high-priest Onias praying; to the left in the fore¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground women and children, and Pope Julius II. on his throne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(the hindmost of the two chair-hearers is the celebrated engraver{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marcantonio Raimondi). This composition is remarkable for its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admirable vigour of expression.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Below the Sacrifice of Isaac : 2. The Mass of Bolsena. An un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}believing priest is convinced of the truth of the doctrine of tran-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}substantiation by the bleeding of the host (comp. p. 65), a miracle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is said to have taken place at Bolsena in 1263; below are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}women and children; opposite the priest, Julius II. kneeling with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calm equanimity; the wrathful cardinal is Riario (founder of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cancelleria). This work is probably the most perfect of Raphael's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes with respect to execution.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Below Noah: 3. Attila repulsed from Rome by Leo I., in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}allusion to the expulsion of the French from Italy after the battle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Novara in 1513. The pope, with the features of Leo X., is seated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on a white mule, around him cardinals and attendants on horse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, above him St. Peter and St. Paul enveloped in a brilliant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}light, and distinctly visible to Attila and his Huns, who are struck{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with terror at the apparition. To the right of this —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Below Jacob's Vision: 4. The Liberation of Peter, in three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sections. Over the window Peter in the dungeon sleeping between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the watchmen and awakened by the angel; right, he is conducted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}away; left, the watchmen awake.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the pictures are painted eleven Caryatides and four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues in grisaille. They are symbolical of a life of peace, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bear the distinct impress of Raphael's inventive genius, notwith¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing considerable restoration. The paintings in different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shades of brown between these, of similar import with the large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures, have been still more freely retouched.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These two apartments were painted by Raphael's own hand, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his progressive freedom and decision of touch are distinctly trace-{% endraw %}
