{% raw %}Exposure to the summer-sun should be avoided as much as pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sible. According to a Roman proverb, dogs and foreigners (Inglesi){% endraw %}
{% raw %}only walk in the sun, Christians in the shade. Umbrellas or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spectacles of coloured glass (grey, concave glasses to protect the whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eye are best) may be used with advantage when a walk in the sun is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unavoidable. Blue veils are recommended to ladies. Repose during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hottest hours is advisable, and a moderate siesta is often refresh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing. Windows should be closed at night.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Health. English and German medical men are to be met with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the larger cities. The Italian therapeutic art does not enjoy a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very high reputation in the rest of Europe. English and German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chemists, where available, are recommended in preference to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian. It may, however, be wise, in the case of maladies arising{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from local causes, to employ native skill. Foreigners frequently suffer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from diarrhoea in Italy, which is generally occasioned by the un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wonted heat. Ice and rice are two of the commonest remedies. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}homoeopathic tincture of camphor may also be mentioned. In such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cases, however, thorough repose is the chief desideratum.{% endraw %}
