{% raw %}Roman Art,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}MEDIEVAL AND MOI'KKN,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Prof. A. Springer of Leipsic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome as Mistress of the world became the centre of contempora¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}neous culture. Art had found with her a new term : and Greece as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fitting tribute to the conqueror laid at her feet the accumulated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wealth of ages — the treasures of her art, which long had embodied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the loftiest conception of the beautiful.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Her supremacy secured, Rome became the chief resort of artists,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and their liberal patron. She dictated the tone, alike in taste and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fashion, and determined the destinies of art. Down to mediaeval{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times Rome continued to receive the proud title of 'Caput mundi'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Presently, however, she laid claim to supremacy in another realm{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than that of art; and this latter, as the ancient traditions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were gradually outlived, finally fell into neglect. In more recent,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as in former times Rome has failed to create for herself, as the out¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}come of her individuality, an art peculiar to and a part of herself.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Her destiny seems to have been to gather from external sources{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wealth in which she revelled, with the difference that while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Rome furnished nothing beyond a magnificent arena for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the art of her day, in later times the artist found in Rome herself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his sources of inspiration , compelled as he was to contemplate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perfection reflected in the dazzling mirror of antique art. Ten{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centuries, however, elapsed ere Rome resumed this proud pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eminence. A glance may now be directed to the interval between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fall of old Rome and the period when, animated with a new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life, Rome drew to herself the foremost representatives of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Renaissance, to whom she afforded inspiration for their grandest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}efforts. It is not, however, the 16th century, not the glories of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Renaissance, that give to the Rome of our day her distinctive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character, but rather the new and imposing exterior which she re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceived at the hand of her architects in the 17th century. The mind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must be disenchanted before the veil can be penetrated and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome of antiquity adequately comprehended.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}rent that Roman art has a history distinct from Italian art. For{% endraw %}
