{% raw %}The Sabine Mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}That chain of the Apennines which descends abruptly and bounds the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman plain on the E., named Sabine Mts. from their ancient inhabitants,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is replete with interest for lovers of the picturesque. The formation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these mountains is limestone, differing entirely from that of the volcanic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alban Mts., and their height, is much greater, attaining to 4200 ft. Owing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the want of railway-communication, the characteristics of city-life which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}produce an unpleasing impression at Frascati and Albano are little known{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here, except at Tivoli. although attempts at extortion are occasionally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made. — As a rule the Inns are good and not expensive, but enquiry as to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charges should be made beforehand; usual charge for board and lodging{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 fr. , and 1/2 fr. gratuity.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Those whose time is short must be satisfied with a visit to Tivoli; but,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if possible, four days at least should be devoted to the Sabine Mts., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may best be spent as follows: 1st day, by Frascati to Pedestriua ; 2nd, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Olevano; 3rd, to Subiaco; 4th, to Tivoli; 5th, back to Rome. The whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trip may be accomplished by carriage, but some of the excursions at least{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be made on foot or on donkey-back. The public conveyances are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not recommended when ladies are of the party, in which case a private{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carriage should be hired. Tivoli affords the best summer quarters, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Subiaco and Olevano are also pleasant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tivoli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Rome to Tivoli, 17 M. One-horse carriage about 20 fr., fee 2 fr.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two-horse 30 fr., fee 5 fr. ; the hirer should expressly stipulate that a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}digression to Hadrian's villa be included in the fare. The charges are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rather higher about Easter. The carriages at the hotels are dearer (40-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 fr.), but they drive more rapidly, a great advantage in hot and dusty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}weather.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Vetturini, whose vehicles are uninviting, convey passengers to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tivoli twice daily, starting from No. 124 Monte Citorio (booking-office No. 15{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vicolo della Guardiola) at 4.30 a. m. and at 2.30 p.m., reaching Tivoli in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4-5 hrs. , and returning from Tivoli at the same hours ; fare S'/z fr. (In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the height of summer a carriage sometimes starts from Rome at 11.30 p.m.,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Those who wish to visit Hadrian's villa, the grottoes, cascades, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Villa d'Este, and return to Rome in the evening, should start at daybreak.{% endraw %}
