{% raw %}S. Paolo Fuori le Mura. ROME. IV. Ancient Rome. 255{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half-hour from the Piazza Campitelli (p. 113; PL II, 17), 6 soldi;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fiacre 11/2-2 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}* S.Paolo Fuori le Mura was founded in 388 by Theodosius and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Valentinian II. on the site of a small church of Constantine, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was restored and embellished by many of the popes, especially Leo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}111. Prior to the conflagration of the night of 15th July, 1823, this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was the finest and most interesting church at Rome. It was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}basilica with double aisles and open roof; and the architrave was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supporled by eighty columns of pavonazzetto and Parian marble,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with busts of the popes. It contained numerous ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosaics and frescoes, and in the Confessio the sarcophagus of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paul, who, according to tradition, was interred by a pious woman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}named Lucina on her property here. The front towards the Tiber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was approached by a colonnade, and early in the middle ages an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arcaded passage connected it with the city.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately after the fire, Leo XII. began the work of restora¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion, which was presided over by Belli, and afterwards by Potetti.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The transept was consecrated by Gregory XVI. in 1840, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole church by Pius IX. in 1854, on the occasion of the meeting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Council. The plan and the dimensions are the same as those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the original building, with which, however, the gorgeous decoration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other details are entirely inconsistent, and we now have a showy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and, in many respects, unpleasing pile, instead of the simple and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}majestic early Christian basilica. The chief facade, as formerly, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turned towards the Tiber. The mosaics on the upper part of it,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed in 1875, representing Christ with SS. Peter and Paul, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the symbolical style of the early Christians, with the four great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prophets below them, were executed hy F. Agricola and Consoni,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the papal mosaic manufactory. The lower half of the facade,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the Atrium, is still uncompleted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The present Entrance is either from the road on the opposite (E.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}side, or by the portico on the N. side. The former, at the back of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}campanile, should be selected.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Small Chamber first entered contains a colossal statue of Gregory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XVI., and a few frescoes and ancient mosaics rescued from the lire. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left is the entrance to the Sacristy , which contains several good oil-pain¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tings. Over the door the Scourging of Christ (attributed to Signorelli), on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right a Madonna with SS. Benedict, Paul, Peter, and Justina. Also four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}single figures of the same saints. — In a straight direction from the entrance-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hall several chapels are reached, containing a few ancient but largely-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored frescoes. The second to the right contains an entrance into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church, and the last to the left the entrance to the court of the monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see below), and another to the church on the right. We first enter the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transept, but the following description begins with the nave.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior (130 yds. in length, 65 yds. in width, 75 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height), with double aisles and a transept, borne by columns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granite from the Simplon, is imposing from its vast dimensions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the valuable materials of which it is built. The best survey of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is obtained from the W. end of the nave.{% endraw %}
