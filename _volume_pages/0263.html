{% raw %}Arch of Gallienus. ROME. II. The Hills. 181{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crocefisso contains the tomb of a French cardinal (d. 1286). — In the Lett{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aisle by the entrance-wall is a stone-slab, on which St. Praxedis is said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have slept. The 2nd Cap. di S. Carlo Borromeo contains a chair and table{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once used by the saint. The 3rd Cap. Agiati contains paintings by the Car.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Arpino. — The marble spout of a fountain in the nave indicates the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spot where St. Praxedis collected the blood of the martyrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Confessio (keys kept by the sacristan) contains ancient sarcophagi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the bones of the sister saints Praxedis and Pudentiana on the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and those of martyrs on the left. The altar is decorated with fine mosaic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 13th cent. Above it an ancient fresco of the Madonna between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sisters. — The Saoiusty contains a Scourging by Giulio Romano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. and S.E. of the Piazza S. Maria Maggiore run the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via S. Eusebio and the Via Merulana (p. 185) , two streets now in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course of construction. In the execution of the municipal improve¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments great alterations in the appearance of the ground have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made here. A great part of the surface has been lowered 13-16 ft.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a process which led to the discovery of numerous remains of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls, fragments of sculptures in marble and bronze, sarcophagi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, etc. The ruins have been partly covered up again, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nothing certain is known of their history.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We follow the Via S. Eusebio (PL II, 25, 28), from which, im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediately to the right, the Via di S. Vito diverges, passing under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Arch of Gallienus. This honorary arch, which closely adjoins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church of S. Vito, was erected in 262 in honour of the Emp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gallienus 'on account of his bravery , surpassed only by his piety'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a certain M. Aurelius Victor. The architecture is simple, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the degraded style of the age.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on in the Via S. Eusebio is the church of S. Eusebio, re-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in the last century, with the exception of the campanile.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ceiling-painting, the glory of St. Eusebius, is one of the earliest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of Raphael Mengs; the high altar-piece is by Bald. Croce.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the church, to the right, are considerable remains of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}water-tower of the Aqua Julia or Claudia (PL II, 28), in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niches of which the so-called trophies of Marius, now on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}balustrade of the Capitol, were formerly placed (p. 213). The ruin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is called Trofei di Mario. — For the prolongation of the street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Porta Maggiore, see p. 183.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turning to the left into the new street between S. Eusebio and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Trofei di Mario, we reach ('/2 M.) the Porta S. Lorenzo (PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II, 31), constructed by Honorius against an arch, over which, ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cording to the inscription, the three aqueducts Marcia, Tepula,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Julia passed. The arch stands on its original site, while the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gateway occupies considerably higher ground. The gate derives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its name from the basilica situated outside the gate , and stands on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the site of the ancient Porta Tiburtina , which led to Tivoli. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road ( Via Tiburtina) is bounded by walls, and does not afford views{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Sabine Mts. until the church is reached, 3/4M. from the gate.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Lorenzo Fuori le Mura (see map, p. 339) occupies the spot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where Constantine first founded a church on the burial-place of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lawrence and St. Cyriaca. In 578 it was rebuilt by Pelagius II.{% endraw %}
