{% raw %}Pal. R. del Quirinale. ROME. II. The Hills. 171{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The conclaves of the cardinals were at one time held here, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of the newly elected pope was proclaimed from the balcony of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the facade towards Monte Cavallo. Pius VII. died here in 1823.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After 20th Sept. 1870, the palace was taken possession of by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian government, and being now the residence of the king, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}greater part is seldom shown to the public (p. 118).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the principal entrance we proceed in a straight direction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hetween the sentinels and ascend the broad staircase to the left at.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the end of the vestibule. At the top of the staircase we write our names{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a hook, and obtain the escort of an attendant (I fr.). Adjacent to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sala Regia , with frescoes by Lanfranco and Saraceni, is the Cappella{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paolina , erected by Carlo Maderna, and decorated with gilded stucco-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work and copies in grisaille of Raphael's Apostles in S. Vincenzo ed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Anastasio alle Tre Fontane, and with tapestry of the 18th cent. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right lies a suite of apartments , Drawing and Reception Rooms, newly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fitted up, and adorned with pictures and tapestries, chiefly modern. In the{% endraw %}
