{% raw %}GROSSETO.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7. Route. >J>{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the wise administration of the grand-dukes of Tuscany, much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was done to counteract the malaria by the drainage and tilling up{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of swamps and the establishment of new farms; but the evil is still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very great. Charcoal-burning and in winter cattle-grazing are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief resources of the inhabitants, all of whom withdraw to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscan hill-country in May, when the malaria begins. A few only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the more densely peopled localities enjoy a tolerably healthy at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosphere. Those of the natives who are compelled to remain suffer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}severely from fever, and their gaunt and emaciated countenances{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afford a sad indication of the curse of the district.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}65 M. Follonica, near the sea, a small but industrial place which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is deserted in summer, possesses considerable smelting-foundries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the iron from Elba. Beautiful view towards the sea; to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the promontory of Piombino and Elba, to the left the promontory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Castiglione with a lighthouse, and the small, grotesquely shaped{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island of Formica. On an eminence to the left rises Massa Marit-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tima, one of the largest towns of the Maremme, with about 13,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhabitants. In the vicinity are extensive copper-mines. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}train again quits the coast in order to avoid the Promontory of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Castiglione.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}74 M. Potassa, station for Gavorrano, situated higher up, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right. Farther on, likewise to the right, on an eminence, lies Co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lonna; and in the distance, at the mouth of the river Bruna, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small fortified harbour of Castiglione della Pescaia is visible. Here,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as in the other seaports of the Maremme, wood and charcoal form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal exports.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}80'/2 M. Monte Pescali, junction of a branch-line from Siena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see p. 21), which runs parallel to our line as far as Grosseto.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}91 M. Grosseto (*Aquila), the capital of the Maremme, a cheer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful little town with 6300 inhab. The cure' Chelli possesses a col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lection of Etruscan antiquities. Branch-line from Grosseto to As-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciano, a station on the Florence, Siena, and Orvieto line (seep. 18).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 31/2 M. to the N. E. of Grosseto (carriage road) are situated the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sulphureous Bagni di Roselle, whence the ruins of Rusellae are reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1/2 hr. (guide necessary). Rusellse, anciently one of the twelve capitals{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Etruscan confederation, has been deserted since the middle of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12th cent, and is thickly overgrown with underwood. The walls, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are nearly 2 M. in circumference, and in most places accessible, consist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}partly of horizontal courses, partly of polygonal blocks (6-8 ft. high, 7-12 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Around Grosseto and to the W. in the direction of Castiglione extends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a plain of considerable magnitude, in ancient times a lake (the Lacus Prelius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cicero), which gradually became shallower (Palude di Castiglione and di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grosseto), and by its exhalations formed one of the chief sources of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}malaria. By means of skilful drainage, and by conducting hither the de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posits of the neighbouring rivers, the government has succeeded in almost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entirely filling up the morass and converting it into a valuable pasture,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12-15 M. in length.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little beyond Grosseto the Ombrone is crossed. The line{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirts the wooded Promontory of Talamone; towards the S. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imposing Monte Argontario (see below) becomes visible.{% endraw %}
