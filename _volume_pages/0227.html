{% raw %}an establishment for the propagation of the Roman Catholic faith,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which pupils of many different nationalities are educated as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}missionaries. The printing-office of the college was formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated as the richest in type for foreign languages. — On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right is the palace of the Spanish ambassador, whence the piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derives its name. Near the column of the Immacolata, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, is the small Piazza Mignanelli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately opposite the Scala di Spagna is the Via de' Con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dotti, with its numerous shops of jewellery, mosaics, antiquities,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}photographs, etc. It terminates in the Corso, opposite the spacious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palazzo Ruspoli (p. 147).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Piazza di Spagna, in a S.E. direction , to the left of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Propaganda, runs the Via de' Due Macelli, and to the right the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via di Propaganda. If we follow the latter, we reach the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Andrea delle Fratte (PI. I, 19) at the corner of the Via di Capo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}le Case, the next transverse street (p. 163). It was erected under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leo XI. by La Guerra; the unsightly dome and campanile are by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Borromini; the facade was added in 1826 by Valadier in consequence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a bequest by Cardinal Consalvi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pictures in the Interior are poor works of the 17th cent.: the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two angels by the tribune, by Bernini, were originally destined for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge of S. Angelo. In the 2nd Chapel on the right is (on the right side){% endraw %}
{% raw %}the monument of Lady Falconet by Miss Hossmer; on the last pillar to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, in front of the aisle, the monument of the artist R. Schadow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by E. Wolff. In the 3rd Chapel to the left, by the right wall, is the tomb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Angelica Kauffmann. The Danish archaeologist Zoega and a converted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prince of Morocco are also interred in this church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the extremity of the Via di S. Andrea delle Fratte we enter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the narrow Via del Nazzareno to the left. On the left is the Col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}legio Nazzareno (in the court several ancient statues), founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Card. Tonti (1622) for the education of destitute boys. Opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Pal. del Bufalo. Then, to the left, the Via dell' Angelo Cus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tode (in which, immediately to the right, is the small church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}■SS. Angeli Custodi) and the Via del Tritone lead direct to the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Barberini (p. 164).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We turn to the right into the Via della Stamperia, so called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the ex-papal Printing-Office situated in it (right). Adjacent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the latter is the extensive royal Engraving Institute(Reyia I'aleo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grafia, p. 110), with warehouse, where the office of the Minister of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Commerce is also now established. No. 4 is the entrance to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German Artists' Association.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now reach the *Fontana di Trevi (PI. I, 19), which vies in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnificence with the Acqua Paola. It is erected on the side-wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Palazzo Poli, and was completed from a design by Niccolb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Salri in 1762; in the central niche Neptune, by Pietro Bracci, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sides Health (left) and Fertility (right); in front of these is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large stone basin.{% endraw %}
