{% raw %}278 V. Right Bank. ROME. The Borgo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly the Piazza Pia, whence four streets diverge to the W.:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the centre, on both sides of the fountain, which like the two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adjacent facades was erected by Pius IX., arc the streets called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Borgo Vecchio (1.) and Borgo Nuovo (r.); to the left, by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river, the Borgo S. Spirito; to the right is the Borgo S. Angelo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N., between the latter and the city-wall, lies a dirty quarter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consisting of mean houses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ordinary route to the Vatican is by the Borgo Nuovo. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right in this street is the church of S. Maria Traspontina (PL I,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7. 5), erected in 1566. Farther on, to the right, in the small Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Scossa Cavalli, is the handsome *Palazzo Giraud (PL 4), now Tor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lonia, the property of Prince Torlonia, erected in 1506 by Bramante{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for Card. Adriano da Corneto ; the poor portal dates from the 18th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent. In an adjacent building are several valuable antiquities, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding the so-called Vesta Giustiniani; visitors not admitted. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near it is the insignificant church of S. Giacomo (PL 7). In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre of the piazza is a small fountain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right in the Borgo Nuovo, farther on, is the Pal. Ric-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciardi, erected for Giacomo da Brescia, the physician of Leo X.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a design attributed to Bald. Peruzzi. Proceeding hence in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}straight direction, we reach the Piazza Rusticucci, 288 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length , which forms a kind of entrance-court to St. Peter's. Ra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phael's house, which stood on the right side, near the Pal. Ac-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coramboni (PL 2), was removed when the piazza was enlarged.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Borgo S. Spirito, issuing from the Piazza del Plebiscito{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(or Pia), terminates under the colonnades of the piazza of St. Peter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left in this street, by the river , is the spacious Ospedale di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Spirito (PL I, 7), established by Innocent III., and embracing a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hospital, a lunatic-asylum, a foundling-institution (shown 2-4 p.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}m. ; permesso obtained at the office of the administration, or in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}library), an establishment for the reception of girls, a refuge for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aged and infirm, and a valuable medical library (open 8-12 o'clock).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The three departments first mentioned can accommodate 1000, 500,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 3000 persons respectively. The Military Hospital is on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite side of the street. The 'borgo', or settlement, of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English was once situated here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on , to the left, is the church of >S. Spirito in Sassia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 12), erected by Antonio da S. Gallo under Paul III., and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade by Mascherino under Sixtus V. It belongs to the adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hospital and contains nothing noteworthy, except a bronze ciborium{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributed to Palladio over the high-altar.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Porta S. Spirito, from which the Via della Longara leads to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trastevere (see p. 320).{% endraw %}
