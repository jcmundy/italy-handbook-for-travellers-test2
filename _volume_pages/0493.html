{% raw %}Tivoli, the Tibur of antiquity, existed, according to tradition,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a colony of the Siculi long before the foundation of Rome. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B.C. 380 Camillus subjugated Tibur along with Praneste, after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which it formed a member of the league of the Latin towns allied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Rome. Hercules and Vesta were especially revered at Tibur.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the Roman nobles, including Maecenas, and the emperor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Augustus himself, founded beautiful villas here ; under Hadrian the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}splendour of the place attained its climax; and in the middle ages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it participated in the fate of Rome. In 1460 Pius II. founded the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}citadel on the ruins of the amphitheatre. The modem town (7000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab.), with its narrow streets, offers few attractions beyond its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charming situation. It is moreover somewhat damp and windy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially in spring.{% endraw %}
