{% raw %}Ancona, the capital of a province, with 28,000 inhab. find.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suburbs 45,700), of whom upwards of 6000 are Jews, and possess¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing an excellent harbour, is beautifully situated between the two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promontories of Monte Ciriaco and Monte Conero or Monte Guasco.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Since 1860 the harbour has been considerably improved by govern¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment, and the trade of the place has increased in consequence. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}port is now entered by upwards of 700 steamers and 1300 sailing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vessels annually. Silk and oil are largely manufactured here. An¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cona is celebrated for the beauty of its women, and deserves a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visit on account of its picturesque situation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ancona is supposed to have been founded by Doric Greeks from Syra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cuse, and was thence named Dorica Ancon (i.e. 'elbow', from the form of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the promontory). It was afterwards a Roman colony, and the harbour was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enlarged by Trajan. In the middle ages it repeatedly recovered from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ravages of the Goths and others, and in 1532 came into the possession of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pope Clement VII. through the instrumentality of Gonzaga. Ancona is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also frequently mentioned as a fortress in the annals of modern warfare.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thus in 1796 it was surrendered to the French , in 1799 to the Austrians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1805 to the French again ; in 1815 it was ceded to the pope, to whom it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonged till 1860. In 1832-38 the citadel was garrisoned by the French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(under the Perier ministry), in order to keep in check the Austrians, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were in possession of Bologna and the surrounding provinces. In 1849 the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town was the scene of many excesses, and on 18th June was re-captured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Austrians. On 20th Sept., 1860, eleven days after the Battle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Castelfidardo (p. 99), it was finally occupied by the Italians.{% endraw %}
