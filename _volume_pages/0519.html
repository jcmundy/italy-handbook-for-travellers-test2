{% raw %}Owing to the extensive alluvial deposits of the Tiber, the delta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which advances on an average 12'/2 ft. annually, the harbour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Ostia was gradually filled with sand, and the lower quarters of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the capital itself were frequently exposed to danger from inundations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}owing to the diminished fall of the channel. The Emp. Claudius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accordingly constructed a new harbour on the coast, enclosed it by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moles, and conducted into it a canal from the Tiber; but the rem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edy did not long remain effectual. In 103 Trajan constructed a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}new harbour and town, which were called after him Portus Trajani,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and soon absorbed the whole traffic with Rome. At the same time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he dug a new canal (Fossa Trajani), which at present forms the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal arm of the Tiber. This harbour is now 2 M. distant from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porto now consists of a Cathedral, dedicated to S. Rufina, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}episcopal Palace, with inscriptions and antiquities, and a Villa of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Prince Torlonia, who has caused excavations to be made here. We{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first reach the walls of the town, then, beyond the farm buildings,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the harbour of Trajan, a large octagonal basin, surrounded by mag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}azines, now a shallow lake only. In the meadows to the N. of this,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extent of the harbour of Claudius is still traceable. Towards the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river are situated the episcopal palace, and the church of St. Rulina,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 10th cent., now modernised.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fiumicino (Locanda), a modern place which derives some im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portance from the river-navigation, is 2 M. from Ostia. The castle,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1773 close to the sea, is now nearly '/2 M. distant from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it. The tower commands a fine view.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Isola Sacra, situated between the two arms of the river, was so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}named at a very early period, either from having been the site of a heathen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple, or from having been presented by Constantine to the Church. Num¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erous herds of cattle are pastured here , against which travellers must be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on their guard, especially in spring. A guide is therefore desirable.{% endraw %}
