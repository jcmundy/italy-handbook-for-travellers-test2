{% raw %}**Baphael's Stanze. The frescoes executed by Raphael in 1508{% endraw %}
{% raw %}-1520 in the papal apartments (Stanze or Camere) of the Vatican, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}order of the Popes Julius II. and Leo X. , are unquestionably the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foremost among the creations of the master and are rivalled by no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern works of art in existence except the ceiling paintings in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cappella Sistina (p. 291). The work, however, in its entire grandeur{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had not been planned, nor the task committed to Raphael from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}outset. Julius II. originally intended these rooms to he decorated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a much simpler style, and he entrusted the task to Perugino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sodoma, and other painters of Umbria and Siena. Among these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was the young Raphael, who had probably been introduced hy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Perugino, and who after the completion of the first frescoes became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so prominent among his fellows, that the work was then entrusted to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him exclusively. Raphael did not, however, live to complete his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}task, and it was finished by his pupils. The earliest pictures in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first and second stanza only are by Raphael's hand. For each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these paintings he received 1200 gold scudi (nearly 500 pounds).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They were seriously injured during the plundering of Rome in 1527,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but were restored by Carlo Maratta under Clement XI. They are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enumerated here chronologically (comp. also p. Liv).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Stanza della Segnatura, so named from the fact that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}papal letters were signed here. Its decoration was undertaken by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Raphael in 1508, at the age of 25, and completed in 1511. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sections of the vaulting of the apartment had already been arranged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sodoma. On the four circular and quadrangular spaces Raphael{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted allegorical figures and Biblical and mythological scenes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which in connection with the paintings in the laTge lunettes are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}symbolical of the four principal spheres of intellectual life.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ceiling Paintings. 1. Theology (divinarum rerum notitia), a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure among clouds, in the left hand a book, with the right pointing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}downwards to the heavenly vision in the Disputa beneath; ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jacent, the Fall of man; 2. Poetry (numine afflatur), crowned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}laurels, seated on a marble throne with hook and lyre; adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it, the Flaying of Marsyas; 3. Philosophy (causarum cognitio),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with diadem, two books (natural and moral science) and a robe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emblematical of the four elements; adjoining it, the Study of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heavenly bodies; 4. Justice (jus suum unicuique tribuens), with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crown, sword, and balance; adjacent, Solomon's Judgment.{% endraw %}
