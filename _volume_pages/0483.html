{% raw %}Railway from Rome to Albano, IT/i M., in about 1 hr.; fares 3 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30, 2 fr. 30, 1 fr. 65 c.; express 4 fr. 40, 2 fr. 95 c. (comp. p. 354).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Soon after quitting the city the train diverges from the line to Civita{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vecchia; on the left is the Porta S. Lorenzo, on the right the arches of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Acqua Felice, then the tombs of the Via Appia. To the left the Sabine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Alban Mts.; at the foot of the latter, Frascati (p. 354) is a conspic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uous object. At (9 M.) Ciampino the line to Frascati diverges to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the S. line approaches the Alban Mts. — 16 M. Marino lies on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nearest chain of hills on the left; above it, on the mountain, is Rocca,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adjoining which on the right rises Monte Cavo with the white monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls. The train then passes through a cutting. To the left, on the olive-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clad hill, appears Castel Gandolfo, immediately beyond which Albano and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ariccia, connected by a viaduct, are visible in the distance. These two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towns possess stat. Albano, or La Cecina, in common, in a lonely and un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attractive situation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An omnibus (fare 1 fr.) runs from the station in 3/4 hr. to the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Albano, 3 M. distant. (As the omnibus is often full, a seat should be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}secured at once.) The ascent is picturesque, although there are few distant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}views. The ruins of Castello Savelli soon appear on the right; La Turri,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Torrelta, on the left. A magnificent view of Ariccia is then obtained,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the ancient castle (p. 361) on the right, and the imposing viaduct{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left, and farther to the left, Albano; to the right, by the entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the town, stands the Villa Loncampa. The omnibus stops in the Piazza.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The High Road from Rome to Albano, the Via Appia Nuova, is also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still much frequented, especially by those who wish to combine this ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cursion with a visit to the ancient Via Appia (comp. p. 342); the vetturini{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mentioned at, p. 113 perform the journey in 2'/2 hrs. (fare 2>/2 fr., not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recommended to strangers).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via Appia Nuova quits Rome by the Porta S. Giovanni (p. 268) ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient Via Appia which is rather longer, by the Porta S. Sebastiano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 260). The two roads unite at the Fratocchie, by the 11th milestone of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}new road. On the left side of the road Clodius once possessed a villa; to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right in the valley lay Bovillae, a colony of Alba Longa, with a sanc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tuary of the Gens Julia, where the remains of a theatre and circus may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still be traced. Remains of walls and tombs are seen on both sides of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road. A large square structure, about 33 ft. in height, with three niches,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was long erroneously regarded as the tomb of Clodius. The road ascends.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The high ground here commands a fine survey of the Campagna, the sea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Rome. Near the gate of Albano, at the 14th milestone, on the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the so-called Tomb of Pompey (p. 360).{% endraw %}
