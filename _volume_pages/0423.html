{% raw %}*Museo Gregoriano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The * Museo Gregoriano of Etruscan Antiquities, founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gregory XVI. in 1836, occupies twelve rooms, and is also on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upper story. Visitors are admitted on Thursdays only (see p. 118).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We ascend the steps from the entrance to the Galleria deiCandelabri{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 313), and reach the door to the right, where we knock. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}museum comprises a number of antiquities excavated chiefly in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1828-36 in the Etruscan cities Vulci, Toscanella, and Chiusi, such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as statues, paintings, vases, golden ornaments, and various domestic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}utensils in bronze, extremely interesting as a link in the history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Italian art, and affording some insight into the habits of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etruscans, of whom but little is yet known. Of the numerous ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jects, which are chiefly of small size, the following deserve special{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mention.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(To the left by the loggia, in the space before the door, is a relief of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medea; right, by the door, another with a contest of Hercules.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Room : Three sarcophagi of terracotta with life-size figures of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased on the covers. On the walls numerous portrait-heads in terracotta,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of peculiar formation. — The II. Room is then entered to the right. One{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the larger sarcophagi on the left is of travertine, adorned with an almost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flat relief of a chariot with a bearded man and musicians, on which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traces of painting are visible; numerous smaller cinerary urns, some of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them of alabaster with mythological reliefs, from Chiusi and Volterra. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}III. Room: In the centre a large sarcophagus of tuffstone with recumbent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure and reliefs of the murder of Clytemnestra, sacrifice of Iphigeneia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eteocles and Polynices, Telephus and Orestes. Beyond this is a fine frieze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in terracotta, recently discovered at Cervetri. In the corners are small and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}strange-looking cinerary urns in the form of houses, perhaps Celtic, found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the lava between Albano and Marino. — IV. Room : containing terra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cottas. ^'Mercury; on each side fragments of female figures with rich dra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pery , from Tivoli. On the right, below, a relief in stucco of Venus and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adonis, Cupid dressing the wound of the latter; left, a relief of Jupiter,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Neptune, and Hercules; on the walls reliefs, cinerary urns, architectural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fragments. By the window small terracottas.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The next four rooms contain the Collection of Vases. These painted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vessels were partly imported from Greece, partly manufactured in Etruria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itself, where Vulci, Chiusi, Volterra, Bomarzo, etc. are proved to have ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celled in this branch of art. The Etruscans imitated the earlier Greek vases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with black, as well as the later with red figures, often without a just ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preciation of the subjects, and with an obvious preference for tragic scenes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially murders. An exhaustive examination of the details will be under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taken by the scientific only; the most interesting objects only need be enu¬{% endraw %}
