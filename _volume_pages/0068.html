{% raw %}Piombino (poor inn) is a small town with 4000 inhab. situated at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. extremity of a wooded promontory, which on the land side is bounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a flat district. A weather-beaten tower on the harbour commands a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnificent "prospect of the sea and the neighbouring island of Elba (in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}front of which rise the cliffs of Cerboli and Palmajola), of S. Giglio and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the coast, and Corsica in the distance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piombino originally belonged to Pisa, in 1399 became a principality of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Appiani, in 1603 was acquired by Spain, and then by the family of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Buoncompagni-Ludovisi, from whom it was wrested by Napoleon in 1805 in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}favour of his brother-in-law, the Corsican Felix Bacciocchi. In 1816 it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored, and till 1859 remained under the Tuscan supremacy. — Steamboat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Elba daily, returning the following morning (p. 13).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 6 M. from Piombino, at the N. extremity of the peninsula, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated the ancient Populonia, the Etruscan Pupluna. The shorter route{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the woods should not be attempted without a guide. The town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with its mediaeval castle, situated on a lofty and precipitous eminence, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a conspicuous object from all sides. Once a prosperous seaport, it suffered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}greatly from a siege by Sulla; in the time of Strabo it had fallen to decay,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is now a poor village. In ancient times the iron of Elba was smelted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here. The old town-walls may still be distinctly traced, and are partic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ularly well preserved on the side towards the sea; they consist of huge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blocks, approaching the polygonal style. The views towards the land and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea are striking and extensive. Several vaults, erroneously said to be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long to an amphitheatre, and a reservoir may also be mentioned as relics{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Roman period. The Etruscan tombs in the vicinity are hardly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worthy of a visit.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The district now begins to exhibit the distinguishing charac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teristics of the Maremme: a world of its own, consisting of forest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and swamp, uncultivated, and in summer poisoned by malaria.{% endraw %}
