{% raw %}The short streets diverging to the right lead to the Piazza and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'"Palazzo della Cancelleria (PL II, 13). The palace, designed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bramante in strict accordance with the rules of the ancient orders{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of architecture, is one of tlie finest structures in Rome and of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}majestic simplicity in its proportions. It contains within its pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cincts the church of S. Lorenzo, originally erected near the theatre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pompey. The elegant Facade (with portal afterwards added by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dom. Fontana) is constructed of blocks of travertine from the Co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}losseum. The *Court, in two stories, is surrounded by arcades.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The columns are ancient; the graceful capitals are decorated with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roses, a flower which belonged to the armorial bearings of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founder Card. Riario. In this palace, in 1848, Pius IX. convoked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a parliament to deliberate on the reforms to be undertaken in tin;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}States of the Church. On 15th Nov. of that year the minister Count{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rossi was assassinated on the first landing of the staircase. This is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the only palace in the interior of the city which the Italian govern¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment still permits to be occupied by the ecclesiastical authorities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right of the palace (with an entrance to the right from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the court) is situated the church of S. Lorenzo in Damaso, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has the above-mentioned facade in common with the palace. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also designed by Bramante (originally erected by Damasus 1.), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is bounded by arcades on three sides. The architecture is the chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}object of interest. The pictures were destroyed during the revolu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of last century. At the end of the right aisle is the tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ill-fated Count Rossi (see above), with a bust by Tenerani.{% endraw %}
