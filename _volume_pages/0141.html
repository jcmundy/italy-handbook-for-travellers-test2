{% raw %}9. From Orvieto to Rome by Bolsena, Montefiascone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Viterbo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7S Jl. High Road. To Viterbo 31'/2 SI. ; from Orvieto to Bolsena, 12 31.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bolsena to Montefiascone, 9 31., thence to Viterbo lO'/z 31.; no regular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}communication; carriage in 5'/2 hrs., 25-30 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Viterbo alone may best be visited from the railway station Orte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 63), with which it is in regular communication (distance 18'/-.' 31.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The diligence, which corresponds with the ordinary morning and evening{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trains (not the express) to Rome, takes 4 hrs. (fare 3 fr.); a party had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}better order a carriage to the station by post-card, addressed to the Im-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presa F. Garinei in Viterbo (two-horse carriage 10-15 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3fost travellers will prefer to return from Viterbo to Orte. The drive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Viterbo to Rome (46'/a M.) takes 10 hrs. (about 40 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The high-road from Orvieto to Montefiascone traverses a some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}what bleak district, passing at some distance from the Lake of Bol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sena, which is almost entirely concealed from view by the surround¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing crater-wall. A far more beautiful though somewhat longer route{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diverges to the W. from this road, about 81/.) M. from Orvieto, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unites at Bolsena with the old road from Siena via Torrenieri, Radi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cofani, and Acquapendente to Rome (p. 18).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 4 31. beyond the above mentioned bifurcation, a road diverges{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left from the direct Orvieto and .Montefiascone road to (3 31.) Bag-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}norea (the ancient Balneum Regis), picturesquely situated on a hill sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rounded by ravines, and interesting to geologists.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bolsena {Hotel in the Piazza), with 2600 inhab., is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below the Roman Volsinii, the birthplace of Sejanus, the favourite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Tiberius. It was one of the twelve capitals of the Etruscan League,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and after various vicissitudes was at length conquered and destroyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Romans. The spoil is said to have included 2000 statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Its wealth has been proved by the discovery, in the vicinity, of nu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merous vases, trinkets, and statues. The present town contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscriptions, columns, and sculptures of the Roman municipium{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which replaced the Etruscan city. The ancient site is reached in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few minutes by an antique causeway of basalt. Among the ruins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is an amphitheatre, worthy of special attention, now converted into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a vegetable-garden. Beautiful views of the lake.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The facade of the church of S. Cristina is embellished with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient relics and a sarcophagus with the triumph of Bacchus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The 'Miracle of Bolsena', the subject of a celebrated picture by Raphael{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Vatican, occurred in 1263. A Bohemian priest, who was somewhat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sceptical as to the doctrine of transubstantiation, was convinced of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}truth by the miraculous appearance of drops of blood on the host which he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had just consecrated. In commemoration of this, Pope Urban IV. in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stituted the festival of Corpus Christi and projected the erection of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}superb cathedral of Orvieto (p. 59).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Lake of Bolsena, the ancient Lacus Vulsiniensis, 994 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above the sea-level, a circular sheet of water, 28 M. in circum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ference , is the vast crater of an extinct volcano, which formed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}central point of a wide sphere of volcanic agency, extending as far{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as Orvieto. The lake abounds in fish (its eels are mentioned by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dante, Purg. 24, 24); but the banks, especially on the W. side,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are bleak and deserted, owing to the malaria confined in the basin{% endraw %}
