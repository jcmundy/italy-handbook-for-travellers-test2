{% raw %}The Via di Porta Maggiore leads in 20 min. from the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria to the * Porta Maggiore (PL II, 35), formed by a monu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment belonging to the Aqua Claudia, above which the Anio Novus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flowed through a second conduit. The inscriptions record the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struction of both aqueducts by the Emp. Claudius, A. D. 52, the.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Claudia, 45 M. in length, bringing water from the neighbourhood{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Subiaco, and the Anio Novus being conducted from the sources of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river of that name, a distance of 62 M. ; and also their resto¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ration by Vespasian in 71, and by Titus in 80. Aurelian converted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the monument into one of the gates of his city wall; and the Co-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lonnas used it in the middle ages as the nucleus of a fortification.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The gate derives its name either from its imposing dimensions, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the church of that name. It was purged of the later additions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Gregory XVI., who closed up the N. archway. Two roads di¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verged hence: to the left, through the now closed arch, the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Labicana, and to the right the Via Praenestina.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between the two roads, outside the gate, on the occasion of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the removal of the fortifications of Honorius, which have been re-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by the wall to the right, was discovered the *Monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Baker Eurysaces, erected in the form of a baker's oven to¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards the close of the republic. The monument was erected by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}baker himself during his lifetime, and the principal inscription,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is repeated several times, is to the effect that — 'This is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monument of Marcus Vergilius Eurysaces, a public purveyor of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bread and an official'. Some of the reliefs represent grinding,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}baking, and other parts of his trade, and others refer to his post of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purveyor to the city.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From this point to the Campagna, see pp. 348, 349.{% endraw %}
