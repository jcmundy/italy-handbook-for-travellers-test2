{% raw %}Villa Borghese.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right, just outside the Porta del Popolo, rises the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Villa Borghese (PI. I, 21), founded by Card. Scipio Borghese,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nephew of Pius V., and subsequently enlarged by the addition of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Giustiniani gardens and the so-called villa of Raphael. which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}last, with a great part of the plantations, was destroyed during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siege of 1849. The beautiful and extensive grounds are open to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the public four times weekly (carriages admitted; comp. p. 117):{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Casino with the collection of antiquities is shown on Saturdays{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only, 1-4 o'clock in winter, 4-7 in summer. The Villa Borghese is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}justly a favourite promenade, and was formerly the scene of popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}festivities in October. The gardens contain a number of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues and inscriptions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On entering, we select the footpath which skirts the carriage-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road on the right, and leads to an Egyptian Gateway (8 min.);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thence in a straight direction , passing a grotto with antique frag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments (left). After 4 min. the road divides (for the branch in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}straight direction, see below). Following the left branch, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads through an artificial ruin with two Doric columns, we observe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on Hie left the private gardens of the prince , and farther on reach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an imitation of a Ruined Temple. Turning to the right here, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}come in 10 min. to a circular space with a Fountain. (Or this spot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be reached by the first broad path to the right beyond the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Doric columns, leading through an avenue of evergreen oaks to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small temple, and thence to the left, through another similar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}avenue.) From this point the carriage-road leads to the Casino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 5 min., whither also beautiful, shady footpaths lead from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left of the fountain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}If we proceed in a straight direction from the above-mentioned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bifurcation of the path, we observe on the left, after 3 min., the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains of Raphael's Villa, and in 3 min. more an arch with a Statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Apollo, whence the road turns to the left and leads to the Casino.{% endraw %}
