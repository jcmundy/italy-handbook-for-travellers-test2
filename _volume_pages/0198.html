{% raw %}Popular Festivals (which have lost much of their former interest): —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Epiphany (6th Jan.), celebrated in the evening in the Piazza Navona,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}since 1873 (formerly near S. Eustachio), array of booths and prodigious din{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of toy-trumpets.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Carnival, which has of late regained a little of its former splen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dour, extends from the second Saturday before Ash-Wednesday to Shrove-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuesday, and consists in a daily procession in the Corso, accompanied hy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the throwing of bouquets and comfits, excepting on Sundays and Fri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days, when a 'gala corso' generally takes place, and concluding with a horse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}race. The last evening is the Moccoli (taper) evening, the tapers being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lighted immediately after sunset. A window in the Corso is the best point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of view. The most animated scene is between the Piazza Colonna and S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carlo. Balconies there are in great request and dear (as high as 600 fr.);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}single places are let on the balconies fitted up for the occasion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The October Festival, once famous, but now comparatively insigni¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficant, takes place during the vintage-season, and consists in singing, dan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cing, and carousals at the osterie outside the gates (e. g. on the 'Testaccio).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Festa dello Statuto, or Festival of the Constitution, introduced{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in consequence of the annexation of Rome, takes place on the first Sunday{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in June. In the forenoon a military parade is held in the Campo di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maccao (p. 177). In the evening a Girandola, i. e. an illumination and ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hibition of fire-works at the Castello di S. Angelo. — On the anniversary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Foundation of Rome (21st April), it has of late been usual to illu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minate the Colosseum and the Forum with Bengal fire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The opening of Parliament is also inaugurated with festivities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Street Scenes. The top of the Scala di Spagna (PI. I, 20) and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Sistina are the favourite haunts of artists' models, chiefly Neapolitans,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose costumes are a well-known subject of photographs and pictures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Campagnoli, whose figures form one of the most singular appari¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions in the streets of Rome, are less frequently seen than formerly. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pass a great part of their lives on horseback, while tending their herds of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oxen and horses. Their equipment usually consists of a low felt-hat, wide,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grey mantle, leathern leggings, and spurs: and they carry a 'pungolo', or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iron-pointed goad, for driving their cattle. The peasants of remote moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain-districts, wearing sandals (whence termed ciocciari), and with swathed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feet and ankles, also present a grotesque appearance. — The favourite haunts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the country - people are in front of the Pantheon (PI. II, 16; especially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on Sundays), the Piazza Montanara (PI. II, 17) below the Capitol, and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the market-place of the Campo de' Fiori (p. 204).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Garrison of Rome consists of 2 regiments of Granatieri, or Gre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nadier Guards; 6 regiments of Infantry (with dark blue coats, grey trou¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sers, white leather belts, and caps); 1 regiment of Bersaglieri or riflemen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(with dark blue coats and red facings, large plumed caps worn on one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side, forming an elite corps like the Austrian Kaiserjager); 1 regiment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cavalry (dark blue coats, and light grey trousers); 1 brigade of Field{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Artillery (dark blue coats and yellow collars); and 1 brigade of Engi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}neers. To these we may add the Carabinieri, or gensdarmes, who wear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}black uniforms with red facings and cocked hats.{% endraw %}
