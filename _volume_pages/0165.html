{% raw %}The village at Borgo at the base is the residence of the wealthier inhabitants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The celebrated epigraphist and numismatist Barlolommeo Borghesi, born at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Savignano in 1781, was from 1821 until his death in 1860, a resident at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Marino, where he arranged and described his admirable collections, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}received visits from foreign savants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Rimini the line skirts the coast, passes (75'/2 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riccione, crosses the streams Marano and Conca (the Crustumius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rapax of Lucan), and reaches (81 M.) La Cattolica, so called from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having been the residence of the Roman Catholic bishops during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Council of Rimini in 359. A chain of hills descends here to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea; the train ascends for some distance, and then passes through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them by means of a long tunnel. It crosses the Tavollo and passes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Villa Vittoria, situated on the left, on the road to Rimini, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence of Queen Caroline of England when Princess of Wales.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Pesaro, with 10,500 inhab. (with suburbs 19,700), the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisaurum, is the capital of the united provinces of Pesaro and Ur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bino, and formerly belonged to the Pentapolis Maritima.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(if the provinces of the former States of the Church situated on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adriatic, the four southern are called the Marches ('■Le Marche'), viz. Pesaro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}L'rbino, Ancona, Macerala, and Ascoli, comprising an area of 3750 Sq. M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 915,500 inhab. In the Roman period the S. part as far as Ancona{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was called Picenum, whereas the N. part belonged to Umbria. — Pesaro,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Roman colony as early as B.C. 184, was destroyed by Tot.ilas, and rebuilt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Belisarius. It was subsequently ruled over by the Malatesta family,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then by the Sforza, and later by the Rovere, dukes of Urbino, under whom,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly through the influence of Lucrezia d'Este, it became a great centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of art and literature , and was visited by Bernardo and Torqualo Tasso.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1631 the town was annexed to the States of the Church. — The figs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesaro are excellent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesaro was the birthplace of the celebrated composer Gioacchino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rossini (b. 1789, d. at Paris 1868), the 'swan of Pesaro', to whom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a bronze statue (on the right as the station is quitted) was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1864 by his admiring friends, Baron Salamanca of Madrid and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}G. Delahante of Paris.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the back of Rossini's monument, and enters the gate from which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old diligence road issues. To the left is the Teatro Rossini, anil{% endraw %}

{% raw %}great hall, which is about 44 yds. long and upwards of 16 yds. wide,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still contains a coffered wooden ceiling with its original painting. —{% endraw %}

{% raw %}and Perticari in marble, behind which is the former church of S.{% endraw %}
