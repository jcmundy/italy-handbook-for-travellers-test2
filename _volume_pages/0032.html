{% raw %}XII. Calculation of Time.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The old Italian reckoning from 1 to 24 o'clock is now disused in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all the larger towns, but is still used by the priests and by the lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}classes, especially in Central and Southern Italy. The ordinary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reckoning of other nations is called the ora francese. The moment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the sun's disappearance below the horizon is 'half past 23 o'clock';{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the twilight lasts about half-an-hour, after which it is '24 o'clock',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or the close of the day, when 'Ave Maria' is rung. The following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hours are usually termed 'un ora di notte', 'due ore di notte', etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This troublesome mode of calculation would necessitate a daily-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alteration of every time-piece in the kingdom, but it is thought{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sufficiently accurate to alter the hour of Ave Maria by a quarter of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an hour about once a fortnight. The following table shows the Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}compared with the ordinary hours at Rome.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}XIII. Climate. Health.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Climate. Most travellers must in some degree alter their mode{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of living whilst in Italy, without however implicitly adopting the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian style. Inhabitants of more northern countries generally become{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unusually susceptible to cold in Italy, and therefore should not omit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be well supplied with warm clothing for the winter. Carpets and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stoves, to the comforts of which the Italians generally appear indif¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ferent, are indispensable in winter. A southern aspect is an absolute{% endraw %}
{% raw %}essential for delicate persons, and highly desirable for the robust.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Colds are most easily caught after sunset, and in rainy weather.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Even in summer it is a wise precaution not to wear too light clothing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Flannel is strongly recommended.{% endraw %}
