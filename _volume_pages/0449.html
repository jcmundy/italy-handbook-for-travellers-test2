{% raw %}centre of the catacomb is the large and nearly quadrangular Basilica{% endraw %}

{% raw %}St. Peter. The basilica, built in the second story of the catacomb,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}ed condition, but the church has recently been partly restored. It{% endraw %}

{% raw %}catacombs are several smaller chapels, some of which are adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with life-size mural paintings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of St. Prsetextatus, on the Via Appia towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Urbano (p. 346), contain decorations similar to those of the sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of the Vigiles at Trastevere (p. 329). In the burial chapel of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vihia (not easily accessible) are still to be seen gnostic heretical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations (Hermes as conductor of the dead, etc.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of St. Priscilla lie on the Via Salara, '/'i M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the gate (p. 350). The oldest part consists of an extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}group of chapels, with interesting paintings of the beginning of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd cent. Farther on, among the decorations of the ceiling, are a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna and the Child, with Isaiah and the star. Coloured in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions on tiles, of the earliest and simplest type, are also occa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sionally found here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of S. Agnese, under the church of S. Agnese Fuori{% endraw %}
{% raw %}le Mura (p. 174), are destitute of painting, but are to a great extent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still in their original condition. They are shown by the sacristan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without a permesso. — About '/4 M. beyond the church is another{% endraw %}
{% raw %}catacomb, called the Ccemeterium Ostrianum, and remarkable for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the number of chapels it contains (accessible on Sund., Tues. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thurs.). Some of these were doubtless used for divine service,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as is indicated by the large pulpits, hewn in tufa. The largest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel, a narrow and lofty room, also contains stone benches and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niches.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Catacombs of S. Sebastiano, situated below the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that name on the Via Appia (see p. 344), the only excavations of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the kind which continued to be visited in mediasval times, have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been deprived of all their enrichments, and are now devoid of inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}est. Near them are the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jewish Catacombs, in the Vigna Randanini (p. 344 ; at present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inaccessible), which were excavated about the 3rd cent. They rather{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resemble the catacombs of Naples than the other Roman catacombs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The inscriptions are exclusively Greek and Latin. The most fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently recurring symbol is the seven-branched candelabrum. Two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chambers are enriched with decorative paintings, in which, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trary to the Mosaic law, figures of animals are depicted. A sarco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phagus here bears traces of gilding.{% endraw %}
