{% raw %}PREFACE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}J_he objects of the Handbook for Italy, which consists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of three volumes, each complete in itself, are to supply the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller with a few remarks on the progress of civilisation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and art among the people he is about to visit, to render{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him as independent as possible of the services of guides and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valets-de-place, to protect him against extortion, and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}every way to aid him in deriving enjoyment and instruction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from his tour in one of the most fascinating countries in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world. The Handbook will also, it is hoped, be the means{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of saving the traveller many a trial of temper; for there is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably no country in Europe where the patience is more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}severely taxed than in some parts of Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Handbook is based on the Editor's personal ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quaintance with the places described, most of which he has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}repeatedly and carefully explored. As, however, changes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are constantly taking place, he will highly appreciate any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}communications with which travellers may kindly favour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him, if the result of their own observation. The information{% endraw %}
{% raw %}already received from numerous correspondents, which he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gratefully acknowledges, has in many cases proved most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serviceable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sixth edition of Central Italy and Rome, like its pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decessor, has been carefully revised and brought up to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}date. For the description of the antiquities of Rome, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Editor is indebted to Professor H. Nissen of Strassburg. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}introductory articles on art by Prof. R. KekuU of Bonn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Prof. A. Springer of Leipsic have been adapted for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}use of English travellers with the kind assistance of Mr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}J. A. Crowe, the eminent historian of art. Professor Springer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has also contributed many valuable incidental remarks on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern art.{% endraw %}
