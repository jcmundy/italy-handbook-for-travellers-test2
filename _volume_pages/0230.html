{% raw %}— Opposite S. Silvestro is the new English Church (p. 113). erected{% endraw %}

{% raw %}On the right is the Pal. Verospi(No. 374), now Torlonia, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Onorio Lunghi, and restored by Alessandro Specchi. A loggia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the first floor is adorned with pleasing mythological frescoes by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fr. Albani, distantly recalling the famous Farnesina works (p. 321).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right, farther on. at the corner of the Piazza Colonna, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extensive Pal. Chigi, begun in 1526 by Giac. della Porta, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed by C. Maderna. It contains a few antiques and a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection of pictures, but is not open to the public. Applicants{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for admission to the Bibliotheca Chisiana, which possesses valuable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}MSS., must be provided with an introduction from their ambassador.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The handsome *Piazza Colonna (PI. I, 16) is bounded on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right by the Pal. Chigi, opposite which is the Pal. Ferrajuoli (PI. 1,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}16, 'JO'). In the Corso is situated the Pal. Piombino (PL I, 16, JJ),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and opposite the Corso the old Post-Office, with ancient Ionic col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns on the facade, brought from Veii (p. 377).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre of the piazza, to which it gives its name, stands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the *Column of'Marcus Aurelius, embellished like that of Trajan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with reliefs from the wars of the emperor against the Marcomanni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other German tribes on the Danube. It consists of 28 blocks,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}besides the basement and capital, being altogether 95 ft. in height,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is approached by steps. Sixtus V. caused it to be restored in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1589, and ascribed it, according to the then prevalent opinion, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antoninus Pius, by whose name it is still frequently designated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the summit is a statue of St. Paul.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The four large candelabra are of recent date. The piazza is very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}animated in the evening. The band , which usually plays on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pincio, performs here in the height of summer (p. 142).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the Piazza Colonna (beyond the post-office) is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza di Monte Citorio, on the right side of which stands the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spacious Camera de' Deputati (PI. I, 16, 24), formerly the po¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lice-office. The design of the building by Bernini was afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modified by C. Fontana. The court in the interior was covered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a roof in 1871, fitted up for the sittings of the Italian parlia¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment, and inaugurated on 27th Nov. of that year. Visitors obtain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cards of admission at the office. The rising of the piazza towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. is due to the buried ruins of the vast amphitheatre of Sta-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tilius Taurus, erected B.C. 31, and the only amphitheatre at Rome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prior to the construction of the Colosseum. Rows of seats have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been discovered here at a depth of 88 ft. below the present level{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the piazza.{% endraw %}
