{% raw %}258 IV. Ancient Rome. ROME.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Appia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The magnificence of the establishment was unparalleled. Numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues, including the Farnese Bull, Hercules, and Flora at Naples,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosaics, etc., have been found here; and hare as the walls now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are, and notwithstanding the destruction of the roof, they still afford{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a reminiscence of the technical perfection of the structure. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}establishment was quadrangular in form, surrounded by a wall, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had its porticoes, race-course, etc. : length 240 yds., width 124{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yds.; total area of grounds 360 yds. in length, by as many in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breadth. The use of all the chambers cannot now be ascertained, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most important only are enumerated here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We first enter in a straight direction a spacious oblong, once sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rounded by columns (Peristyle), and containing scanty remains of mosaic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pavement. Keeping to the left, we enter a large saloon, which appears to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been the Calidarium, or hot-air bath. From the calidarium a second{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peristyle is entered, corresponding to the former. In the new pavement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the places where the columns formerly stood are left open. Around, as in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the other rooms, are placed architectural and sculptural fragments, re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mains of the old pavement, etc. We now traverse the semicircular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Exedra to the Tepidarium, or warm bath, situated in the centre, next to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the calidarium. To the left of this is the Frigidarium, or cold bath, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large round space, the vaulting of which has fallen in. A small flight of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steps by the wall here affords a survey of part of the grounds which sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rounded the baths. On this side lay the stadium. Other remains of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thermse are scattered over the neighbouring vineyards.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now return to the Via di Porta S. Sehastiano, and continue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to follow it. We first reach an arboretum on the left; then, a little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond it, on the right, the church of SS. Nereo edAchilleo (PL III,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}23, 26) , standing on the site of a temple of Isis, founded by Leo III.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 800, and almost entirely rebuilt by Card. Baronius at the end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 16th century. (Festival, 12th May; open in the forenoon.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior exhibits the characteristics of an ancient basilica. At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the end of the nave is an ambo on the left, supposed to be of great age,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transferred hither from S. Silvestro in Capite; opposite is a marble cande¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}labrum for the Easter-candles, of the 15th cent. Above the arch of the tri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bune are fragments of a mosaic of the time of Leo III. Transfiguration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Christ with Moses and Elias, in front the kneeling Apostles, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right the Annunciation, on the left the Madonna enthroned.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The opposite church of S. Sisto, restored by Benedict XIII., con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains nothing worthy of note. The monastery was dedicated to St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dominicus by Honorius III. — The Via della Ferratella then diver¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ges to the left to the Lateran (p. 267) , passing the ruin of a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple of the Lares.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right, a little farther on, is S. Cesareo, a small but cu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rious church , mentioned as early as the time of Gregory the Great,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and finally restored by Clement VIII. (open on the mornings of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sundays and festivals).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. In the centre of the anterior portion of the church are two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altars dating from the close of the 16th cent.; at the farther extremity, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, the old pulpit with sculptures ; Christ as the Lamb, the symbols{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Apostles, and sphynxes; opposite, a modern candelabrum with an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cient basis. The inlaid screen of the Prksbyterium, and the decorations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Hioh-Altak are m dbeval. The tribune contains an ancient episco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pal throne.{% endraw %}
