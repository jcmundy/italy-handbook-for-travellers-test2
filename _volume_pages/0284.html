{% raw %}Passing the palace, we reach the Piazza del Pasquino (PL II,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}13 ). which derives its name from an ancient group of statuary placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the obtuse angle of the Pal. Braschi. This was an admirable,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but now sadly mutilated work of a good period of art, and is said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to have been called Pasquino as early as the end of the 15th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after a tailor of that name who lived in 'the vicinity and was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}notorious for his lampooning propensities. It was once the custom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to affix satires and ebullitions of malice to this statue, the answers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to which used to be attached to the Marforio (p. 159, 218), and to{% endraw %}
