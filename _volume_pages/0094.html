{% raw %}Siena, the capital of the province of that name, with 23,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab., the seat of a university which was in high repute as early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the 14th cent., and the residence of an archbishop, is pictures¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quely situated '25 M. due S. of Florence, and 1330ft. above the sea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on three connected hills (the clayey soil of which is called 'Terra{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Siena'"). It is now a busy trading and manufacturing place; it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also possesses several libraries and scientific societies, and is one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pleasantest towns in Tuscany. The climate is healthy, the at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosphere in summer being tempered by the lofty situation; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}language and manners of the inhabitants are pleasing and pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possessing. Alost of the streets are narrow and crooked, but contain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many palaces and handsome churches. Next to Rome, Florence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Venice, Siena is perhaps the most important town in Italy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the study of the art of the 13th-16th centuries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Siena, the ancient Sena Julia, or Colonia Julia Senensis, is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been founded by the Senonian Gauls and converted into a Roman colony{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Augustus, whence it derives its arms, the female wolf and the twins.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The only Etruscan antiquities here are a few tombs which were discovered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1864 near the Porta Camollia. The town attained the culminating point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its prosperity in the middle ages, after it had become a free state at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the beginning of the 12th cent., and, having banished the nobility, had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}united with the party of the Ghibellines. Farinata degli Uberti and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghibellines from Florence were then welcomed in Siena, and on 4th Sept.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1260, a great victory over the Guelphs was gained near Monte Aperto (6 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distant). The nobility afterwards returned to Siena, but the city kept a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jealous watch over its privileges, and increased to such an extent that it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numbered nearly 100,000 inhab. , and vied with Florence in wealth and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}love of art. At length the supremacy was usurped by tyrants, such as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(about 1500) Pandolfo Petrucci, surnamed /( Magnifico (whom Macchiavelli{% endraw %}
