{% raw %}Foligno. near the ancient Fulginium, an industrial town with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10,000 (incl. suburbs 21,000) inhab., and an episcopal residence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is situated in a fertile district. In 1281 it was destroyed by Perugia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in 1439 annexed to the States of the Church. The earth¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quake of 1831 occasioned serious damage; others in 1839, 1853,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 1854 were less destructive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the entrance to the town, a marble statue was erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1872 to the painter Niccolb del Liberatore, surnamed I'Alunno, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief master of the school of Foligno (p. 48).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via della Fiera leads straight to the Market-place, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Cathedral of S. Feliciano is situated, erected in the 12th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(of which the Romanesque facade of the transept is still extant),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but entirely remodelled in the 16th cent. The handsome dome is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributed, but without authority, to Bramante. — The Palazzo del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Governo, in the same square, contains frescoes by Ottaviano Nelli in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an old chapel.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Following the Via Montogli to the left, and then the Via S. Niccolo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a side-street to the right, we reach the church of S. Niccolb. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd Chapel to the right here contains an altar-piece with numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures, the centre being formed by an Adoration of the Infant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christ by Niccolb Alunno. The chapel to the right of the high-altar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is adorned with a Coronation of the Virgin with a predella by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same master.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church of S. Anna, or delle Contesse, formerly contained the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated Madonna di Foligno by Raphael, now in the Vatican. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria infra Portas contains frescoes by Nic. Alunno, and La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nunziatella a fresco of the Baptism of Christ by Pietro Perugino. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Capp. Betlehemme, Via de' Monasteri, there is a small public{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture-gallery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 4 M. to the E. of Foligno, on the slope of the hills, is situaled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Abbadia di Sassovivo, with cloisters built in 1229, resembling those of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Paolo Fuori at Rome (p. 255).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 5 M. to the W. of Foligno is Bevagna, on the Clitumnus, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Mevania of the Umbri, celebrated for its admirable pastures, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains of an amphitheatre and other antiquities. The churches of S. Sil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vestro and S. Michele, dating from the latter part of the 12th cent., though{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not entirely preserved, present a picturesque appearance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Bevagna (or from Foligno direct 7 M.) the traveller may visit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lofty Montefalco, a small town with several churches containing fine{% endraw %}

{% raw %}choir contains the legend of St. Francis; and the chapel of St. Jerome a{% endraw %}
