{% raw %}the left is an altar adorned with a relief in marble (Pieta); at the sides{% endraw %}

{% raw %}right St. Paul. The altar-statue of St. Helena is an exact copy of the Bar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}berini Juno in the Sala Rotonda of the Vatican (p. 313), with the exception{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that a cross has been substituted for the sceptre in the right hand, and a{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The greater part of the old Cistercian monastery formerly belong¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing to the church is now used as a barrack.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent to S. Croce, in the direction of the Lateran, is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Amphitheatrum Castrense (PL II, 36), of which only 16 arches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the enclosing wall, incorporated with the old city-fortifications,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still exist. The structure is of brick, and so also are the Corinthian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capitals and other decorations. The date of its erection is uncertain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The longer diameter of the amphitheatre is 57 yds. , the shorter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}44 yds., and the arena 41 yds. in length. The outside is best{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen in the course of a walk from the Porta Maggiore to the Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Giovanni (from gate to gate 1 M.). In the interior is a kitchen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the other side of S. Croce is an apse with arched windows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the beginning of adjoining walls, which are supposed to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonged to a Temple of Venus and Cupid , or a Nymphaeum of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexander Severus, or to the Sessorium mentioned above.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From S. Croce to the Lateran is a walk of 5 min. (p. 267).{% endraw %}
