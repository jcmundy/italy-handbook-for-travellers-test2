{% raw %}4 Route 1. ORBETELLO. From Leghorn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At (105 M.) Talamone a beautiful view of the sea is disclosed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The village lies at the extremity of the promontory and possesses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an anchorage sheltered by the island of Giglio and the Monte Ar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gentario. The extent of the creek has been much diminished by al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luvial deposits. Here, in B.C. 225, the Roman legions landed and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}signally defeated the Gauls who were marching against Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train crosses the small river Osa, then the more important{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Albegna (ancient Albinia), at the mouth of which are salt-works.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stat. Albegna.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}114 M. Orbetello (halt of l/^ hr.). On the arrival of the train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an omnibus (1 fr.) starts for Orbetello (poor inns ; the best is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trattoria del Buon Gusto, or Saccoccione), lL^M. distant, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6400 inhab., situated at the extremity of a promontory, not far{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the foot of Monte Argentario, which rises immediately from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea, and is connected with the mainland by two narrow tongues of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land, whereby a large salt-water lagoon is formed. Beyond its re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}markable situation the place contains nothing of interest, except{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the polygonal walls on the side next the sea, which testify to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great antiquity of the town, although its ancient name is unknown.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Orbetello an embankment has been constructed across the shallow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lake, which abounds in fish, to M. Argentario. A carriage-road leads to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. harbour Porto S. Stefano (steamboat to Elba, every Thursday at 3 p.m.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}see p. 13), and to Port' Ercole on the S. side. The Monte Argentario{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2087 ft.) culminates in two peaks, on one of which is situated a monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Passionists. The ascent is very interesting, and is accomplished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Orbetello in 2-3 hrs. (with guide). The 'view embraces the coast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Tuscany and the surrounding district as far as M. Amiata, and the sea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with its numerous rocky islands as far as Sardinia. If time is limited, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first and lower eminence, 3/i hr. from Orbetello, commanding a picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view of the coast, should be visited.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Orbetello lis also the most convenient starting-point for an excursion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the interesting ruins of the ancient Cosa, the present Ansedonia, 4'/2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distant; and also for a visit to the ancient towns of Saturnia and Sovana,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30-35 M. inland. Cosa is an ancient Etruscan town, deserted as early as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 5th cent. The polygonal walls (1600 yds. in circumference) with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their towers are admirably preserved. A beautiful prospect of the sea and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast is enjoyed hence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Orbetello, on an eminence to the right, are the ruins of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cosa (see above). — The train soon enters the former Papal terri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tory, and traverses the Roman Maremma; scenery unattractive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}127 M. Chiarone. It then crosses the Fiora and reaches (135 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Montalto, a poor village.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Montalto the traveller may ascend by the bank of the Fiora to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient Ponte della Badia and the site of Vulci, where very successful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excavations have been made since 1828, and thousands of Etruscan vases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other antiquities have been discovered. The ancient Etruscan city{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itself, the circumference of which is ascertained to have been 5 31., has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entirely disappeared with the exception of its tombs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Montalto the country is more undulating. The line{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the small rivers Arrone and Maria, the outlet of the Lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Bolsena.{% endraw %}
