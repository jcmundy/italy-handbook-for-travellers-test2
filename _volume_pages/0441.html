{% raw %}*S. Maria in Trastevere, which is said to have been founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Calixtus 1. under Alexander Severus, on the spot where a spring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of oil miraculously welled forth at the time of the birth of Christ, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mentioned for the first time in 499, was re-erected by Innocent II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 1140, and consecrated by Innocent III. in 1198. The church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has recently been restored. The present portico was added by ('.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fontanel under Clement XL in 1702. In front are mosaics of Mary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Child, on each side the small figure of a bishop (Innocent II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Eugene III.) and ten virgins, eight of whom have burning,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and two extinguished lamps, a work of the 12th cent. , largely-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored in the 14th. The portico contains the remains of two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Annunciations, one attributed to Cavallini, but now entirely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}repainted, and numerous inscriptions. On the side-wall to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right is the tomb of the librarian Anastasius (d. about 886).{% endraw %}
