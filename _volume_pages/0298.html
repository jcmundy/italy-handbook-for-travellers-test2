{% raw %}The Capitol.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church is generally approached from the Piazza id' the Capitol{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the staircase to the left, at the back of the Capitoline museum, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then to the left from the first landing. Over the door here is an ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosaic of the Madonna between two angels.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is disfigured by modern additions. Tiie nave is supported{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hy 22 ancient columns, most of them of granite, varying greatly in style{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and dimensions. The 3rd on the left bears the inscription 'A cubiculo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aiigiislorum'. The rich ceiling of the nave was executed to commemorate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the victory of Lepanto in 1571.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the wall of the principal Entrance , to the left, is the tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the astronomer Lodovico Grato (1531), with a figure of Christ by Andrea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sansovino; on the right the ''monument of Card. Lebretto (1465) with partially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved painting. — Right Aisle, 1st Chapel: ^Frescoes from the life of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Bernardino of Siena, by Pinturicchio, restored by Camuccini. Frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the ceiling attributed to Franc, da Citta di Castello and L. Signorelli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The 5th Chapel (of St. Matthew) contains good pictures by Muziano. — Left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aisle. In the 2nd Chapel a manger (presepe) is fitted up at Christinas,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}i. e. a gorgeous representation of the Nativity in life-size, with the richly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorated image of the Infant Christ (il santo bambino), which forms the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal ornament of the church. This image is believed to protect per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sons in imminent danger, is frequently invoked and revered, and is some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times conveyed to the houses of the sick, on which occasions passers-by kneel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on its approach. During the week after Christmas, from 3 to 4 o'clock{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daily, a number of children from 5 to 10 years of age address their pe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}titions to the bambino. At the end of the left aisle a tomb-relief of Msgr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crivelli by Donatello (much damaged). — Transept. On the right and left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the pillars of the nave, are two "ainbos from the old choir, by Lau-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rentius and Jacobus Cosmos. The Chapel on the right belongs to the Sa-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}velli; on the right and left (the latter originally an ancient sarcophagus){% endraw %}
{% raw %}are monuments of the family, of the 13th cent, (of the parents and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brother of Honorius IV.). The left transept contains a rectangular canopy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}borne by eight columns of alabaster, called the Cappella Santa, or di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Elena. Beneath the altar, which was destroyed during the French Re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}volution, but was restored in 1835, the remains of S. Helena are said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}repose in an ancient sarcophagus of porphyry. The present altar also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}encloses an ancient altar, bearing the inscription Ara Primogeniti Dei,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is said to have been erected by Augustus. According to a legend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 12th cent., this was the spot where the Sibyl of Tibur appeared{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the emperor, whom the senate proposed to elevate to the rank of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}god, and revealed to him a vision of the Virgin and her Son. This was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the origin of the name, 'Church of the Altar of Heaven'. At the end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ^NT. transept is the monument of Matthaeus of Aquasparta (d. 1302), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal of the Franciscan order, mentioned by Dante. — Choir. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, the monument of Giov. Batt. Savelli (d. 1498). From 1512 down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to 1565 the high-altar was adorned with the Madonna of Foligno by Raphael,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ordered for this church, afterwards at Foligno (p. 75), and now in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vatican Gallery. The donor, Sigismondo Conti da Foligno, is interred in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the choir. The present altar-piece is an ancient picture of the Madonna,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributed to Si. Luke.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of the side-steps from the piazza of the Capitol, has belonged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Frati Minori Osservanti di S. Francesco since 1251, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was formerly the residence of the principal of the order, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part of it is now occupied by the military. Fine view of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome from the corridors. — In the monastery garden fragments of{% endraw %}
