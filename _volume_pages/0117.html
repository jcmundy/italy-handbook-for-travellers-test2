{% raw %}to Perugia. TERONTOLA. 6. Route. 45{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the cathedral is the Baptistery, formerly a Jesuit Church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It contains two pictures by Luca Signorelli, the Conception and Na¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tivity, and three by Fra Angelica da Fiesole, the Annunciation and -two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}predelle, representing scenes from the life of the Virgin and S. Domenico.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Passing the colonnades of the theatre in the Piazza Signorelli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we follow the Via Dardano in a straight direction to the Porta Dar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dano, where we obtain the best survey of the *Ancient Etruscan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Town Walls, constructed of huge blocks, and for the most part well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved, which surround the town in a circumference of about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2860 yds., and along the outside of which we may descend.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ascending the Via S. Margherita from S. Domenico, we reach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(20 min.) the hill commanding the town, on which are situated the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Margherita, and a dilapidated fortress (see below). —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About halfway up, the Via delle Santuccc diverges to the left, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads in a few minutes to the church of S. Niccolb, with a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance court planted with cypresses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior ('/-2 fr.) contains a freely restored fresco and an -altar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piece, painted on both sides (in front the Body of Christ borne by angels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and surrounded by saints; at the back, Madonna della Seggiola with SS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peter and Paull, by Luca Signorelli. — The sacristan will point out a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}direct route, ascending hence by steps to S. Margherita.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church of S. Margherita, a Gothic building by Niccolb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Giovanni Pisano, possesses a handsome rose window, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has of late been partially renewed and enlarged. In the high-altar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the tomb of the saint (13th cent.); the silver front with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}golden crown was presented by Pietro da Cortona. — The visitor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should not omit to ascend somewhat higher to the old *Fortezza,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2165 ft. in height (trifling fee), from the walls of which the noble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prospect is entirely uninterrupted, except at the back, where it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounded by the mountain-chain (Alto di S. Egidio, 3432 ft.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Besides the town-walls , there are several less interesting anti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quities: an ancient vault beneath the Palazzo Cecchetti; near S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Margherita, remains of Roman Baths, erroneously called a '■Temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Bacchus'; outside the gate of S. Agostino, an Etruscan tomb,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 'Grotta di Pitagora\{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The visitor may (by presenting a visiting-card) possibly ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain access to the private collection of Sign. Colonnese in the Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo Madama, Via Nazionale 5: beautiful hall-length picture of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Stephen and a Nativity by Luca Signorelli, a picture of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German school, and two Italian works of the 15th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}76 M. Terontola, an unimportant place near the N.W. angle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Trasimene Lake, is the junction of the lines to Chiusi, Orte,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Rome (see R. 8), and to Perugia and Foligno. Passengers in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the latter direction change carriages here.{% endraw %}
